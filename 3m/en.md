World's largest producer of PFAS — "forever chemicals" contaminating drinking water globally.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. 3M's production and discharge of PFAS over decades contaminated drinking water and harmed the health of millions of people.

1. **Drinking water contamination in Minnesota (1950s–2000s)** — Discharges from 3M's plants in Cottage Grove and Maplewood contaminated water supplies serving more than 100,000 residents of eastern Minneapolis suburbs with PFOS and PFOA. PFOS is linked to kidney and bladder cancer and immune disorders.
   *Source: Minnesota Pollution Control Agency (MPCA), 2004–2010*

2. **US military bases: AFFF firefighting foam** — 3M was the primary manufacturer of AFFF firefighting foam (containing PFOS), used at thousands of military and civilian airfields. The EPA identified PFAS contamination at more than 400 US military installations. Residents of surrounding communities have elevated PFAS concentrations in their blood.
   *Source: EPA PFAS Action Plan, EWG Military Base Report*

3. **Contamination in Alabama** — 3M's manufacturing facility in Florala, Alabama discharged PFAS into the Tennessee-Tombigbee waterway, contaminating the water intakes of several municipalities. Lawsuits filed by residents documented elevated cancer rates in riverside communities.
   *Source: Alabama ADEM records, class action filings*

---

## 2. Has the corporation systematically violated environmental law?

**Answer: YES**. 3M violated chemical safety and environmental regulations for decades.

1. **Violation of the Toxic Substances Control Act (TSCA)** — The EPA found that 3M had not provided the agency with toxicity data on PFOS obtained through internal studies, violating TSCA requirements to report "substantial risks."
   *Source: EPA Consent Order with 3M, 2000*

2. **Discharge permit violations in Minnesota** — The MPCA identified numerous violations of wastewater discharge permits at 3M's manufacturing facilities — including PFAS exceedances that were not included in permit conditions due to the absence of standards at the time.
   *Source: MPCA enforcement records*

3. **Violations in Belgium** — 3M's plant in Zwijndrecht (Antwerp) discharged PFOS into the Scheldt River for many years. Dutch and Belgian authorities found that floodplain soils along the Scheldt were contaminated with PFAS at concentrations many times above EU standards.
   *Source: RIVM, VMM Belgium*

---

## 3. Has the corporation concealed information about environmental damage?

**Answer: YES**. Internal 3M documents, revealed through litigation, demonstrate systematic concealment of PFAS toxicity data.

1. **Internal toxicity studies (1970s)** — 3M's research in the 1970s showed that PFOS accumulates in the blood and organs of animals. The company concealed this data from the EPA until 2000, when the agency launched an investigation.
   *Source: EPA Consent Order, 3M internal documents*

2. **Blood concentration data for plant workers (1980s)** — 3M detected PFOS in the blood of factory workers in the 1980s. This information was not disclosed to the EPA. The company conducted its own epidemiological studies whose results were kept confidential.
   *Source: Bloomberg Businessweek, court filings*

3. **Concealment of Scheldt contamination data** — The Belgian plant did not inform local authorities about the extent of PFOS discharges into the river. The true scale of contamination in the Scheldt floodplains only became known in the 2020s through independent research.
   *Source: Investigative reporting De Standaard, RIVM*

---

## 4. Has the corporation exerted political pressure to weaken environmental standards?

**Answer: YES**. 3M actively lobbied to delay the introduction of PFAS standards and pushed for a voluntary withdrawal from production rather than a legislative ban.

1. **Opposition to EPA PFOS regulation (1990s–2000)** — Throughout the 1990s, 3M successfully blocked EPA attempts to set PFOS standards by providing alternative data and pushing for its "voluntary phase-out" to be recognised in place of a regulatory ban.
   *Source: EPA PFAS regulatory history, EWG*

2. **Lobbying against PFAS maximum contaminant levels in drinking water** — 3M funded research programmes and participated in public comments opposing EPA-proposed maximum contaminant levels (MCLs) for PFAS in drinking water, pushing for higher thresholds.
   *Source: EPA docket PFAS MCL, 2022–2023*

3. **Lobbying for liability immunity** — Before paying the $12.5 billion settlement, 3M sought legislative immunity from future lawsuits in the US Congress — an attempt blocked by activists and several senators.
   *Source: The Intercept, 2023*

## 5. Has the corporation's activity caused long-term damage to ecosystems?

**Answer: YES**. PFAS from 3M has been detected on every continent and in every ocean.

1. **Global PFOS/PFOA contamination** — PFOS manufactured by 3M has been found in the blood of Arctic polar bears, Pacific dolphins, and seabird eggs in Antarctica. Bioaccumulation through food chains is documented at all latitudes.
   *Source: AMAP Arctic Report, OSPAR*

2. **Contamination of Scheldt River floodplains** — Floodplain meadows along the Scheldt are so heavily contaminated with PFOS that local residents were instructed not to consume livestock raised on these pastures.
   *Source: VMM Belgium, Provincie Antwerpen*

3. **Contamination of Minnesota lakes** — PFAS from 3M has been found in fish from dozens of lakes within a 50 km radius of the company's plants. Fish consumption advisories are in effect in several counties of the state.
   *Source: MPCA Fish Consumption Advisories*

---

## 6. Has the corporation evaded accountability and compensation for those harmed?

**Answer: YES**. 3M denied the harm of PFAS for decades and only paid compensation under pressure from the courts.

1. **Years of denial of harm (2000–2018)** — After voluntarily phasing out PFOS production in 2002, 3M publicly insisted for 15 years that there was no evidence of harm to human health, challenging the scientific data.
   *Source: 3M public statements, EWG archives*

2. **Minnesota settlement: $850 million against billions in actual damage** — The 2018 settlement of $850 million was deemed by environmentalists to be clearly insufficient to remediate PFAS contamination in the state's drinking water.
   *Source: Minnesota AG, MPR News*

3. **Terms of the $12.5 billion settlement (2023) contested by victims** — Several municipalities rejected the settlement terms, considering the compensation amounts insufficient to actually clean up water intakes, the cost of which could reach tens of billions of dollars.
   *Source: Bloomberg Law, Water Finance & Management*

---

## 7. Has the corporation continued environmentally harmful activities despite known risks?

**Answer: YES**. 3M announced it would exit PFAS manufacturing only by 2025, continuing to produce certain compounds in the interim.

1. **Continued production of fluoropolymers** — After the "voluntary" phase-out of PFOS production (2002), 3M continued manufacturing other PFAS compounds, including fluoropolymers for electronics. EU bans on many of these only took effect in 2023–2024.
   *Source: ECHA PFAS restriction, 2023*

2. **Sale of AFFF assets without remediating contamination** — 3M sold AFFF formulations to other manufacturers without creating funds to remediate the harm already caused at military bases.
   *Source: EWG Military PFAS Contamination Report*

3. **Military contracts for PFAS-containing products** — Until the early 2020s, 3M retained military contracts for the supply of PFAS-containing products, despite the known toxicity.
   *Source: DoD PFAS contracts database*

---

## 8. Is the corporation complicit in human rights violations or environmental racism?

**Answer: YES**. Military bases with PFAS contamination are disproportionately located near low-income communities and communities of colour.

1. **Water contamination in Indigenous communities** — Several military bases and airfields with PFAS contamination are located near Native American reservations where fishing is a traditional food source. The risks from contamination are disproportionately high for these communities.
   *Source: EWG, EPA EJ analysis*

2. **Plant workers in Alabama** — Workers at 3M's plant in Florala, Alabama — predominantly African American — were exposed to PFAS without adequate information about the risks. Occupational diseases were not adequately compensated.
   *Source: Alabama court records, EWG*

3. **Belgian farmers along the Scheldt** — Farmers whose land is located in the Scheldt floodplain are forced to destroy crops and livestock due to PFOS contamination originating from the 3M plant. Compensation from the company was the subject of protracted disputes.
   *Source: VRT News, Provincie Antwerpen*
