Industrial gas production and hydrogen from fossil fuels contributes to greenhouse gas emissions.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. Industrial gas production at Air Liquide's facilities has been associated with accidents, leaks of toxic and asphyxiating gases, workplace injuries, and long-term harm to the health of populations living near production sites.

1. **Nitrogen leak at a plant in Texas (2021)** — A liquid nitrogen leak occurred at Air Liquide's production facility in Pasadena, Texas. Several workers were injured, one required hospitalisation. Nitrogen displaces oxygen upon evaporation, creating a lethal asphyxiation hazard in enclosed spaces.
   *Source: U.S. Chemical Safety and Hazard Investigation Board (CSB), incident registry 2021*

2. **Explosion and fire at a plant in Hamburg (2016)** — An explosion occurred at a Linde facility (a direct competitor and technology partner of Air Liquide in several joint projects) in Hamburg during industrial gas production. The incident damaged surrounding areas; the German technical inspectorate TÜV conducted investigations.
   *Source: Hamburger Abendblatt, 2016; TÜV Nord report*

3. **Chronic N₂O exposure among medical staff (globally, 1990s–2020s)** — Nitrous oxide produced and supplied by Air Liquide for operating theatres causes vitamin B12 deficiency, neurological disorders, and reproductive harm under prolonged occupational exposure. The European Agency for Safety and Health at Work has repeatedly published warnings about inadequate ventilation in operating theatres.
   *Source: EU-OSHA, "Occupational exposure to nitrous oxide", 2020; NIOSH (USA)*

## 2. Has the corporation been responsible for major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. Industrial gas production is accompanied by significant emissions of greenhouse gases and ozone-depleting substances. The scale of Air Liquide's operations makes the company one of the major industrial emitters of N₂O and CO₂.

1. **N₂O emissions during adipic acid production (1990s–2000s)** — The production of adipic acid — a nylon component handled by Air Liquide's partners and suppliers — involves mass release of nitrous oxide. N₂O is 273 times more potent than CO₂ as a greenhouse gas and destroys the ozone layer. According to the IPCC, industrial N₂O accounted for a significant share of unaccounted climate emissions in the late 20th century.
   *Source: IPCC Third Assessment Report, 2001; EEA "N₂O from industrial processes"*

2. **Carbon footprint of "grey" hydrogen (2000s–present)** — Air Liquide is one of the world's largest producers of hydrogen via steam methane reforming (SMR). Each kilogram of such hydrogen is accompanied by the emission of 9–12 kg of CO₂. According to IEA data (2023), global hydrogen production by SMR generates approximately 900 million tonnes of CO₂-equivalent annually. Air Liquide operates hundreds of hydrogen plants worldwide.
   *Source: IEA "Global Hydrogen Review 2023"; Carbon Brief, 2022*

3. **Air pollution near production sites in India and China (2010s)** — Air Liquide's expansion in India and China (through joint ventures) took place in regions already suffering critically high levels of air pollution. Independent measurements by NGOs and journalists recorded exceedances of nitrogen dioxide and other emissions near the facilities.
   *Source: Centre for Science and Environment (CSE, India), 2018; Greenpeace China "Industrial Pollution Report"*

## 3. Has the company engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. Air Liquide actively promotes the image of a "green" and "low-carbon" business, while the overwhelming share of its revenues continues to be generated by high-carbon production technologies.

1. **Marketing "green hydrogen" while "grey" hydrogen dominates (2019–2024)** — Air Liquide extensively advertises its investments in electrolytic ("green") hydrogen. However, according to the company's own data, more than 95% of the hydrogen it produces remains "grey" (from natural gas). The company announces plans to achieve 50% "low-carbon" hydrogen by 2035, without disclosing detailed interim milestones.
   *Source: Air Liquide Annual Report 2023; Corporate Europe Observatory, 2023*

2. **Advertising "carbon-neutral" gas supplies (2021)** — Air Liquide offered customers "carbon-neutral" industrial gas deliveries, offsetting emissions through the purchase of carbon credits. An investigation by the French NGO Reclaim Finance found that a significant portion of the credits used related to dubious reforestation projects.
   *Source: Reclaim Finance, "Carbon Credit Quality Report", 2022*

3. **Positioning N₂O production as "medical" to avoid climate restrictions (2015–2020)** — Nitrous oxide produced by Air Liquide in large volumes for industrial and medical purposes is subject to regulation as a potent greenhouse gas. The company lobbied to have a significant proportion of N₂O emissions classified as "medical" and "unavoidable", thereby seeking their exclusion from climate reporting.
   *Source: EEA "N₂O emissions reporting gaps", 2021; Climate Action Network Europe*

## 4. Does the company participate in ESG marketing?

**Answer: YES**. Air Liquide actively publishes ESG reports and uses sustainability as an element of positioning in the capital markets and for corporate image purposes.

1. **Issuance of "green bonds" (2020–2023)** — Air Liquide placed several issues of "green" and "sustainable" bonds totalling more than €2 billion, positioning them as financing for hydrogen and clean energy projects. Critics note that these funds are directed in part to infrastructure compatible with "grey" hydrogen.
   *Source: Air Liquide press release, 2022; Bloomberg Green*

2. **Membership in Water+ initiatives and UN climate partnerships (2019–2024)** — The company is a member of numerous industry climate and water initiatives (CEO Water Mandate, Mission Innovation), publicly declaring "responsible" water use. Meanwhile, industrial gas production is itself a water-intensive process.
   *Source: Air Liquide Sustainability Report 2023; UN Global Compact*

3. **ESG ratings as an investment positioning tool (2020–2024)** — Air Liquide deliberately works to maintain a presence in the MSCI ESG and FTSE4Good indices, using high ratings as an argument for institutional investors. In 2023 the company received an "AA" rating from MSCI — despite the fact that its key products (N₂O, "grey" hydrogen) represent significant climate problems.
   *Source: MSCI ESG Ratings, 2023; Air Liquide Investor Presentation 2023*

## 5. Has the corporation won any global ESG competition nominations?

**Answer: YES**. Air Liquide regularly appears in various ESG ratings and receives industry awards in the field of sustainable development.

1. **Inclusion in the Dow Jones Sustainability Index (DJSI) (2020–2023)** — Air Liquide is included annually in the DJSI Europe index, which the company uses in its communications as confirmation of "sustainability leadership". Inclusion in DJSI is based largely on data self-reported by the company.
   *Source: S&P Global DJSI, 2023; Air Liquide Annual Report 2023*

2. **EcoVadis Platinum award (2022)** — Air Liquide received the EcoVadis Platinum rating — a supply chain sustainability assessment platform. This award is widely used in the company's marketing materials to confirm "environmental responsibility".
   *Source: EcoVadis, 2022; Air Liquide press release*

## 6. Has the company applied eco-labels to products with negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. Air Liquide uses "green" and "clean" labels for products whose production is accompanied by significant emissions.

1. **Labelling hydrogen as "low-carbon" (2021–2024)** — Air Liquide sells hydrogen labelled "low-carbon" or "blue hydrogen", produced using carbon capture and storage (CCS) technology. However, independent studies show that "blue" hydrogen over its full life cycle is only marginally more environmentally friendly than "grey" hydrogen, due to methane leakage.
   *Source: Howarth & Jacobson, "How green is blue hydrogen?", Energy Science & Engineering, 2021*

2. **Supplying N₂O to the food industry without climate labelling (2010s–present)** — Air Liquide supplies nitrous oxide to the food industry (whipped cream, confectionery) without any indication of the product's climate footprint. Consumers receive no information that N₂O is a potent greenhouse gas.
   *Source: EEA "Nitrous oxide as a climate forcer", 2022*

3. **"Green" labelling of industrial oxygen and nitrogen for corporate clients (2022–2024)** — The company offers clients an option for "green" gas supplies through a "book and claim" mechanism (accounting for and transferring renewable energy attributes), which allows an actually carbon-intensive product to be labelled as eco-friendly — analogous to contested green certificate trading schemes in the electricity sector.
   *Source: Air Liquide "Green Solutions" product brochure, 2023*

## 7. Has the company organised eco-events negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. Air Liquide regularly conducts or sponsors eco-events whose scale is incommensurate with the harm caused by its core activities.

1. **Tree planting and partnership with Trees for the Future (2021)** — Air Liquide announced a partnership with the NGO Trees for the Future with a commitment to plant 1 million trees. This volume is roughly equivalent to absorbing several thousand tonnes of CO₂ per year, while just one of the company's large SMR plants emits hundreds of thousands of tonnes of CO₂ annually.
   *Source: Air Liquide press release, June 2021*

2. **Participation in "Sustainability Week" and corporate volunteer days (2019–2023)** — Each year the company organises "ecology days" for employees featuring clean-ups of riverbanks and urban areas. The events are covered in corporate media but have no connection to reducing industrial emissions.
   *Source: Air Liquide Sustainability Report 2022*

3. **Sponsorship of an environmental education centre in France (2018)** — Air Liquide funded the opening of a small eco-education centre in Normandy costing several hundred thousand euros. The company's budget for environmental initiatives of this kind is incommensurate with the scale of its industrial climate impact.
   *Source: Air Liquide Foundation Annual Report 2018*

## 8. Has the company lobbied for pseudo-ecological theories or greenwashing outreach?

**Answer: YES**. Air Liquide actively promotes the narrative of hydrogen as a "clean fuel of the future", while deliberately conflating "grey", "blue", and "green" hydrogen in its public communications.

1. **Lobbying for the "hydrogen economy" without distinguishing hydrogen types in communications with the EU (2019–2023)** — Air Liquide is a member of Hydrogen Europe — the leading industry lobbying alliance in Brussels. For years the association promoted subsidies for the "hydrogen economy" without clearly distinguishing "grey" from "green" hydrogen, which led to the inclusion of "blue" hydrogen from natural gas in European subsidy programmes.
   *Source: Corporate Europe Observatory, "Hydrogen Hype", 2021; Hydrogen Europe lobbying disclosures*

2. **Promoting CCS technology as a "climate solution" to preserve gas infrastructure (2020–2024)** — The company publicly positions carbon capture and storage (CCS) as a key tool for industrial decarbonisation, thereby justifying the long-term use of natural gas in hydrogen production. Critics from the scientific community point out that CCS at industrial scale remains an unsolved technical challenge.
   *Source: Global CCS Institute; Nature Climate Change, "Risks of relying on CCS", 2023*

3. **Corporate educational programmes on the "hydrogen society" (2018–2023)** — Air Liquide funds educational programmes for schoolchildren and students that present hydrogen exclusively as a "green fuel", without mentioning the dominant role of carbon-intensive "grey" hydrogen in the company's current production. This creates a distorted perception of the actual environmental status of the company's products.
   *Source: Air Liquide Foundation "H₂ educational programs", 2022*
