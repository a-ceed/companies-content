APRIL Group: Pulp at the Cost of Sumatran Forests

APRIL Group (Asian Pacific Integrated Mill) operates one of the world's largest pulp complexes in Riau Province, Sumatra. Analysis of satellite data by WWF and Eyes on the Forest showed that between 2000 and 2015 the company destroyed more than 150,000 hectares of forests — including peat forests that are critical habitats for Sumatran tigers and orangutans.

Following a large-scale NGO campaign, Staples and other major corporations cancelled their contracts with APRIL, which led to the adoption in 2015 of a new "Forest Conservation Policy" with a zero-deforestation commitment.

Independent monitoring from 2018 to 2022 found partial compliance with commitments on the company's own land, but persistent problems in the supply chains of third-party suppliers.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. Peatland fires triggered by forest clearance on APRIL concessions created smog that damaged the health of millions of people.

1. **Smog from peatland fires in 2015** — Fires on Sumatran peatlands, including in the APRIL concession zone, created catastrophic smog that covered Southeast Asia. A Harvard/Columbia study (2016) estimated mortality linked to this smog at 100,000 people in Indonesia, Malaysia and Singapore.
   *Source: Environmental Research Letters — "Mortality impacts of the 2015 Indonesian fires"*

2. **Violation of the rights of indigenous peoples of Riau** — Eyes on the Forest documented cases where APRIL forest clearance destroyed the traditional lands of Orang Rimba and other indigenous communities of Sumatra, depriving them of their food sources (fishing, hunting, fruit gathering).
   *Source: Eyes on the Forest — APRIL monitoring reports*

3. **Working conditions at the pulp mill** — NGOs recorded that conditions at the RAPP plant (APRIL subsidiary) did not meet safety standards: inadequate protective equipment when working with chlorine and other pulp-bleaching chemicals.
   *Source: RAN — APRIL labor monitoring*

## 2. Has the corporation caused major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. APRIL destroyed more than 150,000 hectares of Sumatran tropical forests, including peatlands with unique biodiversity.

1. **Destruction of 150,000+ hectares of Sumatran forests (2000–2015)** — Satellite analysis by WWF and Eyes on the Forest showed that over 15 years APRIL cleared more than 150,000 hectares of tropical forests — including peatlands storing colossal carbon reserves. This was accompanied by CO₂ emissions comparable to the annual output of entire countries.
   *Source: Eyes on the Forest; WWF — APRIL Deforestation Analysis*

2. **Destruction of the Sumatran tiger's range** — APRIL's deforestation fragmented the dispersal corridors of the Sumatran tiger (Panthera tigris sumatrae), listed as "Critically Endangered" by the IUCN. Estimates suggest fewer than 400 individuals remained in 2015.
   *Source: IUCN Red List; WWF Tiger Report*

3. **Pollution of Sumatran rivers by mill effluents** — The RAPP pulp mill in Pangkalan Kerinci (Riau Province) discharged process wastewater into rivers, causing mass fish kills and eutrophication of water bodies on which riverside communities depended.
   *Source: Jikalahari (Indonesian Forest Advocacy NGO)*

## 3. Has the corporation engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. The "Forest Conservation Policy" of 2015 was presented as a reform, but monitoring revealed persistent violations.

1. **"Forest Conservation Policy" (2015) — declaration without implementation** — Following an NGO campaign that caused major clients (Staples, Office Depot) to cancel purchases, APRIL adopted a zero-deforestation declaration. However, independent monitoring in 2016–2021 continued to record clearances in the supply chain of third-party suppliers.
   *Source: RAN — APRIL monitoring (2019)*

2. **Positioning as a "sustainable" company** — APRIL uses a narrative of "sustainable paper production" and "forest restoration" in B2B marketing, while satellite data showed that clearance volumes among suppliers exceeded restoration volumes.
   *Source: Chain Reaction Research — APRIL Scorecard*

---

## 4. Does the corporation engage in ESG marketing?

**Answer: YES**. APRIL publishes ESG reports and actively communicates on sustainability topics.

1. **Annual Sustainability Reports** — APRIL Group publishes sustainability reports declaring progress on peatland protection, forest restoration and emissions reduction.
   *Source: APRIL Sustainability Report 2023*

2. **RAPP "Restorasi Ekosistem Riau" (RER) programme** — The company launched a programme to restore 150,000 hectares of degraded peatlands on concession land, actively promoting it in ESG communications.
   *Source: RER Official Website*

---

## 5. Has the corporation won awards in global ESG competitions?

**Answer: NO**. APRIL is not included in key international ESG ratings and has not received significant ESG awards.

1. The company is not publicly listed and does not disclose MSCI or Sustainalytics ratings. International environmental NGOs continue to criticise the company for insufficient progress.
   *Source: Chain Reaction Research; RAN*

---

## 6. Has the corporation placed eco-labels on products with negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. APRIL's paper products are sold with environmental certifications whose credibility is disputed.

1. **PEFC certificate despite recorded violations** — Paper and cardboard under APRIL brands are certified by PEFC (Programme for the Endorsement of Forest Certification), positioning them as "responsibly produced". However, independent audits identified violations in supplier chains.
   *Source: Eyes on the Forest; RAN*

---

## 7. Has the corporation organised eco-events negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. The RER peatland restoration programme is negligible compared to the historical destruction caused.

1. **RER programme: 150,000 ha of restoration against 150,000+ ha of destruction** — The company is investing in the restoration of 150,000 hectares of peatlands under the RER programme. However, the volume of forests it historically destroyed is comparable or greater, and the programme applies only to a portion of concession land.
   *Source: Eyes on the Forest analysis of RER*

---

## 8. Has the corporation lobbied for pseudo-ecological theories?

**Answer: YES**. APRIL lobbies for the narrative of forest plantations as a "solution" for the climate.

1. **Promoting acacia as a "climate-neutral" fibre** — APRIL promotes acacia plantations as a "sustainable" source of pulp, while concealing the fact that they were created in place of felled peat forests that stored thousands of times more carbon than young plantations.
   *Source: RAN analysis*

2. **Opposition to stricter peatland moratoria** — Through industry associations, APRIL participated in opposing Indonesian regulatory initiatives to protect primary peatlands, using arguments about the "economic necessity" of plantations for the development of Riau.
   *Source: Chain Reaction Research*
