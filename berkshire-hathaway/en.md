PacifiCorp — largest US coal power generator — emits millions of tonnes of CO₂ annually.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. Coal-fired power plants operated by PacifiCorp emit mercury, toxic ash and fine particulate matter that cause cancer, cardiovascular and respiratory diseases among the populations of western US states. Derailments of BNSF Railway trains have led to oil and toxic chemical spills.

1. **BNSF Railway train derailment oil spill in Montana (2015)** — Near Glendive, Montana, a BNSF Railway train derailed and spilled approximately 30,000 gallons of crude oil into the Yellowstone River. More than 6,000 residents of Glendive lost access to drinking water. The river was closed for use for several weeks.
   *Source: Associated Press, January 2015; Montana DEQ*

2. **Mercury contamination from PacifiCorp coal plants in Utah and Wyoming (2010–2021)** — The EPA determined that PacifiCorp's coal-fired power plants, including Hunter and Huntington in Utah, are among the largest industrial sources of mercury emissions in the western United States. Mercury accumulates in fish and, through the food chain, damages the nervous systems of children and pregnant women.
   *Source: EPA National Emissions Inventory; Sierra Club, 2021*

3. **Deaths in the BNSF train derailment carrying hazardous chemicals in Illinois (2012)** — A BNSF freight train derailment near Cherry Valley, Illinois, led to a fire and ethanol release. The accident killed 2 people, thousands of residents were evacuated, and Kishwaukee Creek was contaminated.
   *Source: NTSB Accident Report DCA-10-MR-010, 2012*

## 2. Has the corporation been responsible for major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. Berkshire Hathaway's operations through PacifiCorp and BNSF Railway have had a devastating impact on aquatic and terrestrial ecosystems in the western and central United States.

1. **Mercury contamination of rivers and lakes in the PacifiCorp coal plant zones (ongoing)** — Mercury emissions from PacifiCorp's coal-fired power plants in Utah, Wyoming and Montana settle in bodies of water and accumulate in fish. Health authorities in several states have imposed restrictions on fish consumption from rivers within the affected zones.
   *Source: EPA, Utah Division of Water Quality; Sierra Club*

2. **Coal dust contamination along BNSF Railway routes (ongoing)** — BNSF open gondola cars lose between 500 and 3,000 pounds of coal dust per car on a 1,000-mile journey. The coal settles in rivers, lakes and agricultural land along routes through Montana, Idaho, Washington and Oregon. Fine coal particles cause acidification of bodies of water.
   *Source: Columbia Riverkeeper; Pacific Environment, 2012*

3. **Oil spill in the Yellowstone River, Montana (2011)** — An ExxonMobil pipeline ruptured beneath the Yellowstone River; however, BNSF Railway was one of the largest oil carriers in the region and faced criticism for numerous tank-car leaks. The Yellowstone River itself was contaminated with oil along 240 km, causing damage to fish stocks and riparian vegetation.
   *Source: Montana DEQ; National Wildlife Federation, 2011*

## 3. Has the corporation violated the rights of indigenous peoples?

**Answer: YES**. The routing of BNSF Railway lines and coal extraction in the Powder River Basin region violated the rights of the Lakota, Crow and Cheyenne peoples.

1. **Transport of oil through sacred Lakota lands (2014–2016)** — BNSF Railway transported Bakken crude oil through territories the Lakota (Sioux Nation) consider sacred and where ancestral burial sites are located. Communities repeatedly lodged protests, but BNSF refused to reroute.
   *Source: Standing Rock Sioux Tribe statements; Indian Country Today, 2015*

2. **Coal mines on Crow Nation lands in Montana (ongoing)** — Coal extraction in the Powder River Basin, transported by BNSF, includes operations in native territories. Some extraction takes place in zones where the Crow and Northern Cheyenne Nations sought the right to control their subsurface resources, but contract terms remained non-transparent.
   *Source: Northern Cheyenne Tribe; Earthjustice filings, 2013*

3. **Damage to indigenous fishing grounds in the northwestern United States from coal dust (2010–2015)** — Coal dust drifting from BNSF cars damaged salmon spawning grounds on the Columbia and Snake Rivers, which are traditional fishing grounds of the Nez Perce, Yakama and other tribes. Fish absorb coal particles, affecting their biochemical composition.
   *Source: Columbia River Inter-Tribal Fish Commission, 2013*

## 4. Has the corporation lied to consumers or authorities?

**Answer: YES**. Berkshire Hathaway evaded transparent disclosure of climate risks and the real environmental footprint of its subsidiaries.

1. **Refusal to disclose climate risks in reporting (2020–2022)** — Berkshire Hathaway shareholders at the 2020 and 2021 annual meetings proposed resolutions requiring mandatory disclosure of climate risks in accordance with TCFD standards. Warren Buffett and the board of directors rejected the resolutions, claiming climate risks were "insufficiently material" — a position that contradicted the assessments of most financial analysts.
   *Source: Berkshire Hathaway Proxy Statements, 2020, 2021; As You Sow*

2. **Underreporting of PacifiCorp emissions to regulators (2015)** — PacifiCorp was caught underestimating mercury emission levels in permit applications submitted to the EPA. The company used outdated emission factors that produced values lower than actual independent measurements.
   *Source: Sierra Club Sierra legal complaint, 2016*

3. **False decarbonisation commitments by MidAmerican Energy (2020)** — The MidAmerican Energy subsidiary publicly announced plans to achieve 100% renewable generation in Iowa, but environmental groups found that the company was simultaneously extending coal plant licences and seeking regulatory approval to continue operating them beyond 2030.
   *Source: Iowa Environmental Council; Iowa Utilities Board filings, 2021*

## 5. Has the corporation had systematic safety violations?

**Answer: YES**. BNSF Railway has a long history of safety violations — derailments, hazardous material spills and breaches of labour law.

1. **Series of Bakken crude oil train derailments (2013–2016)** — Between 2013 and 2016, BNSF Railway trains carrying Bakken oil (North Dakota) derailed at least 8 times across different states. The 2015 derailment in Casselman, Virginia, caused a fire that threatened residential neighbourhoods. The Federal Railroad Administration (FRA) issued notices of violation that BNSF implemented with delays.
   *Source: NTSB; FRA Enforcement Actions, 2015–2016*

2. **Violations of engineer hours-of-service rules (ongoing)** — The FRA repeatedly fined BNSF Railway for violating rest requirements for engineers who were forced to work beyond the 12-hour limit due to chronic staff shortages. Engineer fatigue was cited as a contributing factor in several derailments.
   *Source: FRA Compliance Records; Bureau of Transportation Statistics*

3. **Safety standard violations at PacifiCorp coal plants (2018)** — The EPA brought enforcement actions against PacifiCorp for violations of Clean Air Act standards at the Hunter (Utah) and Jim Bridger (Wyoming) plants, including SO₂ and particulate emissions that exceeded permitted levels.
   *Source: EPA Region 8 Enforcement; Earthjustice, 2018*

## 6. Has the corporation lobbied against environmental regulations?

**Answer: YES**. Berkshire Hathaway, through PacifiCorp, MidAmerican Energy and BNSF Railway, systematically opposes the tightening of environmental standards for the coal and rail industries.

1. **Opposition to coal plant closures in Utah and Wyoming (2017–2021)** — PacifiCorp lobbied state regulatory bodies in Utah and Wyoming against the accelerated closure of coal-fired power plants required under decarbonisation plans. The company hired lobbyists to pressure legislators, securing delays in the closure of the Hunter and Huntington plants.
   *Source: Utah Public Service Commission filings; Sierra Club*

2. **Lobbying against stricter coal dust standards for BNSF (2012–2014)** — BNSF Railway, through the industry association Association of American Railroads (AAR), sought to defeat or weaken EPA proposals for mandatory covering of coal dust cargo. The standards were never adopted at the federal level.
   *Source: Columbia Riverkeeper; EPA docket*

3. **Blocking shareholder climate resolutions (2020–2023)** — Berkshire Hathaway's management consistently recommended that shareholders vote "against" all climate transparency resolutions, including proposals to set emissions reduction targets for PacifiCorp and MidAmerican Energy, citing "management autonomy."
   *Source: As You Sow; Berkshire Hathaway Proxy 2020–2023*

## 7. Has the corporation's activity contributed to climate change?

**Answer: YES**. Berkshire Hathaway is one of the largest corporate participants in the US coal economy, providing both its transport infrastructure through BNSF and its end consumption through PacifiCorp.

1. **Annual CO₂ emissions from PacifiCorp coal plants exceed 30 million tonnes (ongoing)** — PacifiCorp operates coal capacity of more than 5 GW. According to EIA data, its CO₂ emissions amount to approximately 30–35 million tonnes per year — more than some European nations and significantly more than any other publicly traded utility on the US West Coast.
   *Source: EIA; EPA eGRID database*

2. **BNSF transports hundreds of millions of tonnes of coal annually** — BNSF Railway handles transportation of coal from the Powder River Basin — the largest coal deposit in the United States. BNSF annually carries 130–150 million tonnes of coal, keeping dozens of coal-fired power plants running across the western and central parts of the country.
   *Source: BNSF Annual Report; Energy Information Administration*

3. **Major investments in Chevron and Occidental Petroleum** — Despite the climate crisis, Berkshire Hathaway holds Chevron shares worth more than $19 billion (as of 2023) and Occidental Petroleum shares worth more than $12 billion. This makes Berkshire one of the largest private investors in the oil sector in the world, directly contradicting Buffett's stated concern for the climate.
   *Source: Berkshire Hathaway 13-F filings, 2023; Bloomberg*

## 8. Has the corporation shifted environmental costs onto society?

**Answer: YES**. The health damage to the population from PacifiCorp's coal plants and the contamination of waterways by BNSF is paid for by the healthcare system and state budgets, not by Berkshire Hathaway.

1. **Medical costs from coal plant air pollution borne by insurers and the state** — A Harvard School of Public Health study (2011) showed that the "hidden" costs of coal power generation in the United States amount to $345 billion per year — including treatment costs for asthma, COPD and cardiovascular diseases. These costs are borne by patients, insurers and government Medicaid/Medicare programmes, but not by coal companies.
   *Source: Harvard School of Public Health, "Full cost accounting for the life cycle of coal", 2011*

2. **BNSF oil spill clean-up funded by the state** — After major BNSF train derailments involving oil tankers (Glendive 2015, Cherry Valley 2012, and others), a significant portion of river and bank remediation work was funded from government emergency spill-response funds. BNSF covered only part of the actual damage.
   *Source: Montana DEQ; NTSB accident cost estimates*

3. **Mercury contamination of waterways: fishing bans and losses for anglers** — Mercury contamination of rivers in PacifiCorp coal plant zones forced the states of Utah and Wyoming to introduce restrictions on consumption of caught fish. Losses to local fishermen and fishing tourism were not compensated by PacifiCorp.
   *Source: Utah Division of Wildlife Resources; EPA Fish Consumption Advisories*

## Summary of Answers

1. **Coal dependence** — PacifiCorp remains the largest coal generator in the western United States, emitting tens of millions of tonnes of CO₂ and toxic pollutants annually.
2. **Transport infrastructure of the coal economy** — BNSF Railway carries 130–150 million tonnes of coal per year, making Berkshire an indispensable link in the carbon-intensive economy.
3. **Mercury contamination** — PacifiCorp's coal plants are among the largest sources of mercury in the atmosphere of the western states.
4. **Train derailments and oil spills** — The series of BNSF accidents in 2012–2016 caused damage to rivers, communities and ecosystems.
5. **Oil investments** — Multi-billion dollar stakes in Chevron and Occidental Petroleum make Berkshire one of the largest fossil fuel investors.
6. **Blocking climate transparency** — Management systematically rejects shareholder resolutions on climate risks and emission targets.
7. **Violation of indigenous peoples' rights** — BNSF routes pass through sacred lands and fishing grounds of indigenous peoples.
8. **Shifting costs** — The health damage from coal pollution and the costs of accident clean-up are borne by society, not by Berkshire.
