Carnival Corporation: Floating Polluters

Carnival Corporation — the world's largest cruise operator — has been subject to major criminal investigations for illegal discharges of contaminated wastewater at sea.

In 2016, Carnival paid $40 million in fines and agreed to environmental oversight after Princess Cruises pleaded guilty to illegally dumping oily waste from seven ships directly into the ocean — bypassing treatment systems. Leaked documents revealed that the company used a "magic pipe" bypass to circumvent mandatory filters.

In Alaska, Carnival cruise ships annually discharge tens of millions of gallons of "grey" wastewater (from showers and kitchens) containing detergents, pathogens and heavy metals directly into fjords. In 2022, the US Senate passed legislation tightening discharge rules in Alaskan waters.

In 2019, the company again paid $20 million for violating the terms of its probation.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. Illegal discharges from Carnival ships created health risks for coastal populations and port communities.

1. **"Magic pipe" — illegal discharge of oily waste (2016)** — Princess Cruises (a Carnival subsidiary) was convicted of using bypass pipes to circumvent filters when discharging oil-contaminated bilge water from seven ships into the ocean. The company pleaded guilty to criminal charges (environmental crime) and paid $40 million in fines.
   *Source: U.S. DOJ — Princess Cruises criminal plea (2016)*

2. **Outbreaks of infectious diseases on board** — Carnival Corporation ships have repeatedly been the site of norovirus and COVID-19 outbreaks. In spring 2020, Carnival cruise ships (Princess Cruises' Diamond Princess and Grand Princess) became among the first significant COVID-19 clusters, resulting in passenger deaths. The company was criticised for delayed reporting and inadequate sanitary measures.
   *Source: CDC — Cruise Ship COVID-19 Outbreak Reports (2020)*

3. **Contamination of Alaskan coastal waters with wastewater** — Carnival ships annually discharge tens of millions of gallons of grey wastewater into Alaskan fjords. These waters contain detergents, pathogens, heavy metals and food waste, threatening the fishing grounds of Alaska Native peoples.
   *Source: NRDC — "Cruise Control" Report; Oceana Cruise Ship Pollution*

## 2. Has the corporation been the cause of major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. Systematic illegal ocean discharges and releases of toxic substances in coastal waters are documented environmental violations.

1. **Illegal discharges of oil waste from 7 ships (2016–2019)** — The discovered "magic pipe" scheme covered seven Princess Cruises ships and involved regular illegal discharges of oil-contaminated bilge water. Falsification of oil record books was also discovered.
   *Source: U.S. DOJ Princess Cruises*

2. **$20 million fine for probation violation (2019)** — Carnival again violated the terms of its environmental probation: Costa Cruises ships were discharging plastic bags and food waste in prohibited waters around the Bahamas. An additional $20 million fine was imposed.
   *Source: U.S. DOJ — Carnival plea agreement update (2019)*

3. **SOx emissions in Mediterranean ports** — The Cruise Lines International Association acknowledged that cruise ships in the Mediterranean produce six times more sulphur oxides than all European cars combined. Carnival is the largest operator in the region.
   *Source: Transport & Environment — "Cruise Ships Pollution" (2019)*

## 3. Has the corporation engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. Carnival declares "sustainable cruising" against a backdrop of documented criminal environmental violations.

1. **"Sustainable Cruising" marketing after criminal conviction** — After Princess Cruises was convicted in 2016 of a criminal environmental offence, Carnival Corporation continued running marketing campaigns about "sustainable cruising" and "ocean conservation", without informing consumers of the subsidiary's criminal status.
   *Source: Carnival Corporation marketing vs U.S. DOJ records*

2. **"Ocean Holiday Initiative"** — Carnival launched an "Ocean Holiday" programme focused on marine ecosystem preservation. Critics note that this programme appeared precisely after the illegal discharge scandals — a typical example of reactive greenwashing.
   *Source: Oceana analysis*

3. **"LNG as the solution" declarations** — Carnival positioned the switch to LNG (liquefied natural gas) on new ships as a climate solution, while concealing that LNG creates methane leaks and only marginally reduces SOx without addressing CO₂.
   *Source: Transport & Environment analysis*

---

## 4. Does the corporation engage in ESG marketing?

**Answer: YES**. Carnival actively communicates on sustainability in its corporate reports.

1. **Annual Corporate Sustainability Reports** — Carnival Corporation annually publishes sustainability reports declaring targets for reducing CO₂ emissions by 40% by 2030 and achieving "zero" wastewater discharge.
   *Source: Carnival Corporation Sustainability Report 2023*

2. **"Save the Waves"** — The company sponsors the "Save the Waves" programme and other marine conservation initiatives, which are actively used in marketing communications.
   *Source: Carnival Corp CSR communications*

---

## 5. Has the corporation won awards in global ESG competitions?

**Answer: NO**. Carnival does not feature in major ESG indices and has repeatedly received anti-awards from environmental NGOs.

1. Oceana and other marine NGOs annually place Carnival in top lists of the most ocean-polluting companies. MSCI and Sustainalytics ratings classify the company as high ESG risk.
   *Source: Oceana Annual Reports; MSCI ESG*

---

## 6. Has the corporation placed eco-labels on products with negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. Cruise tours are sold with a narrative of "sustainable" and "responsible" recreation.

1. **"Sustainable Cruise" labelling of tours** — Some Carnival/Princess cruise products are positioned in advertising materials as "environmentally responsible" and "sustainable" travel, without disclosing the actual ecological footprint per passenger (incomparably larger than that of an aeroplane).
   *Source: Carnival marketing; Transport & Environment*

---

## 7. Has the corporation organised eco-events negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. Subsidisation of marine reserves and beach clean-ups are incomparable with the scale of pollution from the fleet.

1. **Sponsorship of sea turtle conservation** — Carnival sponsors small sea turtle conservation programmes, which are used as PR material without any proportional reduction in shipboard waste discharges.
   *Source: Carnival CSR*

2. **Organisation of beach clean-ups** — Carnival funds beach clean-ups in port cities — a typical example of a negligible eco-event against the backdrop of thousands of tonnes of waste annually discharged by the fleet.
   *Source: Oceana — Cruise Ship Waste*

---

## 8. Has the corporation lobbied for pseudo-ecological theories?

**Answer: YES**. Carnival lobbies against tightening environmental requirements for maritime shipping.

1. **Opposition to stricter discharge standards in Alaska** — The Cruise Lines International Association (CLIA), of which Carnival is a member, lobbied against Alaskan legislative initiatives to tighten wastewater discharge requirements for cruise ships, using arguments about "economic burden".
   *Source: Alaska Legislature records; NRDC advocacy*

2. **Promotion of "LNG as the clean future" of shipping** — Carnival and CLIA promote LNG as the "environmentally clean" fuel of the future, while independent research shows that methane leaks from LNG use can negate its climate advantages over heavy fuel oil.
   *Source: Transport & Environment — LNG methane slip analysis*
