According to an analysis conducted by Greenpeace, Coca-Cola produces over 100 billion plastic bottles every year — or 3,400 per second.

«Coca-Cola does not plan to phase out single-use plastic bottles in the near future, as drinks in such packaging are in high demand among consumers," said the company’s vice president, Bea Perez.

By producing a product that has been repeatedly recognized as harmful to health, whose popularity in children’s minds has been cultivated by marketers in the best traditions of consumer society, Coca-Cola is a global leader in plastic pollution.

These are the results of 239 brand audits conducted by Break Free From Plastic and Greenpeace volunteers in 42 countries across six continents.

Coca-Cola Corporation, unable to solve the technical problem related to the safe design of its product’s packaging, with a complete disregard for this design principle and an awareness of the global environmental catastrophe, continues its eco-terrorist business to this day.

«Business won’t be business if we stop focusing on consumer demands," said Bea Perez.

The corporation has already inflicted irreparable damage on nature, witnessed by everyone from inhabitants of oceanic islands to mountain conquerors. There are virtually no places accessible to people where Coca-Cola bottles cannot be found.

Meanwhile, plastic production could double in the next 20 years and quadruple by 2050.

According to research by the Ellen MacArthur Foundation, between 5 to 13 million tons of plastic enter the world’s oceans annually, and by 2050 the ocean will contain more plastic by weight than fish.

Scientists estimate that 90% of seabirds' bodies contain plastic waste.

Scientists from Ghent University in Belgium recently estimated that people who consume seafood ingest up to 11,000 tiny pieces of plastic each year.

Plastic is carcinogenic and, when accumulated, leads to death. Not only fish, but also huge whales die because their stomachs are stuffed with packaging from Coca-Cola, Pepsi, Nestle, Mars, and others.

Coca-Cola has been repeatedly condemned by the scientific community. However, with vast experience in countering various medical and environmental organizations, backed by brilliant marketing talent and substantial capital, the company fights for its existence.

The company aggressively uses greenwashing techniques to convince the public that it is a nature-friendly organization and a leader in eco-innovation.

For several years in a row, Coca-Cola has been recognized as a leader in sustainable development* and almost always ranks among the top three global and European beverage producers.

*Mentioning the concept of sustainable development, it is worth noting that this organization, over its 50-year formation, has failed all its environmental goals and, due to a lack of understanding or unwillingness to see the root causes of the global systemic crisis, will fail them in the future, choosing as its sustainable leader-the planet’s sustainable eco-terrorist.
