ConocoPhillips and Arctic Oil

ConocoPhillips is the largest oil producer in Alaska and one of the main contenders for developing the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge (ANWR) — one of the last intact Arctic ecosystems in the USA. The Willow Project (approved by the Biden administration in 2023) involves drilling 200+ wells in the tundra, threatening the habitat of caribou, polar bears and birds.

Independent expert calculations estimate that over its 30-year operational life, the Willow Project will release approximately 278 million tonnes of CO₂-equivalent into the atmosphere. Associated gas is produced during extraction, part of which is flared.

Oil extraction on permafrost creates specific environmental risks: permafrost thawing due to climate change can destabilise pipeline infrastructure, increasing the risk of spills in extremely vulnerable Arctic ecosystems.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. ConocoPhillips' operations have harmed the health and way of life of indigenous communities in Alaska and the Niger Delta, and hydraulic fracturing in the USA contaminated drinking water in residential areas.

1. **Alaska — violation of Iñupiat rights and livelihoods (2005–2024)** — Oil extraction in the North Slope region affects traditional Iñupiat hunting grounds. Pipeline ruptures and oil spills at ConocoPhillips facilities near Prudhoe Bay were recorded annually. Several Iñupiat communities documented the impact of pollution on fish stocks — the foundation of their food supply.
   *Source: Alaska Department of Environmental Conservation (ADEC) spill reports; Earthjustice, "Willow project impacts", 2023; Gwich'in Steering Committee*

2. **Nigeria, Niger Delta — spills and illness (1990s–2010s)** — ConocoPhillips operated in the Niger Delta through a subsidiary, where chronic spills destroyed mangrove forests and poisoned fishing grounds. According to Amnesty International, residents suffered from skin diseases and gastrointestinal illnesses due to water contamination from oil pollution.
   *Source: Amnesty International, "Petroleum, Pollution and Poverty in the Niger Delta", 2009; NOSDRA Nigeria spill database*

3. **Fracking in Pennsylvania and Colorado — drinking water contamination (2000s–2010s)** — ConocoPhillips hydraulic fracturing operations in Pennsylvania and Colorado were accompanied by resident complaints about well contamination with methane and drilling chemicals. State environmental agencies recorded several confirmed cases of groundwater contamination near the company's wells.
   *Source: EPA, "Hydraulic Fracturing for Oil and Gas", 2016; Pennsylvania DEP complaint records; ProPublica "Fracking the Eagle Ford Shale", 2012*

## 2. Has the corporation been the cause of major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. ConocoPhillips is linked to major oil spills in Alaska and the North Sea and is the initiator of a project threatening the Arctic ecosystem.

1. **Alaska oil spill — North Slope pipeline (2006)** — In March 2006, approximately 1,000 barrels of oil spilled from a ConocoPhillips pipeline near Prudhoe Bay. The pipeline was found to have suffered internal corrosion that the company had failed to remedy in time despite warnings from oversight services. The EPA and US Coast Guard initiated an investigation.
   *Source: U.S. Department of Transportation Pipeline and Hazardous Materials Safety Administration (PHMSA) incident report, 2006; Anchorage Daily News*

2. **Willow Project — threat to Arctic tundra (2023–2030)** — The project approved in 2023 involves large-scale industrial development of previously undisturbed tundra in Alaska. According to NRDC and Earthjustice assessments, road construction, flare facilities and drilling platforms will irreversibly disrupt caribou migration routes, salmon spawning grounds and polar bear habitats.
   *Source: NRDC, "Willow Project", 2023; Earthjustice legal brief; U.S. Bureau of Land Management EIS, 2023*

3. **North Sea spill — Norway (2008)** — An oil spill occurred at a ConocoPhillips Norwegian offshore facility affecting the North Sea. The Norwegian Petroleum Directorate (Petroleumstilsynet) issued an order to remedy safety violations; the incident required emergency response measures.
   *Source: Petroleumstilsynet (PSA Norway) incident report, 2008; Aftenposten*

## 3. Has the corporation engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. ConocoPhillips declares climate targets and "responsible extraction" while simultaneously expanding Arctic extraction and fracking in the USA.

1. **"Net zero" by 2050 declaration while expanding Arctic extraction (2021–2023)** — ConocoPhillips announced a goal of achieving carbon neutrality by 2050 for Scope 1 and 2 emissions. Simultaneously, the company continued lobbying and received approval for the Willow Project, which will generate significant CO₂ emissions annually over 30 years. Carbon Tracker noted a contradiction between declared targets and actual capital investments.
   *Source: Carbon Tracker, "Absolute Impact", 2022; ConocoPhillips Sustainability Report 2023; Earthjustice*

2. **"Responsible Arctic extraction" advertising** — ConocoPhillips publishes materials about a "minimal footprint" and "advanced environmental practices" in the Arctic region, using the term "responsible development". The Gwich'in Steering Committee and NRDC refuted these claims, pointing to the absence of independent verification and documented cases of tundra degradation.
   *Source: ConocoPhillips "Low Impact Development" marketing; Gwich'in Steering Committee rebuttal, 2023*

3. **Participation in OGMP 2.0 methane reduction partnership while emissions grow** — ConocoPhillips joined the Oil and Gas Climate Initiative (OGCI) and Oil and Gas Methane Partnership, declaring methane leak reductions. Independent Environmental Defense Fund analysis showed that actual methane emissions at the company's facilities are understated compared to satellite monitoring data.
   *Source: Environmental Defense Fund methane satellite analysis, 2022; OGCI company reporting*

## 4. Does the corporation engage in ESG marketing?

**Answer: YES**. ConocoPhillips actively uses ESG metrics in investor communications without changing the key parameters of its business model.

1. **ESG section in annual reports (2019–2024)** — Annual ConocoPhillips corporate reports contain extensive sections on ESG progress: carbon intensity reduction, gender diversity, occupational safety. However, the targets cover only Scope 1 and 2 emissions, ignoring Scope 3 — the largest category, associated with the combustion of extracted fuel by consumers.
   *Source: ConocoPhillips Sustainability Report 2022; Carbon Tracker analysis*

2. **"Pathways to Zero" programme — 2022** — ConocoPhillips announced a decarbonisation roadmap focused on carbon capture and storage (CCS). Independent analysts noted that the scale of the company's planned CCS projects amounts to less than 5% of its total emissions.
   *Source: ConocoPhillips Pathways to Zero, 2022; Institute for Energy Economics and Financial Analysis (IEEFA)*

3. **Emphasis on "ESG leadership" in shareholder negotiations (2021–2023)** — Company management systematically appeals to ESG ratings (MSCI "A", DJSI) when discussing shareholder climate resolutions, successfully blocking the most stringent requirements.
   *Source: ConocoPhillips proxy statements 2021–2023; Ceres investor engagement reports*

## 5. Has the corporation won awards in global ESG competitions?

**Answer: YES**. ConocoPhillips received ESG ratings and industry awards used to build a reputation as a responsible company.

1. **Inclusion in Dow Jones Sustainability Index — North America (2017–2022)** — The company was repeatedly included in DJSI North America, cited in corporate reports as confirmation of ESG leadership. DJSI rating criteria are criticised for insufficient weighting of climate risks in the oil and gas sector.
   *Source: S&P Global DJSI annual reports; ConocoPhillips Annual Report 2021*

2. **MSCI ESG rating "A" (2020–2022)** — ConocoPhillips received one of the highest MSCI ESG ratings among oil and gas companies, which the company cited in negotiations with institutional investors. IEEFA analysts noted that the MSCI methodology does not account for climate commitments incompatible with production plans.
   *Source: MSCI ESG Research; IEEFA analysis, 2022*

3. **Hart Energy ESG Award — "Best Practices" (2021)** — Industry publication Hart Energy awarded ConocoPhillips for "best ESG practices" in the US oil and gas industry. Environmental advocates viewed the award as an instrument for normalising Arctic extraction.
   *Source: Hart Energy ESG Awards 2021; Sierra Club response statement*

## 6. Has the corporation placed eco-labels on products with negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. ConocoPhillips promotes oil extraction using environmental rhetoric, creating an impression of "green" standards for partners and consumers.

1. **"Low Carbon" labelling for Alaska crude in marketing materials** — The company positioned Alaskan crude oil as having a "lower carbon footprint compared to the global average", using the term "low carbon intensity crude" in communications with oil buyers. Independent audit of the calculation methodology has not been conducted publicly.
   *Source: ConocoPhillips Alaska crude oil marketing materials; S&P Global Platts carbon intensity tracker*

2. **76 filling station fuel branding with environmental attributes (2000s–2010s)** — Before the Phillips 66 spin-off, the 76 fuel network (managed by ConocoPhillips) used advertising materials emphasising fuel "cleanliness" and reduction of toxic emissions. Under FTC Green Guides, such claims without qualified substantiation are considered misleading.
   *Source: FTC Green Guides, 16 CFR Part 260; 76 fuel advertising archive*

3. **"Environmentally responsible" brand for Alaska extraction** — In advertising publications and on the corporate website, ConocoPhillips describes tundra oil extraction as "fundamentally more environmentally sound" than in other regions of the world, citing "advanced drilling technologies". Environmental organisations dispute these claims.
   *Source: ConocoPhillips Alaska Operations overview; NRDC rebuttal, 2023*

## 7. Has the corporation organised eco-events negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. ConocoPhillips conducts small conservation events incomparable with the scale of the environmental consequences of its activities.

1. **Coral reef restoration programme in Australia (2010s)** — ConocoPhillips Australia funded a small coral reef restoration project in north-western Australia, widely covered in corporate media. The funding amount was several hundred thousand dollars, while the company's oil and gas extraction in the region generated multi-million impacts on the marine environment.
   *Source: ConocoPhillips Australia sustainability report, 2016; Australian Marine Conservation Society*

2. **School eco-project competition in Houston (annually)** — ConocoPhillips' Houston headquarters annually organises an ecological project competition for schoolchildren, coinciding with the release of the corporate ESG report. The event receives active coverage in local media at minimal cost.
   *Source: ConocoPhillips Houston community programs; Houston Chronicle, 2022*

3. **Medina River bank clean-up in Texas as corporate volunteering (2019)** — ConocoPhillips employees participated in a Medina River bank clean-up in Texas — an annual corporate volunteering event. Environmentalists noted that in the same year the company lobbied to weaken wastewater standards for Texas oil fields.
   *Source: ConocoPhillips volunteer program report, 2019; Texas Commission on Environmental Quality lobbying records*

## 8. Has the corporation lobbied for pseudo-ecological theories?

**Answer: YES**. ConocoPhillips consistently lobbies against tightening climate regulation and supports organisations questioning the scientific consensus on climate change.

1. **Funding organisations opposing climate regulation (2000s–2010s)** — ConocoPhillips was a member of the American Petroleum Institute (API) and contributed to ALEC. Both organisations for decades funded campaigns against the adoption of greenhouse gas limiting legislation in the USA. InfluenceMap classified ConocoPhillips as one of the companies with the highest level of negative climate lobbying.
   *Source: InfluenceMap, ConocoPhillips profile, 2022; API lobbying disclosures; ALEC membership records*

2. **Lobbying against EPA methane regulation (2017–2019)** — During the Trump presidency, ConocoPhillips actively supported the repeal of the EPA rule limiting methane emissions from oil and gas production. The company submitted comments to the EPA arguing against the standards, characterising them as "excessive regulation".
   *Source: EPA public comment records, 2018; EDF methane policy tracker; Inside Climate News*

3. **"Educational" materials about the "reliability" of fossil fuels for schools (2010s)** — ConocoPhillips funded educational resources through its partnership with API Education, which distributed materials about the "indispensability" of oil and gas in American schools without mentioning climate risks. Educational organisations (National Science Teaching Association) criticised such corporate teaching materials.
   *Source: API Education program; Media Matters for America analysis, 2019; NSTA policy statement*
