Eni in Nigeria: Mangroves in Oil

Eni (through subsidiary Agip Nigeria) has operated in the Niger Delta since 1962. Human rights defenders have documented hundreds of oil spills from Eni's infrastructure and allegations of underreporting their volumes.

In 2019, a Dutch court accepted a lawsuit by Nigerian fishermen against Eni's Dutch subsidiary, accusing the company of inadequate pipeline maintenance. In 2021, the court ordered Eni to pay compensation to four Nigerian farmers for damage from an oil spill — a landmark ruling.

In 2019, Eni signed a gas exploration agreement in Mozambique (Cabo Delgado) — a region with an active armed conflict. Human rights organisations argue that extraction intensified the social tensions that led to one of the largest humanitarian crises in Africa. Coastal mangrove areas were affected by LNG facility construction.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. Eni's oil spills in Nigeria destroyed drinking water sources and fishing grounds for hundreds of thousands of people; the company's activities in Mozambique are linked to the intensification of armed conflict.

1. **Nigeria, Goi and Oruma — Agip/Eni spills (2010, 2012)** — Large-scale spills occurred on Agip (Eni) pipelines in the Goi and Oruma communities (Niger Delta). Rights defenders Milieudefensie and Amnesty International documented well contamination, fish deaths and cessation of agriculture. Local residents reported increased skin and gastrointestinal diseases. A Dutch court found Eni responsible in 2021.
   *Source: The Hague District Court ruling, Milieudefensie vs. Eni, 2021; Amnesty International "Claiming Rights", 2015*

2. **Mozambique — armed conflict and forced displacement (2019–2023)** — Eni is developing the Area 4 gas block in Mozambique jointly with ExxonMobil. The largest humanitarian crisis in the region — attacks by armed groups — forced more than 950,000 people to flee their homes. Chatham House analysts link the conflict to social stratification exacerbated by oil and gas projects.
   *Source: UNHCR Mozambique displacement data; Chatham House, "Mozambique gas and conflict", 2021; Human Rights Watch 2021*

3. **Nigeria, Bille spill (2010)** — An oil spill near Bille (Rivers State) on Agip infrastructure led to the destruction of mangrove ecosystems on which several thousand fishermen were entirely dependent. Communities lost their primary source of income and food. Clean-up proceeded extremely slowly; some contamination persisted years later.
   *Source: Amnesty International, "Nigeria: Oil Companies Must Come Clean", 2016; NOSDRA spill database; Friends of the Earth Nigeria*

## 2. Has the corporation been the cause of major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. Eni is linked to hundreds of oil spills in the Niger Delta, underreporting of their volumes, destruction of mangrove ecosystems, and environmental damage from LNG facility construction in Mozambique.

1. **Niger Delta — systematic Agip/Eni spills (1962–present)** — According to NOSDRA (Nigeria's regulator), between several dozen and more than one hundred spills occurred annually at Eni/Agip infrastructure. Amnesty International established that the company systematically underreported spill volumes and shifted blame onto "sabotage", although many ruptures occurred due to pipeline corrosion.
   *Source: NOSDRA spill database 2011–2019; Amnesty International, "A Licence to Spill", 2018*

2. **Mangrove forests of the Bight of Benin (Niger Delta)** — A study by Amnesty International and CEHRD (the Nigerian Centre for Environmental and Human Rights Defence) documented the destruction of extensive mangrove forest areas as a result of Eni/Agip spills. Mangroves are crucial spawning grounds and fish nurseries, as well as protection against coastal erosion.
   *Source: Amnesty International / CEHRD, "The True Tragedy", 2011; UNEP Niger Delta assessment*

3. **Construction of Coral Sul FLNG facilities in Mozambique (2018–2024)** — Construction of the Coral Sul floating LNG platform in Comoro Bay was accompanied by dredging that damaged coral reefs. Environmental assessments by WWF and IUCN pointed to inadequate marine biodiversity protection measures.
   *Source: WWF Mozambique, marine impact assessment, 2020; IUCN statement on Coral Sul FLNG*

## 3. Has the corporation engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. Eni systematically presents natural gas as an "environmentally clean solution" and declares climate targets incompatible with its actual production plans.

1. **Promotion of gas as "green fuel" in EU advertising (2019–2022)** — Eni launched a large-scale European advertising campaign positioning natural gas as a "clean" alternative to coal and "a bridge to renewable energy". In 2023, Italian regulator AGCM fined Eni for misleading environmental advertising of HVOlution biofuel, finding "emission neutrality" claims unsubstantiated.
   *Source: AGCM ruling, February 2023; Le Monde, Corporate Europe Observatory*

2. **"HVOlution" greenwashing lawsuit — biodiesel (2023)** — Eni's HVOlution biodiesel was advertised as a "CO₂-neutral" product. The Italian Antitrust Authority AGCM investigated and found that "zero emission" claims did not account for the full production lifecycle and the raw material (palm oil) linked to deforestation.
   *Source: AGCM ruling No. 30, February 2023; Greenpeace Italy; Reuters*

3. **"Net zero by 2050" declarations while planning production growth (2021–2023)** — Eni declares carbon neutrality by 2050, but the company's strategic plan for 2022–2025 envisages growth in oil and gas production. Carbon Tracker noted a fundamental contradiction between climate declarations and production plans.
   *Source: Carbon Tracker, "Absolute Impact", 2022; Eni Strategic Plan 2022–2025*

## 4. Does the corporation engage in ESG marketing?

**Answer: YES**. Eni positions itself as an ESG leader in Italian business, actively using ESG rhetoric in investor relations and public communications.

1. **Eni ESG reporting — integrated reports (2018–2024)** — Eni annually publishes integrated reports combining financial and ESG data. The company claims "leadership in the energy transition", citing partial moves towards renewables and biofuels. However, natural gas forms the core of the portfolio, and renewables account for less than 10% of investment.
   *Source: Eni Integrated Annual Report 2023; Carbon Tracker analysis*

2. **"Versalis" programme — bioplastics as an ESG narrative (2020s)** — The Versalis subsidiary (Eni) actively promotes bioplastics and the "bioeconomy" in the group's ESG communications. Environmentalists note that Versalis continues to produce significant volumes of conventional plastics, while the bioeconomy accounts for only a small share of revenues.
   *Source: Eni Versalis sustainability reports; Plastic Europe data*

3. **Indexing management bonuses to ESG metrics (2021)** — Eni linked part of senior management remuneration to achieving ESG metrics (methane emissions, renewable capacity). Critics noted that the chosen metrics do not cover Scope 3 and allow "targets" to be met without real change to the company's climate profile.
   *Source: Eni Remuneration Report 2022; Reclaim Finance analysis*

## 5. Has the corporation won awards in global ESG competitions?

**Answer: YES**. Eni received ESG recognition from international rating agencies and industry organisations.

1. **Inclusion in Euronext Vigeo Eiris "Europe 120" (2019–2022)** — Eni was included in the Vigeo Eiris ESG index among European corporations, mentioned in annual reports as confirmation of sustainability. Critics noted that the methodology did not reflect documented violations in Nigeria.
   *Source: Vigeo Eiris index reports; Eni Annual Report 2021*

2. **MSCI ESG rating "BBB" (2021–2023)** — Eni received a mid-range MSCI ESG rating allowing inclusion in certain "responsible" investment products. In parallel, court proceedings continued in the Nigerian bribery case (OPL 245) and fishermen's lawsuits.
   *Source: MSCI ESG Ratings; Global Witness OPL 245 case tracker*

3. **S&P Global industry award — "Sustainability Yearbook" (2022)** — Eni was included in the S&P Global Sustainability Yearbook as one of the oil and gas sector leaders in ESG transparency. NGOs FERN and ReCommon criticised the inclusion in the context of the Nigerian corruption case.
   *Source: S&P Global Sustainability Yearbook 2022; ReCommon press release, 2022*

## 6. Has the corporation placed eco-labels on products with negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. Eni labelled biofuels and petroleum products with environmental symbols misleading consumers.

1. **HVOlution biodiesel — "CO₂ neutral" (2022–2023)** — At Eni filling stations, HVOlution fuel (hydrotreated vegetable oil) was labelled as "net zero" and "carbon neutral", and adorned with green symbols. Italian antitrust authority AGCM found this labelling misleading and fined Eni.
   *Source: AGCM ruling, February 2023; Eni HVOlution product labeling; Reuters*

2. **Eni Diesel+ fuel with "EcoSolution" symbol** — The Eni Diesel+ motor fuel range is sold with an "EcoSolution" label and green leaves declaring reduced harmful emissions. Independent verification by Transport & Environment showed that the difference in emissions compared to standard diesel is negligible.
   *Source: Transport & Environment, EU fuel analysis; Eni Diesel+ packaging*

3. **Agip Bike "green" lubricants range** — Agip (Eni) launched a motorcycle oil range emphasising the "ecological benefits" of biodegradable components. The stated proportion of biodegradable substances constituted only a small fraction of the composition, while the remaining components were standard petroleum products.
   *Source: Agip Bike product documentation; Italian Consumer Association (Altroconsumo) analysis*

## 7. Has the corporation organised eco-events negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. Eni implements small environmental programmes that create a positive PR image against the backdrop of large-scale damage.

1. **"Jazz in the Garden" programme and office greening in Rome (annually)** — Eni's Rome headquarters sponsors cultural-environmental events with tree and flower planting on the complex grounds. Environmental activists noted that such events are used in company press materials as a symbol of "ecological culture".
   *Source: Eni corporate social responsibility reports; ReCommon Italy critique*

2. **"Young Ecologists Club" programme in Nigeria (2015–2020)** — Agip/Eni funded school environmental clubs in Delta State (Nigeria). The programme reached several thousand children and included nature conservation lessons. Local activists described the programme as a PR response to growing pressure over oil spills, noting the absence of real clean-up measures for contaminated areas.
   *Source: Eni Nigeria community reports; Environmental Rights Action Nigeria (ERA/FoEN) critique*

3. **Ravenna coastline clean-up (Italy, 2018)** — Eni organised an Adriatic Sea coastline clean-up near Ravenna with the participation of company employees and local residents. The event coincided with the Italian Parliament's consideration of legislation to tighten standards for offshore oil platforms.
   *Source: Eni press release, June 2018; Il Fatto Quotidiano*

## 8. Has the corporation lobbied for pseudo-ecological theories?

**Answer: YES**. Eni systematically lobbies in favour of natural gas as a "climate solution" and funds "eco-educational" projects promoting the oil and gas industry.

1. **OPL 245 corruption case — Nigeria (2011–2021)** — In 2019–2021, a Milan trial took place involving Eni and Shell on charges of an alleged $1.1 billion bribe to Nigerian officials for oil block OPL 245. Eni and its senior managers were acquitted by the Italian court, but Global Witness and Amnesty International contested several key defence arguments. Investigations continue in the Netherlands and the UK.
   *Source: Global Witness, "The Eni/Shell trial", 2021; Amnesty International; Financial Times*

2. **Lobbying for gas inclusion in the EU "green" taxonomy (2020–2022)** — Eni actively lobbied for recognition of natural gas as a "transition" fuel within the European sustainable investment taxonomy. The company submitted materials to the European Commission arguing that gas is necessary for the energy transition, thereby securing access to "green" financing.
   *Source: Corporate Europe Observatory, gas lobbying, 2021; InfluenceMap EU taxonomy tracker*

3. **"Environmental education" through Eni Magazine corporate publication** — Eni publishes a quarterly magazine and online platform with content about "sustainable energy", "African development" and "the role of gas in fighting poverty". Independent analysts characterised the publication as an instrument for forming a favourable narrative about the oil and gas industry.
   *Source: Eni Magazine archive; DeSmog analysis of oil company media, 2022*
