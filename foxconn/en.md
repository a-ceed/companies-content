Foxconn: Electronics at the Cost of Rivers

Foxconn — the world's largest contract electronics assembler — manufactures iPhones, PlayStations and numerous other devices. At plants in China's Guangdong Province, Greenpeace China repeatedly found discharges of industrial effluent contaminated with heavy metals: mercury, lead, cadmium.

In 2011, explosions of aluminium dust used in polishing iPad cases occurred at Foxconn plants in Chengdu and Shantou: 4 people died and 77 were injured. Working conditions and worker health at Foxconn enterprises are systematically criticised by SACOM (Students & Scholars Against Corporate Misbehaviour).

In Wisconsin (USA), Foxconn received $4.5 billion in subsidies to build a factory, promising to create 13,000 jobs. Wetlands within the Great Lakes watershed were drained for construction; in the end, the plant occupied a minuscule fraction of the promised area.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. Working conditions at Foxconn plants led to mass suicides among workers, explosions and poisonings, as well as systemic exploitation of child and overtime labour.

1. **Wave of suicides at the Shenzhen factory (2010)** — In 2010, 14 workers committed suicide at the Longhua factory (Shenzhen), with several more suffering serious injuries. Journalist and NGO investigations exposed working conditions: 12-hour shifts, bans on talking during work, barracks-style housing. In response, Foxconn installed "safety nets" under buildings rather than improving conditions.
   *Source: Students & Scholars Against Corporate Misbehaviour (SACOM), 2010; New York Times, 2010*

2. **Dust explosions at plants in Chengdu and Shantou (2011)** — Aluminium dust explosions occurred at plants polishing iPad cases: 4 people died and 77 sustained burns. Apple's investigation and independent inspections confirmed safety violations — excessive concentrations of metallic dust in the air of working spaces.
   *Source: Apple Supplier Responsibility Report, 2012; Bloomberg, 2011*

3. **Child and overtime labour at Chinese plants (2010s)** — SACOM and China Labour Watch documented the use of student "interns" aged 16–17 on assembly lines with mandatory night shifts. Students were coerced under threat of expulsion into "voluntary" factory placements. PRC legislation prohibits this type of "internship".
   *Source: China Labour Watch, 2012; SACOM, 2011*

## 2. Has the corporation been the cause of major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. Foxconn plants systematically contaminated rivers and soil with heavy metals and solvents.

1. **River contamination near Guangdong Province plants (2010s)** — Greenpeace China, during inspections in 2011–2013, found discharges of industrial effluent from Foxconn supplier plants — containing lead, mercury, cadmium and chlorinated organic compounds — into Guangdong Province rivers. Water samples showed multiple exceedances of Chinese standards.
   *Source: Greenpeace China, "Toxic Threads", 2013; Greenpeace, "Poisoning the Pearl", 2011*

2. **Draining of Wisconsin wetlands (2017–2019)** — To build the factory in Mount Pleasant, Foxconn drained wetlands within the Great Lakes watershed. The project received a special "environmental exemption" from the state administration; critics pointed to violations of the federal Clean Water Act.
   *Source: ProPublica Wisconsin, 2019; Wisconsin Department of Natural Resources records*

3. **Soil contamination at the Wisconsin construction site** — Environmental inspections of the plant site found unauthorised earthwork without necessary permits, disrupting water flow regimes. State inspectors issued stop-work orders on several occasions.
   *Source: Wisconsin DNR inspection reports, 2019; Milwaukee Journal Sentinel*

## 3. Has the corporation engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. Foxconn publicly declared environmental commitments while simultaneously continuing to contaminate rivers and destroy wetlands.

1. **"Green" statements during Wisconsin plant construction** — Foxconn advertised the future Wisconsin factory as a "smart" and "green" production facility, while the project's implementation was accompanied by wetland draining and water protection standard violations.
   *Source: Foxconn press releases, 2017–2018; Environmental Law & Policy Center*

2. **Corporate reports about "reducing environmental impact"** — Foxconn Technology Group annually published reports on reduced water consumption and emissions at its production facilities, without including data on contamination documented by Greenpeace China and NGOs.
   *Source: Foxconn ESG Reports, 2015–2020*

3. **"Net zero by 2050" pledges without interim plans** — In 2021, Foxconn announced a carbon neutrality target for 2050 and "30% renewables in the energy mix by 2030", without detailed disclosure of plans and investments. Independent analysts characterised the statement as unverifiable.
   *Source: Foxconn ESG Report 2021; CDP Non-Disclosure Campaign*

---

## 4. Does the corporation engage in ESG marketing?

**Answer: YES**. Foxconn actively uses ESG narratives to attract large corporate clients and Western investors.

1. **Apple's ESG requirements and Foxconn's public responses** — Foxconn regularly participates in Apple's labour and environmental audits, publicly reporting "improvements". Independent CLW and SACOM inspections documented a gap between declared and actual conditions.
   *Source: Apple Supplier Responsibility Reports; China Labour Watch annual reports*

2. **Hon Hai inclusion in Taiwanese ESG indices** — The parent company Hon Hai Precision is included in Taiwanese corporate governance and sustainability indices, using this to attract international institutional investors with ESG mandates.
   *Source: Taiwan Corporate Governance Center; MSCI ESG Ratings*

3. **Public "responsible supply chain" commitments** — Foxconn signed a number of international declarations on responsible supply chains, including RBA (Responsible Business Alliance) commitments, despite systematic labour standard violations documented by auditors.
   *Source: Responsible Business Alliance membership; SACOM audit reports, 2018–2022*

---

## 5. Has the corporation won awards in global ESG competitions?

**Answer: YES**. Foxconn received industry and regional sustainability recognition.

1. **Inclusion in the CDP Supply Chain Program** — Foxconn, as a key Apple supplier, participates in the CDP programme for disclosing environmental impact data, which certain rating agencies interpreted as an indicator of "transparency" — despite serious violations at production facilities.
   *Source: CDP Supply Chain Program participants list*

2. **Taiwanese corporate CSR awards (2010s)** — Hon Hai Precision received Taiwanese awards in the "governance" and "sustainability" categories from local business publications, despite international scandals related to labour conditions and environmental violations.
   *Source: CommonWealth Magazine Taiwan CSR Awards*

---

## 6. Has the corporation placed eco-labels on products with negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. Foxconn positioned its production facilities and products as "environmentally advanced" without real compliance with the declared standards.

1. **"Green factory" labelling in China** — A number of Foxconn production facilities received "green factory" status under China's state certification programme, despite Greenpeace-documented violations of discharge standards and contamination of adjacent water bodies.
   *Source: China Ministry of Industry and Information Technology green factory list; Greenpeace China, 2013*

2. **"Responsible" electronics manufacturing as a marketing claim** — Foxconn actively used the "responsible manufacturing" claim in presentations for corporate clients (Apple, Sony, Microsoft), creating an image of a reliable, environmentally conscious partner while systemic violations continued.
   *Source: Foxconn corporate presentations; RBA audit reports*

3. **"Environmental" energy efficiency certificates for individual workshops** — Individual Foxconn production buildings received energy efficiency certificates (ISO 14001), which the company used in marketing as proof of general environmental responsibility.
   *Source: Foxconn ESG Report, 2019*

---

## 7. Has the corporation organised eco-events negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. Foxconn conducted symbolic environmental events incomparable with industrial pollution and working conditions.

1. **Greening of factory grounds in China** — Foxconn invested in greening the internal infrastructure of its largest factory towns: parks, flower beds, artificial ponds. In corporate reports, this was positioned as "improving the ecology" of the production environment.
   *Source: Foxconn CSR reports, 2014–2018*

2. **Environmental lectures for workers as part of "corporate training"** — Foxconn included "environmental education" in mandatory onboarding programmes for new employees — while workers had no mechanisms for reporting actual environmental violations in production.
   *Source: Foxconn employee onboarding materials; CLW worker testimonies*

3. **"Green Volunteers" programme at Taiwanese facilities** — A volunteering programme for cleaning areas near Taiwanese plants, annually covering several hundred employees, was presented in annual reports as evidence of "corporate environmental responsibility".
   *Source: Foxconn ESG Report Taiwan, 2018*

---

## 8. Has the corporation lobbied for pseudo-ecological theories?

**Answer: YES**. Foxconn used the rhetoric of "smart manufacturing" and "green industrialisation" to obtain government subsidies and weaken environmental requirements.

1. **Lobbying for environmental exemptions in Wisconsin (2017–2018)** — To implement the Wisconsin factory project, Foxconn, with the support of Governor Walker's administration, secured a special legislative exemption from the state Clean Water Act's wetland requirements, presenting this as a necessary condition for "green technologies of the future".
   *Source: Wisconsin Act 58, 2017; ProPublica, 2019*

2. **Promoting the "smart" and "ecological" factory narrative as a pretext for subsidies** — Foxconn positioned the Wisconsin project as the "eighth wonder of the world" — a model of future production with "artificial intelligence and zero emissions", which secured $4.5 billion in subsidies without adequate environmental assessment.
   *Source: The Guardian, 2019; Wisconsin Legislative Audit Bureau*

3. **Promoting "responsible electronics" through RBA without real improvement** — Foxconn actively participated in public RBA (Responsible Business Alliance) forums, presenting itself as a leader in "responsible" electronics manufacturing, while independent audits continued to document violations of labour and environmental standards.
   *Source: RBA Annual Progress Report; China Labour Watch, 2019–2022*

## Summary of Answers

1. **Mass harm to human lives?** — **YES** — 14 suicides at Longhua factory in 2010; 4 killed and 77 injured in Chengdu and Shantou explosions (2011); systematic child and forced overtime labour.

2. **Major environmental disasters?** — **YES** — Guangdong river contamination with lead, mercury and cadmium; draining of Great Lakes watershed wetlands in Wisconsin; water protection standard violations during plant construction.

3. **Greenwashing?** — **YES** — "Green" factory advertising while draining wetlands; ESG reports without Greenpeace-documented violations; carbon neutrality pledges without verifiable plans.

4. **ESG marketing?** — **YES** — Apple audits as a PR instrument; Hon Hai inclusion in Taiwanese ESG indices; RBA participation as a responsibility showcase while violations continued.

5. **Wins in ESG competitions?** — **YES** — CDP Supply Chain Program participation; Taiwanese corporate CSR awards.

6. **Eco-labels on harmful products?** — **YES** — "Green factory" status in China despite documented violations; ISO 14001 certificates as a general marketing argument.

7. **Negligible eco-events?** — **YES** — Factory courtyard greening; mandatory eco-lectures for workers; volunteer clean-ups — incomparable with the scale of industrial pollution.

8. **Lobbying for pseudo-ecological theories?** — **YES** — Lobbying for exemptions from Wisconsin water legislation; promoting the "smart green factory" narrative to obtain subsidies; RBA participation as an instrument for neutralising criticism.
