The world's largest open-pit copper-gold mine dumps billions of tonnes of waste into Indonesian rivers.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. Destruction of Kamoro fishing livelihoods, military operations involving Freeport security forces, and documented human rights violations.

1. **Destruction of Kamoro fishing and food sources** — 230,000 tonnes of tailings dumped daily into the Ajkwa river have destroyed the fishing of the Kamoro people — their main source of protein and employment. Chronic hunger and child mortality rates in downstream villages have been documented by NGOs and the UN.
   *Source: HRW; UN Special Rapporteur report on Papua*

2. **Funding of military operations and civilian deaths** — Freeport paid hundreds of millions of dollars to the Indonesian army and police for "security" at the mine. Human Rights Watch documented killings, torture, and arbitrary detention of indigenous Papuans by Indonesian security forces operating within the Grasberg security zone.
   *Source: HRW — "Paying for Protection: The Freeport Mine and the Indonesian Security Forces" (2006)*

3. **Copper contamination of drinking water** — Copper concentrations in the Ajkwa river exceeded drinking water standards by thousands of times. Children in villages using river water were exposed to heavy metal toxicity.
   *Source: EarthWorks — Grasberg mine pollution assessment*


## 2. Has the corporation been responsible for major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. Grasberg is one of the world's largest sources of river contamination from mining waste.

1. **230,000 tonnes of tailings per day into the Ajkwa river** — The mine dumps 230,000 tonnes of tailings daily directly into the Ajkwa-Ajkwa river system. According to EarthWorks, this is equivalent to covering one third of New York's Central Park with a 30 cm layer every day. Over 900 million tonnes of mining waste have accumulated to date.
   *Source: EarthWorks — Grasberg Tailings Assessment*

2. **Destruction of coastal ecosystems in Omawka Bay** — Tailings have filled the Ajkwa delta and extended several kilometres into the Arafura Sea. Marine ecosystems, mangroves, and coral reefs in Omawka Bay have been destroyed by metal-laden silt.
   *Source: MiningWatch Canada; EarthWorks*

3. **Destruction of the Puncak Jaya glacier** — Open-pit mining on the summit of Puncak Jaya has destroyed a significant portion of Southeast Asia's only tropical glacier. The rate of glacial retreat in the mining zone is anomalously high.
   *Source: Nature — Tropical Glacier Studies*


## 3. Has the company engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. Freeport declares "responsible mining" while continuing to discharge tailings into rivers.

1. **"Responsible Mining" narrative alongside riverine tailings disposal** — Freeport uses the narrative of "responsible mining" and "stewardship" in corporate reports, without mentioning that the mine discharges tailings into a river — a practice banned in all developed countries.
   *Source: Freeport Annual Reports vs EarthWorks analysis*

2. **ICMM membership without compliance with standards** — Freeport is a member of the International Council on Mining & Metals (ICMM), an organisation that declares responsible mining standards. Critics point out that Grasberg's riverine tailings disposal is incompatible with these standards.
   *Source: MiningWatch Canada — ICMM analysis*


## 4. Does the company participate in ESG marketing?

**Answer: YES**. Freeport-McMoRan publishes ESG reports and positions copper as a "green metal".

1. **ESG Reports and "Copper is Green" narrative** — The company publishes annual Sustainability Reports, positioning copper as a critical metal for electrification and the "green" transition.
   *Source: Freeport-McMoRan Sustainability Report 2023*

2. **"Responsible Copper" for EU markets** — Freeport promotes the concept of "responsible copper" for European buyers of EV components, without disclosing that some copper is mined at Grasberg using riverine tailings disposal.
   *Source: Freeport marketing materials*


## 5. Has the corporation won any global ESG competition nominations?

**Answer: NO**. Freeport is not included in key ESG indices due to environmental and human rights violations.

1. Freeport has been excluded from MSCI ESG Leaders and has a low Sustainalytics rating. EarthWorks and MiningWatch annually place the company on lists of the most irresponsible mining companies.
   *Source: MSCI; Sustainalytics; EarthWorks*


## 6. Has the company applied eco-labels to products with negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. Freeport copper enters "green" products bearing sustainability certificates.

1. **"Certified Copper" in Tesla vehicles and other EVs** — Freeport copper is used in electric vehicles marketed as "environmentally friendly", while its extraction via riverine tailings disposal is one of the most destructive practices in the mining industry.
   *Source: EarthWorks; supply chain analysis*


## 7. Has the company organised eco-events negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. Tailings "management" programmes and river bio-monitoring are negligible compared to the scale of pollution.

1. **"Tailings Management Programme"** — Freeport declares a programme to monitor metal concentrations in the Ajkwa river. Critics describe this as "monitoring the disaster instead of preventing it", since tailings discharge continues.
   *Source: EarthWorks analysis*

2. **Sponsorship of ecological research** — The company funds scientific research on New Guinea ecosystems, which critics view as an attempt to legitimise the mine's presence in a uniquely biodiverse region.
   *Source: MiningWatch Canada*


## 8. Has the company lobbied for pseudo-ecological theories or greenwashing outreach?

**Answer: YES**. Freeport lobbies the narrative of "green" copper as an indispensable component of decarbonisation.

1. **Lobbying against tightening tailings discharge standards in Indonesia** — Freeport obtained an exemption from the Indonesian government allowing Grasberg to continue riverine tailings disposal despite a general ban on the practice, arguing that alternative technologies are "unviable" for this mine.
   *Source: Global Witness; MiningWatch Canada*

2. **"Copper = green transition" in the US Congress** — Freeport actively lobbies in the US Congress, promoting the narrative that copper production is critically necessary for US climate goals, pushing for accelerated licensing of new mines in Arizona.
   *Source: Federal lobbying disclosures; Freeport Government Affairs*
