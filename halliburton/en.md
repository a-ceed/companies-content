Hydraulic fracturing (fracking) contaminates groundwater and triggers seismic activity.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. Drinking water contamination, induced seismicity and occupational diseases among fracking workers are all documented.

1. **Contamination of drinking water with methane and fracking chemicals** — The EPA acknowledged in 2016 that fracking can contaminate drinking water "under certain circumstances". Hundreds of households in Pennsylvania, West Virginia and Texas near wells serviced by Halliburton documented contamination of their wells with methane and chemicals. Residents received drinking water delivered by tanker trucks.
   *Source: EPA — Hydraulic Fracturing and Drinking Water Resources (2016)*

2. **Harvard study: cancer mortality in fracking areas** — A study by the Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health (2017–2018) found a statistically significant increase in mortality from cardiovascular and respiratory diseases in Pennsylvania counties with a high density of fracking wells.
   *Source: Environmental Health Perspectives — Harvard study on fracking mortality*

3. **The "Halliburton Loophole" — regulatory exemption (2005)** — The Energy Policy Act of 2005, lobbied by Halliburton (connected to Vice President Cheney), excluded fracking from the Safe Drinking Water Act. This allowed companies to withhold the composition of fracking fluids, depriving communities of the right to information about risks.
   *Source: U.S. Energy Policy Act 2005; Congressional records*


## 2. Has the corporation been responsible for major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. The Deepwater Horizon oil-waste spill, onshore well accidents and seismic events are all documented catastrophes.

1. **Deepwater Horizon (2010): Halliburton's role in the largest oil disaster** — The investigation (Presidential Commission, 2011) found that substandard cementing of the wellhead, performed by Halliburton, was one of the key causes of the explosion that killed 11 people and released 4.9 million barrels of oil into the Gulf of Mexico. In 2013 Halliburton admitted destroying evidence and paid a $1.1 billion settlement.
   *Source: National Commission on the BP Deepwater Horizon Oil Spill*

2. **Induced earthquakes in Oklahoma** — Injection of fracking wastewater into deep formations triggered a wave of earthquakes in Oklahoma (600+ in 2015 with magnitude 3+). The 5.8-magnitude earthquake near Prague (2016) damaged dozens of buildings. Halliburton is the largest wastewater injection operator in the region.
   *Source: USGS — Induced Earthquake Science; Oklahoma Geological Survey*

3. **Fracking fluid spill in Pennsylvania (2011)** — At a Well Services Corp site (Halliburton contractor), 10,000 gallons of fracking fluid were released into a stream near Canton, Pennsylvania. The PA DEP issued an emergency order.
   *Source: Pennsylvania DEP records*


## 3. Has the company engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. Halliburton promotes fracking as a "clean" gas extraction technology.

1. **"Natural Gas as Clean Energy Solution" — corporate narrative** — Halliburton actively promotes natural gas as a "bridge to clean energy", positioning fracking as a climate-responsible technology. Research shows, however, that methane leaks from fracking partially negate the climate advantages of gas over coal.
   *Source: Science — Methane leakage from U.S. natural gas systems*

2. **"Water Recycling" marketing alongside documented aquifer contamination** — Halliburton advertises its water-recycling technologies for fracking as "environmentally responsible", while groundwater contamination continues to be recorded in areas where the company operates.
   *Source: EPA Hydraulic Fracturing Study*


## 4. Does the company participate in ESG marketing?

**Answer: YES**. Halliburton publishes ESG reports with a focus on the "energy transition".

1. **Annual ESG Reports** — Halliburton publishes ESG Reports annually, declaring targets to reduce Scope 1 and 2 emissions, cut routine gas flaring and transition to "energy transition" technologies.
   *Source: Halliburton ESG Report 2023*

2. **"Energy Transition" division** — The company created a dedicated division focused on "energy transition" technologies (geothermal energy, CO₂ storage), which is actively promoted in ESG communications.
   *Source: Halliburton Energy Transition*


## 5. Has the corporation won any global ESG competition nominations?

**Answer: NO**. Halliburton has not received significant ESG awards and has low scores from ESG rating agencies.

1. Sustainalytics assigns Halliburton a high ESG risk; the company is not included in the DJSI or FTSE4Good. The Deepwater Horizon settlement has made it a permanent fixture in negative rankings.
   *Source: Sustainalytics; S&P Global*


## 6. Has the company applied eco-labels to products with negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. Halliburton labels fracking chemicals as "environmentally safe".

1. **"CleanStim" — "eco-friendly" fracking fluids** — Halliburton launched the CleanStim line of fracking fluids, marketing them as "safer for the environment" and "made from food ingredients". Independent compositional analyses showed that "food ingredients" do not exclude toxic components.
   *Source: EWG — CleanStim analysis*


## 7. Has the company organised eco-events negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. Minor gas-flaring reduction initiatives are incomparable in scale to the company's operations.

1. **"Zero Routine Flaring" programme** — Halliburton declares participation in the World Bank's Zero Routine Flaring initiative. Actual volumes of associated gas flaring at Halliburton clients' facilities remain enormous.
   *Source: World Bank Zero Routine Flaring Initiative*

2. **Corporate volunteering: waterway clean-ups** — Halliburton employees participate in river and lake clean-ups in Texas and Oklahoma, which are incomparable to the documented aquifer contamination from fracking.
   *Source: Halliburton CSR reports*


## 8. Has the company lobbied for pseudo-ecological theories or greenwashing outreach?

**Answer: YES**. Halliburton is a key lobbyist for the "Halliburton Loophole" and the "natural gas = climate solution" narrative.

1. **The "Halliburton Loophole" (2005)** — Through connections with the Cheney administration, the company secured the exclusion of fracking from the Safe Drinking Water Act, effectively creating a regulatory "desert" for the industry and blocking disclosure of fracking fluid compositions.
   *Source: U.S. Energy Policy Act; Congressional Records*

2. **Lobbying against the EPA Fracking Study** — Halliburton actively lobbied against broadening the EPA's epidemiological studies on the health impacts of fracking, pushing for methodological restrictions.
   *Source: Natural Resources Defense Council advocacy records*

3. **"Natural gas as a bridge to renewables" narrative in Congress** — The company regularly testifies before relevant Congressional committees, promoting the narrative that natural gas is an indispensable "transitional" fuel and thereby obstructing a faster shift to renewable energy sources.
   *Source: Federal lobbying disclosures; Congressional testimony records*
