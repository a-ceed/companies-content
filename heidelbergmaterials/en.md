Cement production is one of the world's largest sources of CO₂ emissions.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. Cement plant dust causes chronic lung diseases in workers and residents of surrounding areas.

1. **Occupational diseases among cement plant workers** — Inhalation of cement dust and clinker leads to silicosis, chronic obstructive pulmonary diseases and lung cancer. NGOs and trade unions have documented cases of occupational illness at HeidelbergMaterials plants (Hanson UK, Italcementi Italy).
   *Source: EU-OSHA reports on cement dust; WHO occupational health data*

2. **Air pollution near plants** — HeidelbergMaterials cement plants in India (Andhra Pradesh) have repeatedly received notices from regulators for exceeding dust emission standards. Residents of surrounding villages have reported deteriorating health.
   *Source: Centre for Science and Environment (India) cement pollution reports*

3. **Blasting operations and harm from noise and vibration** — Limestone quarrying at HeidelbergMaterials sites near residential areas (Germany, UK) generated noise and vibration above permissible limits, negatively affecting quality of life and the health of local populations.
   *Source: BUND Germany; Hanson UK complaints*


## 2. Has the corporation been responsible for major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. Large-scale limestone extraction and cement production create irreversible environmental consequences.

1. **Ewekoro limestone quarry (Nigeria) — forest destruction** — The Ewekoro Quarry in Ogun State (Nigeria) — the largest limestone quarry in West Africa — has destroyed hundreds of hectares of savannah forest and disrupted watercourses on which local communities depend for agriculture.
   *Source: Nigeria Environmental Study/Action Team; local media*

2. **Coral reef pollution (Indonesia)** — HeidelbergMaterials cement plants near the coast of Java discharged suspended matter into coastal waters, which has been associated with deterioration of coral reefs. Local fishermen reported declining catches.
   *Source: Indonesian EPA reports on cement coastal impact*

3. **CO₂ emissions on the scale of small nations** — HeidelbergMaterials produces approximately 120 million tonnes of cement annually. Direct CO₂ emissions (~0.6–0.8 tonnes CO₂ per tonne of cement) amount to 72–96 million tonnes CO₂/year — comparable to the annual emissions of Portugal or Austria.
   *Source: Global Cement; Chatham House cement CO₂ analysis*


## 3. Has the company engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. HeidelbergMaterials promotes "low-carbon cement" while achieving minimal reductions in absolute emissions.

1. **"ECOPact" — "green cement"** — HeidelbergMaterials sells the ECOPact "green" cement line with claimed emission reductions of up to 70% compared to standard cement. Critics note that these products account for a negligible share of sales and do not change the company's total emissions.
   *Source: Transport & Environment cement analysis; HeidelbergMaterials marketing*

2. **CCS projects as future marketing** — The company actively promotes its CO₂ capture project at the Brevik plant (Norway) as a "revolution". This is, however, the company's only CCS project; total carbon capture will represent a negligible fraction of overall emissions.
   *Source: HeidelbergMaterials Brevik CCS Project; Global Cement*


## 4. Does the company participate in ESG marketing?

**Answer: YES**. HeidelbergMaterials actively promotes itself as a leader in "green construction".

1. **ESG Reports and "net zero by 2050"** — The company publishes detailed sustainability reports annually, declaring a goal of achieving "net zero" across Scope 1–3 by 2050.
   *Source: HeidelbergMaterials Sustainability Report 2023*

2. **Brevik CCS: active PR** — The company uses the Brevik CCS project under construction in Norway as its flagship ESG asset, actively positioning it at international climate forums.
   *Source: HeidelbergMaterials communications*


## 5. Has the corporation won any global ESG competition nominations?

**Answer: YES**. HeidelbergMaterials has received ESG recognition in the construction sector.

1. **Inclusion in DJSI** — The company is included in the Dow Jones Sustainability Index Europe as one of the sustainability leaders in the cement industry.
   *Source: S&P Global DJSI*

2. **CDP Climate Score "B"** — HeidelbergMaterials has received CDP scores above the sector average for building materials.
   *Source: CDP Climate Scores*


## 6. Has the company applied eco-labels to products with negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. The ECOPact "green" cement line is labelled as climate-responsible.

1. **ECOPact — "green concrete"** — Cement and concrete under the ECOPact brand are sold to construction companies with emphasis on a "reduced carbon footprint", creating the impression of climate neutrality among clients, even though production still generates significant CO₂ emissions.
   *Source: HeidelbergMaterials ECOPact marketing*

2. **"Low Carbon" labelling alongside growing production volumes** — Despite eco-labelling of individual products, HeidelbergMaterials' overall production volumes are growing and absolute emissions are not declining.
   *Source: HeidelbergMaterials Annual Reports*


## 7. Has the company organised eco-events negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. Quarry rehabilitation programmes and biodiversity conservation initiatives are negligible in scale.

1. **Quarry rehabilitation as nature reserves** — HeidelbergMaterials declares programmes to convert exhausted quarries into nature reserves for birds and insects in Germany and the UK. These programmes cover a few hundred hectares against thousands of hectares of active quarries.
   *Source: HeidelbergMaterials Quarry Rehabilitation Reports*

2. **Collaboration with Nigerian national park rangers** — The company funds a small biodiversity conservation programme in Nigeria, incomparable to the ecological footprint of the Ewekoro Quarry.
   *Source: HeidelbergMaterials Africa CSR reports*


## 8. Has the company lobbied for pseudo-ecological theories or greenwashing outreach?

**Answer: YES**. HeidelbergMaterials lobbies the narrative that CCS is the primary climate solution for cement.

1. **Lobbying for CCS subsidies in the EU** — The company actively lobbies for subsidies for carbon capture technology within the European Innovation Fund and the EU ETS, promoting CCS as the main climate solution for the cement industry and thereby deferring demands for more radical production cuts.
   *Source: EU ETS lobbying records; Cement Innovation Fund applications*

2. **"No cement, no green transition" narrative** — HeidelbergMaterials and the Global Cement and Concrete Association (GCCA) actively promote the narrative that concrete is indispensable for wind turbines, solar farms and electricity grids, creating the impression among regulators that reducing cement production would contradict climate goals.
   *Source: GCCA policy papers; HeidelbergMaterials sustainability communications*
