Holcim: The Cement Giant and ISIS Financing

Holcim (formerly LafargeHolcim) — the world's largest cement producer — was subject to unprecedented criminal liability in the USA and France in 2022: subsidiary Lafarge Syria financed banned organisations (including organisations linked to ISIS) to continue operating a plant in Syria after the civil war began.

From an environmental standpoint, Holcim's cement plants are among the largest industrial sources of PM10 and PM2.5 dust in their regions of operation. In India (Rajasthan State), residents of villages near Ambuja Cements plants report chronic respiratory diseases and water contamination from limestone dust.

In Bangladesh, sand and gravel quarrying from rivers for Holcim is accused of destabilising the banks of the Surma and Meghna rivers, intensifying flooding.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. Holcim/Lafarge plants in various countries have been documented as sources of chronic air pollution causing mass respiratory diseases. Additionally, the Syrian subsidiary financed terrorists, endangering civilians.

1. **Air pollution near Ambuja Cements plants (India, 2010s–present)** — Residents of villages near Ambuja Cements plants in Rajasthan and Gujarat systematically report chronic coughs, bronchial asthma and lung diseases. A study by independent Indian NGOs recorded PM10 levels near the plants 3–5 times above WHO norms.
   *Source: Centre for Science and Environment (CSE), New Delhi, "Cement Industry and Air Pollution" report, 2018*

2. **Financing terrorist groups in Syria (2013–2014)** — Lafarge Syria subsidiary made payments to structures linked to ISIS and Jabhat al-Nusra to ensure unimpeded raw material supply to the Jalabiya plant. According to investigators, payments totalled several million dollars. The company pleaded guilty and paid $778 million in fines in the USA.
   *Source: Reuters, "Lafarge pleads guilty to providing material support to ISIS", 18 October 2022*

3. **Toxic emissions from waste burning in cement kilns (Africa, 2000s–present)** — At Holcim plants in West Africa and South Asia, industrial and hazardous waste — including used tyres, petroleum sludge and chemical waste — is burned as alternative fuel. This generates dioxin and furan emissions exceeding EU standards, which do not apply in those regions.
   *Source: Bankwatch, "Burning Waste in Cement Kilns", report 2019*

## 2. Has the corporation been the cause of major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. Holcim and its subsidiaries' activities have repeatedly led to major environmental incidents — from river contamination in Bangladesh to catastrophic dioxin emissions in Latin America.

1. **Destabilisation of river banks in Bangladesh (2010s)** — River gravel and sand extraction for Holcim's needs in the Surma and Meghna basins led to bank undercutting, destruction of agricultural land and intensification of flooding. Thousands of families were forced to abandon riverside areas.
   *Source: Bangladesh Environmental Lawyers Association (BELA), report on river resource extraction, 2017*

2. **Limestone dust contamination of water bodies in India (ongoing)** — Near Holcim/Ambuja plants in Himachal Pradesh and Rajasthan, contamination of streams and wells with limestone dust was recorded. Water pH rose to 9–10 units, killing freshwater fauna and rendering water unfit for drinking.
   *Source: Toxics Link, New Delhi, "Limestone Dust Pollution near Cement Plants", 2016*

3. **Mercury and heavy metal emissions from waste burning (Latin America)** — At LafargeHolcim plants in Brazil and Mexico, burning hazardous industrial waste led to mercury, lead and cadmium atmospheric emission exceedances. Local NGOs pushed for halting the "co-processing" programme but did not succeed.
   *Source: CEMDA (Centro Mexicano de Derecho Ambiental), report 2020*

## 3. Has the corporation engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. Holcim actively promotes a "green" company image while expanding production with some of the highest specific CO₂ emissions in industry.

1. **ECOPact "green cement" claims (2020–present)** — Holcim actively advertises ECOPact cement as "green" with a reduced carbon footprint. However, independent analysis shows the reduction is only 30–40% from baseline, while the product is positioned as "a net-zero construction solution". The gap between advertising claims and reality was documented in a Carbon Market Watch report.
   *Source: Carbon Market Watch, "Green Claims in Building Materials", 2022*

2. **Participation in climate initiatives while absolute emissions grew** — Holcim is a member of the Science Based Targets initiative (SBTi) and declares reductions in emission intensity per tonne of cement. Nevertheless, the company's absolute emission volume grew between 2019 and 2022 due to new asset acquisitions. The Global Cement and Concrete Association acknowledged that the sector as a whole is not on track for Paris Agreement targets.
   *Source: Global Cement and Concrete Association, Progress Report 2023*

3. **"Build a better future" advertising campaign (2021)** — A major Holcim advertising campaign in European media presented cement production as environmentally neutral thanks to the use of "recycled materials". The proportion of recycled components in production was less than 10%, and the real carbon footprint of one tonne of cement remained above 600 kg CO₂.
   *Source: Influence Map, "Corporate Climate Lobbying", 2022*

## 4. Does the corporation engage in ESG marketing?

**Answer: YES**. Holcim actively uses the ESG narrative to attract investors and improve its public image, despite continuing environmental and social violations.

1. **ESG ratings and sustainability bonds** — In 2021, Holcim issued €850 million in "green bonds", with funds purportedly directed towards emission reduction. Critics from Reclaim Finance noted that a significant portion of the funds went to refinancing existing debt rather than real climate investments.
   *Source: Reclaim Finance, "Green Bond Watchdog", 2021*

2. **Inclusion in ESG indices alongside labour rights violations** — Holcim is in the MSCI ESG Leaders Index, despite documented labour rights violations at plants in Africa and Asia, identified by Swiss NGO Public Eye.
   *Source: Public Eye, "Labour Rights in Cement Supply Chains", 2022*

3. **Carbon neutrality declarations for 2050** — Holcim has publicly committed to achieving carbon neutrality by 2050, but the action plan is criticised as insufficiently specific. InfluenceMap assigned the company a "C–" rating for discrepancy between climate declarations and actual lobbying against strict climate standards.
   *Source: InfluenceMap, Corporate Climate Policy Footprint, 2023*

---

## 5. Has the corporation won awards in global ESG competitions?

**Answer: YES**. Holcim, despite environmental scandals, systematically receives sector ESG awards.

1. **Inclusion in Dow Jones Sustainability Index (DJSI)** — Holcim has repeatedly been included in DJSI World and Europe as a representative of the "sustainable" construction sector. Critics note that DJSI inclusion is based on company self-reporting rather than independent audit.
   *Source: S&P Global, DJSI Yearbook 2022*

2. **CDP "A-List" award for climate transparency (2021)** — The Carbon Disclosure Project assigned Holcim an "A-" rating in the climate information disclosure category. Environmental organisations criticised CDP for insufficient accounting of absolute emission growth when assigning high ratings.
   *Source: CDP Climate Scores, 2021*

3. **Recognition as sustainable construction leader by FTSE4Good (2022–2023)** — Holcim is included in the FTSE4Good index, formed for "ethical investors". Inclusion occurred despite ongoing litigation over the Lafarge Syria case and environmental standard violations in India.
   *Source: FTSE Russell, FTSE4Good Index Series Report, 2022*

## 6. Has the corporation placed eco-labels on products with negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. Holcim labels certain products as environmentally preferable despite their significant environmental impact.

1. **ECOPact labelling as "low carbon"** — ECOPact cement is positioned as "low carbon", although its production still emits more than 400–500 kg CO₂ per tonne. The labelling misleads consumers and architects about the real climate footprint.
   *Source: Carbon Leadership Forum, "Embodied Carbon in Building Materials", 2022*

2. **Eco-labelling of aggregates as "recycled"** — Holcim markets crushed stone and gravel labelled as "recycled material", but this label often covers crushed construction waste with contaminant residues, which does not correspond to strict recycling standards.
   *Source: European Aggregates Association (UEPG), secondary materials report, 2021*

3. **Use of FSC/PEFC certificates on packaging without production certification** — On certain European markets, Holcim product packaging contained references to environmental certification that did not extend to the production process itself. The European consumer protection organisation documented instances of misleading labelling.
   *Source: European Consumer Organisation (BEUC), "Greenwashing in Construction", 2022*

## 7. Has the corporation organised eco-events negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. Holcim organises environmental events that are incomparable in scale with the real harm caused by production.

1. **"Green days" at plants (annually)** — Holcim holds annual "eco-days" at its plants, including employee tree planting and facility clean-ups. The number of trees planted is 5,000–20,000 per year, while a single plant annually emits more than 1 million tonnes of CO₂.
   *Source: Holcim Sustainability Report 2022, official website*

2. **Partnership with WWF on "quarry biodiversity"** — Holcim announced a partnership with WWF for restoring biodiversity in exhausted quarries. The project covers 10 quarries in 5 countries, while the company operates more than 1,400 quarries worldwide.
   *Source: WWF-Holcim Partnership Report, 2020*

3. **"Cement for society" programme** — Holcim implements charitable projects to build schools and roads in deprived areas. The programme's annual budget is approximately $2 million — less than 0.01% of company revenue ($27 billion in 2022).
   *Source: Holcim Foundation for Sustainable Construction, Annual Report 2022*

## 8. Has the corporation lobbied for pseudo-ecological theories?

**Answer: YES**. Holcim actively lobbies to weaken environmental standards for the cement industry, simultaneously funding "educational" projects about "green construction".

1. **Lobbying against strict emission standards for the cement sector in the EU** — Holcim is a member of the European Cement Association (CEMBUREAU), which systematically opposes reducing CO₂ emission quotas under the EU ETS. In 2021–2022, CEMBUREAU lobbied for extension of free emission quotas for the cement sector, against the position of the European Parliament.
   *Source: InfluenceMap, "European Cement Industry Lobbying", 2022*

2. **Promoting the "carbon capture" concept as an alternative to emission reduction** — Holcim actively funds and publicly promotes CCS (carbon capture and storage) technology as a solution to the climate crisis, diverting attention from the need to reduce cement production volumes. Independent scientists note that CCS in the cement sector remains commercially unviable.
   *Source: Global CCS Institute and scientific criticism in Nature Climate Change, 2021*

3. **Funding the "Holcim Foundation for Sustainable Construction" as a PR instrument** — The company funds architectural awards and academic programmes under the sustainable construction brand, forming a positive image in academic circles. Award recipients are often unaware of the sponsoring company's environmental violations.
   *Source: Holcim Foundation, "Awards for Sustainable Construction"; Corporate Europe Observatory analysis, 2021*

1. **Human health harm**: Holcim/Ambuja plants systematically pollute the air with PM10 and PM2.5 in India and Africa, causing mass respiratory diseases; the Syrian subsidiary financed terrorist groups.
2. **Environmental disasters**: Raw material extraction destabilises river banks in Bangladesh; burning hazardous waste in cement kilns causes dioxin contamination in countries with lenient regulation.
3. **Greenwashing**: ECOPact product advertised as "green" while producing more than 400 kg CO₂ per tonne; actual absolute emissions are growing.
4. **ESG marketing**: Company attracts "green investment" through bonds and ESG ratings, using self-reporting without independent audit.
5. **ESG awards**: Holcim is in DJSI, FTSE4Good and receives CDP ratings despite litigation and violations.
6. **False eco-labels**: ECOPact and "recycled" aggregates labelling misleads consumers about the real ecological footprint of products.
7. **Decorative eco-events**: Planting thousands of trees and "quarry biodiversity" are incomparable in scale with millions of tonnes of annual emissions.
8. **Lobbying**: Through CEMBUREAU, the company blocks tightening of EU standards; promotes CCS as an alternative to real emission reduction; uses academic programmes to improve reputation.
