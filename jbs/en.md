JBS and the Destruction of the Amazon

JBS — the world's largest beef processor — is a central link in the economy destroying the Amazon rainforest. According to Greenpeace, cattle farming is responsible for 80% of deforestation in the Amazon. JBS purchased cattle from illegally deforested areas, documented by investigative journalists and NGOs.

In 2009, Greenpeace published the "Slaughterhouse" report, directly linking JBS to forest destruction. Under pressure from major retail chains, JBS signed the "Cattle Agreement" in 2009, prohibiting purchases from farms on recently deforested land. However, subsequent investigations showed systematic violations of these commitments.

In 2017, one of the largest corruption scandals in Brazil implicated JBS: the company admitted to bribing more than 1,800 politicians, including to obtain favourable environmental permit terms. The Batista brothers, JBS founders, paid $3.2 billion in fines.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. JBS systematically violated workers' rights at its slaughterhouses in Brazil, the US and Australia, creating dangerous working conditions with high injury rates, and contributed to the spread of COVID-19 in 2020.

1. **COVID-19 outbreak at JBS plants (2020)** — Mass COVID-19 outbreaks occurred at JBS meat processing facilities in Greeley (Colorado), Nebraska and Pennsylvania in spring 2020. The Greeley plant with more than 6,000 workers became one of the largest infection clusters in the US. Unions accused JBS of forcing sick workers to come in for shifts and concealing information about infections.
   *Source: Associated Press, "JBS plant becomes one of nation's largest COVID-19 clusters", April 2020*

2. **Systematic injuries at Brazilian slaughterhouses (2000s–2010s)** — According to Brazil's Ministry of Labour, JBS enterprises consistently ranked among the country's most dangerous workplaces. Workers suffered injuries from cutting equipment, repetitive stress injuries (carpal tunnel syndrome), and psychological disorders. In 2013, Brazilian prosecutors filed lawsuits against JBS for systematic occupational safety violations.
   *Source: Ministério do Trabalho e Emprego, Brasil, 2013*

3. **Slave labour in the supply chain (2009, 2017)** — Brazilian authorities during "Carne Fraca" Operation (2017) established that several JBS supplier farms used workers under conditions equivalent to slavery: debt bondage, restriction of freedom of movement, absence of safe living conditions. JBS was included in Brazil's "dirty list" of enterprises using forced labour.
   *Source: Ministério do Trabalho, "Lista Suja", 2017; Thomson Reuters Foundation*

## 2. Has the corporation been the cause of major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. JBS is one of the main corporate drivers of Amazon destruction — the planet's largest tropical forest — and also contaminated waterways on multiple continents.

1. **Purchasing cattle from illegally deforested Amazon areas (2009–2022)** — Despite signing the "Cattle Agreement" in 2009, JBS was repeatedly caught purchasing from farms on illegally cleared land. An investigation by Brazilian NGO Imazon (2020) showed that at least 12 direct JBS suppliers violated the moratorium. The activities of JBS suppliers are estimated to have caused the destruction of hundreds of thousands of hectares of forest.
   *Source: Imazon, "Deforestation in Amazon JBS Supply Chain", 2020; Repórter Brasil*

2. **Colorado River contamination and EPA fines (2000s)** — The JBS plant in Greeley, Colorado repeatedly received EPA enforcement notices for discharging contaminated wastewater containing blood, fats and chemicals into the municipal sewerage system above permissible limits. The company paid fines but violations recurred.
   *Source: EPA Enforcement Actions, JBS USA, Colorado, 2008–2015*

3. **"Carne Fraca" Operation and rotten meat scandal (2017)** — Brazil's Federal Police established that JBS and other companies were selling expired and chemically treated meat, including for export. More than 20 countries imposed temporary bans on Brazilian meat imports. The scandal damaged the reputation of the Brazilian meat industry as a whole and affected the global food safety ecosystem.
   *Source: Polícia Federal do Brasil, "Operação Carne Fraca", March 2017*

## 3. Has the corporation engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. JBS actively uses "green" rhetoric and declares ambitious environmental targets, the realisation of which contradicts the company's actual activities and continuing deforestation in its supply chains.

1. **Carbon neutrality pledge by 2040 while deforestation continued (2021)** — In 2021, JBS declared a goal of achieving zero emissions by 2040 and "zero deforestation" in the supply chain by 2035. However, in the same year journalistic investigations showed the company continuing to purchase from farms clearing forests in the Amazon. Critics called these pledges "the largest greenwashing in the meat industry".
   *Source: Global Witness, "JBS: The Destruction Continues", 2022; The Guardian*

2. **"Zero deforestation" only for direct suppliers (2009–2022)** — JBS tracked compliance with zero deforestation requirements only for direct cattle suppliers, ignoring "indirect" second and third-tier suppliers (farms raising calves before transfer to feedlots). According to the Amazon Environmental Research Institute (IPAM), up to 65% of cattle from deforested zones passed through this loophole.
   *Source: IPAM, "Indirect Suppliers and Deforestation", 2021*

3. **Funding "reforestation" projects while destroying forests on a massive scale (2021–2022)** — JBS announced $1 billion investments in environmental programmes, including forest restoration. Meanwhile, the scale of forests annually destroyed through its supply chains remained incomparably larger. Environmentalists characterised these investments as "indulgences" that do not change the company's business model.
   *Source: Amazon Watch, "JBS Climate Pledges Analysis", 2022*

## 4. Does the corporation engage in ESG marketing?

**Answer: YES**. JBS actively publishes ESG reporting and positions itself as a responsible company, while simultaneously being one of the world's largest corporate sources of greenhouse gas emissions and deforestation.

1. **ESG reports with "sustainable beef" declarations (2018–2023)** — JBS annually publishes detailed ESG reports with sections on reduced water use, emission reductions and "responsible land use". Independent rating agencies (Sustainalytics, MSCI) assigned JBS low ESG scores due to high risk in the area of deforestation and supply chain management.
   *Source: Sustainalytics ESG Risk Rating, JBS S.A., 2022*

2. **Attempted NYSE IPO with ESG positioning (2021–2022)** — JBS announced plans for NYSE listing, positioning itself as a company oriented toward ESG principles to attract Western institutional investors. Plans were suspended after intensified pressure from NGOs pointing to the contradiction between ESG statements and real deforestation data.
   *Source: Reuters, "JBS NYSE Listing Plans", 2022; Rainforest Action Network*

3. **Publication of "sustainable livestock science" (2022–2023)** — JBS funded scientific publications and conferences claiming that cattle farming can be "climate neutral" using certain practices. These materials were used in the company's ESG communications, although leading climate scientists disputed the conclusions drawn.
   *Source: Food Climate Research Network, critique of JBS publications, 2022*

## 5. Has the corporation won awards in global ESG competitions?

**Answer: YES**. JBS received certain industry recognition that the company used to create an ESG leader image, despite documented environmental and social violations.

1. **Dow Jones Sustainability Index ratings (2019–2020)** — In certain years, JBS was included in the Dow Jones Sustainability Indices in the Latin American segment, which the company widely mentioned in investor presentations. This inclusion was possible thanks to the index methodology that evaluates company policies and statements rather than real results.
   *Source: JBS Annual Report 2020, ESG section*

2. **LEED certification at individual facilities (2015–2018)** — Several JBS administrative buildings received the "green" LEED certificate. JBS widely publicised this information without clarifying that the certification concerned only offices, not the production facilities carrying the main environmental burden.
   *Source: JBS Sustainability Report, 2018*

3. **Inclusion in CDP list of "climate-transparent companies" (2020)** — CDP included JBS in the list of companies that provided complete greenhouse gas emission data. JBS used the fact of data publication as evidence of "climate responsibility", although the data itself demonstrated one of the highest carbon footprints in the food industry.
   *Source: CDP Climate Disclosure, JBS, 2020*

## 6. Has the corporation placed eco-labels on products with negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. JBS placed "green" labels on meat products whose production is associated with large-scale tropical forest destruction and significant greenhouse gas emissions.

1. **"Verified Beef" labelling with unverified suppliers (2018–2021)** — The Swift brand (US) used labelling indicating verification of beef origin, while audits found cattle arriving from suppliers violating environmental requirements. Independent auditors pointed to incompleteness in the traceability chain.
   *Source: Global Witness, "The Beef Trade", 2021*

2. **"Grass-Fed" brand with inadequate verification (2019–2022)** — A number of JBS/Swift products were positioned as "grass-fed", associated in consumer minds with environmental friendliness. Consumer Reports inspections found discrepancies between labelling and actual animal husbandry conditions at some suppliers.
   *Source: Consumer Reports, "Grass-Fed Beef Label Claims", 2021*

3. **"Rainforest Alliance Certified" labelling on products from deforestation zones (2020–2022)** — Some JBS products carried Rainforest Alliance certification, while parallel investigations established purchases of raw materials from areas where the certification was not in effect or was not properly verified. NGOs pointed to structural weaknesses in this certification scheme as applied to Brazilian cattle farming.
   *Source: Mighty Earth, "Certification and Deforestation", 2021*

## 7. Has the corporation organised eco-events negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. JBS conducted environmental campaigns and announced "green" initiatives whose scale was incomparable with the volume of environmental damage caused.

1. **Forest monitoring programme through satellite imagery (2010s)** — JBS announced the launch of a satellite monitoring system to track suppliers' compliance with the deforestation moratorium. This system, however, covered only direct suppliers and only territory within the Amazon, leaving outside its scope the vast Cerrado biome and "indirect" suppliers.
   *Source: Imazon, "Monitoring Gaps in JBS Supply Chain", 2020*

2. **Corporate "Meat-Free Day" in Brazilian offices (2021)** — JBS announced the introduction of a "Meat-Free Day" on Fridays in its administrative offices as a demonstration of commitment to sustainable consumption. This initiative did not affect the company's production capacities, which process more than 35 million head of cattle per year.
   *Source: JBS Sustainability Report, 2021*

3. **Planting 1,000 trees in the Cerrado (2022)** — JBS organised a public tree-planting event in the Cerrado with local schoolchildren, receiving wide coverage in corporate media. In the same year, auditors recorded the felling of hundreds of thousands of hectares of the biome in JBS supplier operational zones.
   *Source: JBS Corporate Communications, 2022; Mapbiomas Brazil*

## 8. Has the corporation lobbied for pseudo-ecological theories?

**Answer: YES**. JBS financed political parties and individual politicians in Brazil to weaken environmental regulation, and promoted narratives justifying industrial cattle farming.

1. **Bribing officials to obtain environmental permits (2017)** — According to "Lava Jato" case materials and JBS's own admission, the company systematically bribed federal and regional officials in Brazil, including to accelerate the issuance of permits for new slaughterhouse construction and to weaken environmental requirements. The bribe total amounted to hundreds of millions of reals.
   *Source: Ministerio Público Federal, Brasil, "JBS plea agreement", 2017*

2. **Funding agricultural lobbying against strengthening Brazil's Forest Code (2010–2012)** — During revision of Brazil's Forest Code, JBS through industry associations funded campaigns seeking to weaken requirements for riparian buffer zones and reduce the mandatory reserve forest area (Reserva Legal) on agricultural land.
   *Source: Greenpeace Brasil, "The Revision of the Forest Code", 2011*

3. **Promoting the "sustainable beef" narrative through industry associations (2020–2022)** — JBS funded ABIEC (Brazilian Beef Exporters Association) research and publications promoting the thesis that Brazilian cattle farming is "climate neutral" due to the sequestration capacity of pasture ecosystems — an argument rejected by most climate scientists.
   *Source: ABIEC, "Carbon Balance of Brazilian Beef", 2021; criticism — Trase.earth, 2022*

## Summary of Answers

1. **Mass harm to human lives**: COVID-19 outbreak at Greeley plant (2020, thousands infected); systematic injuries at Brazilian slaughterhouses; slave labour in the supply chain ("Carne Fraca" Operation, 2017).

2. **Major environmental disasters**: Purchasing cattle from illegally deforested Amazon areas (hundreds of thousands of hectares); Colorado River contamination and EPA fines; rotten meat scandal ("Carne Fraca" Operation, 2017, import ban in 20 countries).

3. **Greenwashing**: Carbon neutrality declaration by 2040 while deforestation continued; monitoring only direct suppliers as a loophole; "reforestation" investments incomparable with the scale of damage.

4. **ESG marketing**: ESG reports with "sustainable beef" declarations; NYSE IPO attempt with ESG positioning; funding "scientific" publications on climate-neutral cattle farming.

5. **ESG awards**: Inclusion in Dow Jones Sustainability Indices (2019–2020); LEED certificates for administrative buildings; inclusion in CDP list of "climate-transparent companies".

6. **False eco-labels**: "Verified Beef" labelling with unverified suppliers; "Grass-Fed" brand with inadequate verification; "Rainforest Alliance Certified" labelling despite purchases from deforestation zones.

7. **Negligible eco-events**: Satellite monitoring covering only direct suppliers; corporate "Meat-Free Day" in offices; planting 1,000 trees while hundreds of thousands of hectares were felled.

8. **Lobbying for pseudo-ecological theories**: Bribing officials for environmental permits ("Lava Jato" case, 2017); funding agricultural lobbying against strengthening Brazil's Forest Code; promoting "climate-neutral beef" narrative.
