Major plastic packaging producer and consumer of palm oil of questionable origin.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. Food safety violations, plastic packaging with migrating chemicals, and agrochemicals in the supply chain are documented.

1. **Chemicals migrating from packaging into food** — The EWG documented that Kraft Heinz's multi-layer plastic packaging contains phthalates and other endocrine disruptors that can migrate into food. These chemicals are linked to reproductive health damage, especially in children.
   *Source: EWG — Food Packaging Chemicals*

2. **Product recalls due to food safety violations** — In 2019, Kraft Heinz recalled batches of Oscar Mayer meat products in the US due to Listeria contamination. In 2022, a batch of Heinz ketchup was recalled due to sugar content violations (EU). Numerous recalls demonstrate systemic quality control problems.
   *Source: FDA food recall database — Kraft Heinz*

3. **Agrochemicals in soy and palm supply chains** — Kraft Heinz sources soy from Brazil, where intensive use of glyphosate (Roundup) on agricultural land is documented by NGOs. Studies by the Universidad de Córdoba have linked glyphosate exposure to cancer in agricultural regions.
   *Source: RAN — Kraft Heinz soy supply chain*


## 2. Has the corporation been responsible for major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. Plastic pollution and palm oil from deforestation zones are documented problems.

1. **Largest producer of non-recyclable packaging** — Break Free From Plastic regularly places Kraft Heinz in the top five producers of non-recyclable plastic packaging in the food industry. The multi-layer flexible packaging for Oscar Mayer and Velveeta is accepted for recycling almost nowhere.
   *Source: Break Free From Plastic Brand Audit Reports*

2. **Palm oil from deforestation zones** — In 2019, RAN included Kraft Heinz in the list of companies with the most opaque palm oil supply chains. Investigators identified suppliers linked to deforestation in Kalimantan.
   *Source: Rainforest Action Network — Kraft Heinz Palm Oil Scorecard (2019)*

3. **Agricultural runoff from supplier fields** — Tomato cultivation for Heinz in California's San Joaquin Valley with intensive pesticide use creates runoff that contaminates groundwater in one of the most agriculturally polluted regions of the US.
   *Source: California State Water Resources Control Board*


## 3. Has the company engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. Kraft Heinz declares "sustainable packaging" without reducing production of non-recyclable plastic.

1. **"100% sustainable packaging by 2025"** — Kraft Heinz stated the goal of making 100% of its packaging "sustainable" (recyclable, reusable, or compostable) by 2025. According to Break Free From Plastic, progress fell significantly short of stated targets.
   *Source: Break Free From Plastic; Kraft Heinz ESG Report*

2. **"Eco-friendly" ketchup in "green" packaging** — Heinz launched ketchup in sugarcane-based packaging for the UK market as a "sustainable" alternative. NGOs noted that sugarcane plastic has limited recyclability under real-world conditions.
   *Source: The Guardian — Heinz sustainable packaging*


## 4. Does the company participate in ESG marketing?

**Answer: YES**. Kraft Heinz publishes ESG reports as part of its "ESG Champions" strategy.

1. **"Our ESG story" — corporate sustainability strategy** — The company published an updated ESG strategy including targets for reducing emissions, transitioning to "sustainable" packaging, and responsible supply chains.
   *Source: Kraft Heinz ESG Report 2023*

2. **Partnership with WWF** — Kraft Heinz announced a partnership with WWF to assess biodiversity impacts in supply chains.
   *Source: WWF Corporate Partnerships; Kraft Heinz press releases*


## 5. Has the corporation won any global ESG competition nominations?

**Answer: NO**. Kraft Heinz is not included in key ESG ratings and receives average scores from ESG agencies.

1. Kraft Heinz is not included in DJSI or FTSE4Good. MSCI and Sustainalytics ratings assess the company as an "average" ESG risk with specific issues in packaging and supply chains.
   *Source: MSCI ESG; Sustainalytics*


## 6. Has the company applied eco-labels to products with negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. Packaging labelled "recyclable" is in reality not accepted for recycling in most regions.

1. **"Recyclable" labelling on non-recyclable packaging** — A number of Oscar Mayer and other brand packages carry "recyclable" labelling, even though most US recycling systems do not accept these multi-layer packages. The FTC has raised concerns about such claims.
   *Source: FTC Green Guides; Break Free From Plastic*

2. **"Sustainably Sourced" palm oil without verification** — Kraft Heinz claimed to purchase "sustainable" palm oil (RSPO-certified), while RAN identified suppliers linked to deforestation.
   *Source: RAN Kraft Heinz analysis*


## 7. Has the company organised eco-events negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. Kraft Heinz's packaging reduction initiatives are negligible relative to its production volumes.

1. **"Grow" platform — reusable packaging pilot** — The company launched a pilot project for reusable sauce containers in several cities. The programme's reach is incomparable to the billions of single-use packaging units produced annually.
   *Source: Kraft Heinz Sustainability communications*


## 8. Has the company lobbied for pseudo-ecological theories or greenwashing outreach?

**Answer: YES**. Kraft Heinz lobbies against strict packaging disclosure requirements.

1. **Opposition to Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR)** — Kraft Heinz, through industry associations (Consumer Brands Association), lobbied against Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) laws in a number of US states that would require manufacturers to fund the recycling of their packaging.
   *Source: Consumer Brands Association lobbying; state EPR legislative records*

2. **Support for "advanced recycling" instead of plastic reduction** — Kraft Heinz backs the narrative of "innovative recycling" (chemical recycling) as the solution to the plastics problem, while independent research shows negligible effectiveness of chemical recycling at the scale of real-world waste.
   *Source: GAIA — Chemical Recycling analysis*
