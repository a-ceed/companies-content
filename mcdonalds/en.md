McDonald's is one of the largest sources of fast food plastic litter in the world. The corporation serves over 69 million customers daily, generating billions of plastic straws, cups, wrappers and bags. Its packaging is routinely found among the most common items in coastal and urban cleanups.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. McDonald's is systematically criticised for marketing unhealthy food to children, mistreating workers, and for a number of mass food-poisoning incidents.

1. **Mass E. coli food poisoning in the USA (2018–2019)** — McDonald's recalled seasonal salads in 14 states after an outbreak of E. coli O157:H7 that affected more than 500 people. The source was contaminated romaine lettuce from a supplier. The investigation revealed systemic quality-control deficiencies in the fresh-produce supply chain.
   *Source: CDC Outbreak Investigation, July 2018; FDA Statement*

2. **Aggressive marketing of unhealthy food to children (1990–2023)** — Years of research by the Rudd Center and the American Academy of Pediatrics documents that McDonald's deliberately targets advertising at children under 12, using Happy Meal toys, characters, and play areas. By 2023 the "Stop Targeting Kids" campaign had compiled thousands of documented cases.
   *Source: American Academy of Pediatrics, Policy Statement 2011; Rudd Center for Food Policy, 2022*

3. **Labour rights violations and worker injuries (2015–2022)** — The US National Labor Relations Board (NLRB) repeatedly found McDonald's in violation of workers' rights: unlawful dismissals of activists, obstruction of union activities. In 2022 McDonald's paid $26 million to settle a California lawsuit alleging labour-law violations.
   *Source: NLRB Case Records; California Settlement, 2022*

## 2. Has the corporation been the cause of major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. McDonald's bears direct responsibility for large-scale Amazon deforestation through beef and soy purchasing, and is one of the world's largest generators of plastic waste.

1. **Amazon deforestation for beef and soy (2000–2023)** — According to a Mighty Earth study (2018) and a Yale School of Environment report, McDonald's beef suppliers in Brazil are linked to the destruction of Amazon rainforest. JBS, McDonald's largest supplier, has repeatedly been accused of purchasing cattle from ranches located on illegally cleared land.
   *Source: Mighty Earth "Mystery Meat", 2018; Global Forest Watch, Amazon Beef Report 2021*

2. **Plastic packaging — billions of pieces annually (2000–2023)** — According to Break Free From Plastic, McDonald's ranks in the top 3 brands for global plastic pollution in annual waste audits. In the US alone McDonald's generates approximately 700 million pounds of packaging waste per year. The company's packaging is found in more than 120 countries during coastal clean-ups.
   *Source: Break Free From Plastic Brand Audit 2019–2022; As You Sow Report, 2021*

3. **PFAS chemicals in food packaging (2019–2022)** — Research found that McDonald's packaging for french fries and burgers contained PFAS (forever chemicals) that migrate into food. In 2021 McDonald's announced a phased elimination of PFAS, but NGO Toxic-Free Future detected their presence in packaging as late as 2022.
   *Source: Environmental Science & Technology, 2017; Toxic-Free Future Testing Report, 2022*

## 3. Has the company engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. McDonald's regularly announces environmental commitments on packaging, climate, and sustainable supply chains, systematically missing its stated deadlines.

1. **Promise to switch to "sustainable packaging" by 2025 (2018)** — In 2018 McDonald's stated that by 2025 all primary packaging would be made from recycled, certified, or compostable materials. By 2022, according to independent monitoring by As You Sow, more than 60% of packaging still did not meet these criteria.
   *Source: As You Sow Shareholder Resolution Analysis, 2022; McDonald's Global Impact Report*

2. **Advertising "McPlant" as an eco-friendly alternative (2021–2022)** — McDonald's promoted the plant-based McPlant burger as an environmentally responsible choice, but independent analysis showed that at the chain's scale it replaces less than 0.1% of beef sales, leaving the company's real carbon footprint virtually unchanged.
   *Source: The Guardian, "McDonald's McPlant sustainability claims", 2022*

3. **"Green" restaurant redesign in Europe (2009–2015)** — McDonald's repainted some European restaurants from red to green, announcing an "ecological reimagining of the brand". Environmentalists pointed out that changing the colour of the signage has nothing to do with reducing the company's environmental impact.
   *Source: Greenpeace Germany statement, 2010; Der Spiegel, 2009*

## 4. Does the company engage in ESG marketing?

**Answer: YES**. McDonald's actively uses ESG narratives through global sustainability reports, climate commitments, and partnerships with international organisations.

1. **Annual "Global Impact Report" (2017–2023)** — McDonald's publishes multi-page ESG reports with detailed sections on climate, packaging, workers' rights, and communities. The reports are actively distributed to investors and the media as proof of "responsible business".
   *Source: McDonald's Corporation, Global Impact Reports 2018–2023*

2. **Science Based Targets (SBTi) climate goals (2018)** — McDonald's was one of the first restaurant chains to register climate targets with the Science Based Targets initiative. The commitments were widely advertised, yet independent experts noted a lack of transparency in Scope 3 emissions calculations.
   *Source: Science Based Targets initiative; InfluenceMap Corporate Climate Responsibility Monitor, 2021*

3. **Partnership with Rainforest Alliance for coffee purchasing (2019–2022)** — McCafé advertised coffee as "Rainforest Alliance certified", using the label in marketing materials. The actual share of certified coffee in purchasing remained below 50%.
   *Source: Rainforest Alliance Supply Chain Data; Ethical Consumer Magazine, 2021*

---

## 5. Has the corporation won any global ESG awards?

**Answer: YES**. McDonald's has received ESG ratings and awards from sustainability organisations despite the scale of its real environmental and social harm.

1. **Inclusion in the Dow Jones Sustainability Index (DJSI) North America (2017–2021)** — McDonald's was included in the DJSI under the "Restaurants" category, which was actively used in press releases and annual reports as a corporate sustainability indicator.
   *Source: S&P Global DJSI Yearbooks 2018–2021*

2. **CDP Climate rating of "B" (2020–2022)** — CDP assigned McDonald's a "B" rating for climate disclosure. The company widely cited this rating in marketing materials, sidestepping the limited coverage of supply-chain emissions data.
   *Source: CDP Scores 2020–2022*

3. **"Best Sustainability Initiative" award from Nation's Restaurant News (2020)** — McDonald's received an industry sustainability award from the trade publication, even as environmentalists were recording growth in the company's total plastic waste during the same period.
   *Source: Nation's Restaurant News Catalyst Awards, 2020*

## 6. Has the company applied eco-labels to products with negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. McDonald's places environmental labels on packaging and in advertising materials that do not correspond to the products' actual environmental impact.

1. **Rainforest Alliance label on McCafé cups (2019–2022)** — McCafé cups carry the Rainforest Alliance logo, although the cup itself is made from a plastic composite and is not recyclable in most countries. The label creates a false impression of the product's environmental friendliness.
   *Source: Ethical Consumer UK Report, 2021; Tearfund Plastic Report, 2020*

2. **"Sustainable sourcing" label on fish products (2016–2022)** — McDonald's labels fish burgers as made from "sustainably caught fish" (MSC certified), but critics note that the MSC certification has repeatedly been challenged for awarding status to depleted fish stocks.
   *Source: The Guardian "MSC controversies", 2019; Marine Conservation Society Report*

3. **"Eco-packaging" leaflets on the switch to cardboard (2018–2021)** — After partially replacing plastic straws with paper ones, McDonald's launched a global advertising campaign labelling the change a "packaging revolution". Independent research showed that paper straws are often non-recyclable due to food contamination.
   *Source: BBC "Paper straw recycling", 2019; WRAP UK Report, 2020*

---

## 7. Has the company organised eco-events negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. McDonald's regularly organises environmental events whose real scale is incommensurable with the damage caused by the corporation's daily operations.

1. **"McHappy Clean-up" — corporate volunteer days (2015–2022)** — McDonald's organises annual litter-picks around its restaurants as part of the McHappy Day programme. One such event collects a few kilograms of litter, while a single McDonald's restaurant produces hundreds of kilograms of non-recyclable waste every day.
   *Source: McDonald's CSR Reports; Environmental Defense Fund Analysis, 2020*

2. **"Go Green" campaign — repainting facades in Europe (2010–2015)** — McDonald's invested in the "environmental" redesign of some European restaurants, replacing red with green. The investment in cosmetic changes is incommensurable with the cost of genuinely reducing plastic pollution.
   *Source: McDonald's Europe Press Release, 2010*

3. **Tree planting at restaurants under "Scale for Good" (2020–2022)** — McDonald's announced a tree-planting programme in countries where it operates. The total scale of the events amounted to thousands of trees — while beef-supplier deforestation in the Amazon destroys hundreds of thousands of hectares per year.
   *Source: McDonald's Scale for Good Report, 2021; Amazon Deforestation Data, Global Forest Watch*

---

## 8. Has the company engaged in lobbying for pseudo-ecological theories?

**Answer: YES**. McDonald's actively lobbied for voluntary industry approaches instead of mandatory regulation and funded educational initiatives that shift responsibility onto consumers.

1. **Lobbying against mandatory plastic bans (2018–2021)** — McDonald's, through industry associations (National Restaurant Association and others), opposed legislative initiatives to ban single-use plastic packaging in several US states, promoting the concept of "voluntary industry standards".
   *Source: Center for Responsive Politics lobbying data; Plastics Industry Association Filings, 2019–2021*

2. **"Reduce, Reuse, Recycle" education programme for schoolchildren (2010–2020)** — McDonald's funded environmental education programmes for children that emphasised personal responsibility for waste sorting. Critics noted that the programme concealed the role of manufacturers in creating non-recyclable packaging.
   *Source: Corporate Accountability International, 2018; Break Free From Plastic analysis*

3. **Participation in lobbying against Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) in the USA (2020–2022)** — McDonald's, together with other companies, opposed the passage of extended producer responsibility legislation in California and other states, insisting that existing market mechanisms were sufficient.
   *Source: InfluenceMap Corporate Lobbying Tracker, 2021; California EPR Legislative Hearings*
