Monsanto, acquired by Bayer in 2018 for $63 billion, created Roundup — the world's most widely used glyphosate-based herbicide. The International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC) classified glyphosate as "probably carcinogenic" to humans in 2015.

Large-scale application of Roundup in the USA has destroyed more than 90% of the monarch butterfly population: the herbicide eliminated milkweed — the only plant on which the butterflies breed. The disappearance of pollinators threatens food security.

In Argentina, aerial spraying of Roundup Ready soy has contaminated groundwater and air in rural areas. Studies by the University of Córdoba documented increased cancer rates in zones of intensive glyphosate application.

By 2021 Bayer had settled approximately 100,000 lawsuits in the USA related to cancer among Roundup users, paying more than $10 billion.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. 100,000+ lawsuits over glyphosate-linked lymphoma, destruction of monarch butterfly populations, and documented increases in cancer rates in Argentine agricultural areas.

1. **100,000+ non-Hodgkin's lymphoma lawsuits from Roundup** — By 2021 Bayer had settled more than 100,000 lawsuits totalling more than $10 billion. Key cases (Dewayne Johnson, 2018; Edwin Hardeman, 2019; Alva and Alberta Pilliod, 2019) were accompanied by jury verdicts awarding hundreds of millions of dollars, based on establishing a causal link between Roundup and cancer.
   *Source: Reuters; Bayer AG Annual Report; California court records*

2. **Cancer rates in Argentine agricultural areas** — The Universidad Nacional de Córdoba documented a statistically anomalous rise in cancer rates in settlements in Córdoba province subjected to aerial spraying of Roundup Ready soy. Local doctors formed a "Network of Physicians of Fumigated Towns".
   *Source: Medicos de Pueblos Fumigados (Argentina); Universidad Nacional de Córdoba research*

3. **Destruction of the monarch butterfly population** — Large-scale application of Roundup on US GMO fields eliminated milkweed (Asclepias) — the only food plant for monarch caterpillars. The monarch butterfly population declined by more than 90% over 20 years. The monarch has been listed on the IUCN Red List as "endangered".
   *Source: IUCN Red List (2022); Xerces Society*

## 2. Has the corporation been the cause of major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. Destruction of agricultural biodiversity, contamination of water bodies and soils with glyphosate constitute systemic environmental disasters.

1. **Glyphosate in all watercourses of agricultural regions** — USGS detected glyphosate in 70%+ of water samples from rivers and streams in the American Midwest. Glyphosate and AMPA (its breakdown product) are found in sediments where they persist for years.
   *Source: USGS — Glyphosate in US Waters*

2. **Resistant "superweeds"** — More than 50 weed species have developed glyphosate resistance, forcing farmers to apply more toxic herbicides in greater quantities. The spread of resistant weeds has become a global agro-ecological disaster.
   *Source: Weed Science Society of America; USDA weed resistance data*

3. **GMO contamination of organic fields** — Pollen from Monsanto's GMO varieties (Roundup Ready soy, maize) contaminated organic farmers' crops in the USA, Canada, and Mexico, stripping them of organic certification. This led to years of legal battles.
   *Source: Center for Food Safety; Schmeiser v. Monsanto Canada*

## 3. Has the company engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. Bayer/Monsanto promotes GMOs and glyphosate as a "solution" for sustainable agriculture.

1. **"Roundup — safer than table salt" (historical marketing)** — For decades Monsanto marketed Roundup as "safe", "biodegradable", and "harmless to soil". A French court in 2009 found these claims to constitute "false advertising".
   *Source: France v. Monsanto — false advertising ruling (2009)*

2. **"Sustainable Agriculture" — Bayer's narrative** — Bayer CropScience promotes the concept of "sustainable agriculture", positioning GMOs and Roundup as tools for increasing yields with reduced environmental impact. Yet the documented environmental consequences (superweeds, monarch disappearance, water contamination) point in the opposite direction.
   *Source: Bayer CropScience marketing vs. independent research*

3. **"Climate-smart agriculture" marketing** — Bayer promotes its seeds and agrochemicals as tools for "climate-smart agriculture", without disclosing the carbon footprint of glyphosate production and nitrogen fertilisers.
   *Source: Bayer Climate-Smart Agriculture communications*

   *Source: Bayer Food Chain Partnership*

   *Source: SBTi Corporate Action*

## 6. Has the company applied eco-labels to products with negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. Roundup was labelled as "safe" and "biodegradable" despite proven carcinogenicity.

1. **"Biodegradable" labelling on Roundup** — Monsanto used a "biodegradable" label on Roundup packaging in France and several other countries, which courts found to constitute false advertising: glyphosate persists in soil and water far longer than this claim implies.
   *Source: France v. Monsanto (2009); New York AG settlement*

2. **"Helps preserve soil" — labelling for No-Till agrochemicals** — Monsanto promoted the application of Roundup in no-till farming as an "environmentally beneficial" soil-conservation practice, creating an image of environmentally responsible use among farmers and regulators.
   *Source: Monsanto no-till marketing materials*

## 7. Has the company organised eco-events negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. Bayer's bee and monarch restoration programmes are negligible compared with the harm caused.

1. **"Bee Care" programme** — Bayer launched "Bee Care Centres" — demonstration centres for supporting bee health. Critics note that Bayer's neuroactive insecticides (neonicotinoids) are one of the main causes of bee die-offs, and "Bee Care" is a PR response to the scandal.
   *Source: PAN (Pesticide Action Network) — Bayer Bee Care analysis*

2. **Monarch habitat restoration programme** — Bayer funds small milkweed-planting programmes for the monarch butterfly, while Roundup application on GMO fields remains the main factor in milkweed disappearance across entire states.
   *Source: Xerces Society; Bayer Monarch programmes*

## 8. Has the company engaged in lobbying for pseudo-ecological theories?

**Answer: YES**. Monsanto/Bayer lobbies for glyphosate safety at the level of EU and US regulators.

1. **"Monsanto Papers" — manipulation of scientific data** — Internal Monsanto documents (revealed in litigation) showed that the company systematically influenced academic publications on glyphosate safety: it helped write articles and attached the names of scientists who had not participated in the research ("ghost-writing"). This was used to convince regulators of the product's safety.
   *Source: "The Monsanto Papers" — US Federal Court Discovery*

2. **Lobbying at EFSA and EPA** — Bayer/Monsanto lobbied for glyphosate re-registration in the EU (2023: the EU extended authorisation for 10 years), using data prepared by industry consortia rather than independent scientists.
   *Source: EFSA glyphosate re-evaluation (2023); Politico EU lobbying coverage*

3. **"GMO = climate solution" narrative** — Bayer promotes GMO crops (drought-tolerant, nitrogen-fixing) as a tool for adapting to climate change and reducing fertiliser needs, positioning this as climate-responsible innovation.
   *Source: Bayer CropScience policy advocacy*
