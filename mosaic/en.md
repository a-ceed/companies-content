Mosaic Company — the world's largest producer of phosphate fertilisers — mines phosphate ore in central Florida. The refining process creates "phosphogypsum" — a mildly radioactive by-product containing uranium and radium-226.

In 2016 Mosaic reported the discovery of a massive sinkhole approximately 75 metres in diameter beneath a phosphogypsum storage stack at the New Wales plant in Mulberry, Florida. Through the sinkhole approximately 200 million gallons of mildly radioactive water leaked into the Floridan Aquifer — the state's main source of drinking water.

In Louisiana the Mosaic St. James plant is a source of chronic air and water pollution in the "Cancer Alley" area of the Mississippi — a region with anomalously high cancer rates among the African-American population.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. Contamination of drinking water with phosphogypsum and radioactive elements, and industrial emissions in "Cancer Alley", are documented.

1. **Florida sinkhole: 200 million gallons of radioactive water into the aquifer (2016)** — A sinkhole roughly 75 m in diameter beneath the New Wales (Mulberry, Florida) phosphogypsum stack allowed approximately 200 million gallons of mildly radioactive water to seep into the Floridan Aquifer — the state's primary drinking water source. Mosaic reported the incident three weeks after it was discovered.
   *Source: EPA; Florida DEP; The Guardian — "Mosaic sinkhole"*

2. **Mississippi "Cancer Alley" and the Mosaic St. James plant** — The Mosaic plant in St. James Parish, Louisiana is located in the "Cancer Alley" industrial belt along the Mississippi with anomalously high rates of lung and bladder cancer among the African-American population. Activists and researchers attribute these statistics to industrial emissions.
   *Source: ProPublica — Cancer Alley; EPA EJScreen*

3. **Air contamination with hydrogen fluoride and ammonia** — Processing of phosphate ore releases fluoride compounds and ammonia. Residents of nearby communities in Florida and Louisiana recorded exceedances of air pollution standards and associated respiratory ailments.
   *Source: EarthJustice — Mosaic Florida; FL Dept of Health*

## 2. Has the corporation been the cause of major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. The 2016 sinkhole, phosphogypsum stacks, and damage to the Floridan Aquifer constitute major environmental incidents.

1. **Sinkhole beneath the phosphogypsum stack (2016)** — The breach in the New Wales phosphogypsum stack is one of the most serious groundwater contamination accidents in the history of US mining. The Floridan Aquifer — the primary drinking water source for millions of residents — was contaminated with radioactive elements (uranium, radium, thorium).
   *Source: EPA; Florida DEP; NRDC*

2. **Chronic spills from gypsum wastewater stacks** — In 2004 a spill occurred from a gypsum wastewater stack in Riverview, Florida, contaminating Tampa Bay. Similar incidents — small spills from phosphogypsum stacks — recur regularly.
   *Source: EarthJustice — Mosaic consent decree*

3. **Groundwater contamination in Brazil** — At Mosaic's mines in Mato Grosso (Brazil), mine water and beneficiation tailings create a risk of contaminating watercourses that feed the Pantanal river system.
   *Source: Global Witness; Brazilian environmental agency records*

## 3. Has the company engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. Mosaic positions fertilisers as a tool for "sustainable" agriculture while concealing the radioactive by-products of production.

1. **"Fertilizer = Food Security" narrative** — Mosaic declares its mission as "feeding the world" through "sustainable" fertiliser production, without disclosing in public communications that phosphate fertiliser production creates enormous volumes of radioactive phosphogypsum.
   *Source: Mosaic Corporate Website vs. EPA Phosphogypsum data*

2. **Late disclosure of the 2016 sinkhole** — Mosaic reported the sinkhole beneath the phosphogypsum stack nearly three weeks after its discovery — a textbook case of concealment of environmental information.
   *Source: Florida DEP; The Guardian*

## 4. Does the company engage in ESG marketing?

**Answer: YES**. Mosaic publishes ESG reports and uses a "sustainable nutrition" narrative.

1. **ESG Reports "Nourishing the World Responsibly"** — Annual sustainability reports declare progress on emissions reductions, safe phosphogypsum management, and biodiversity programmes.
   *Source: Mosaic Sustainability Report 2023*

2. **Membership in the "Fertilizer Industry Sustainability Initiative"** — Mosaic participates in industry sustainability initiatives for fertiliser production.
   *Source: The Fertilizer Institute sustainability programmes*

## 5. Has the corporation won any global ESG awards?

**Answer: NO**. Mosaic is not included in key ESG indices and receives average ratings.

1. Mosaic is not included in DJSI or FTSE4Good. Sustainalytics ratings record a high ESG risk level linked to phosphogypsum management and a history of violations.
   *Source: Sustainalytics; MSCI ESG*

## 6. Has the company applied eco-labels to products with negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. Mosaic fertilisers are sold with a narrative of "efficient and sustainable soil nutrition".

1. **"Efficient Nutrient Use" labelling** — Mosaic promotes its product lines as "efficient" for crop nutrition, creating an impression of environmental responsibility among farmers. Application of phosphate fertilisers creates nitrate-phosphate runoff and "dead zones" in coastal waters.
   *Source: Mosaic product marketing; NOAA Dead Zone monitoring*

---

## 7. Has the company organised eco-events negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. Florida quarry reclamation programmes are negligible in scale.

1. **Quarry reclamation as "Florida Lands"** — Mosaic declares large-scale reclamation of worked-out phosphate quarries in Florida, converting them into wetlands. Independent assessments show that restored ecosystems are significantly less biologically diverse than the original natural habitats.
   *Source: Florida Wildlife Conservation Commission analysis*

2. **Partnership with Florida Audubon Society** — Mosaic sponsors a bird conservation programme with the Florida Audubon Society, used in ESG communications against a backdrop of contamination of Florida water bodies from the company's operations.
   *Source: Mosaic CSR reports*

---

## 8. Has the company engaged in lobbying for pseudo-ecological theories?

**Answer: YES**. Mosaic and industry associations lobby against tightening requirements for phosphogypsum.

1. **Lobbying against phosphogypsum storage prohibition** — The EPA has repeatedly considered tightening requirements for phosphogypsum storage facilities. Mosaic, through The Fertilizer Institute, lobbied against, arguing that the industry cannot afford safer storage.
   *Source: EPA Phosphogypsum rulemaking records; TFI lobbying disclosures*

2. **"Phosphorus is a non-renewable resource = must mine now" narrative** — Mosaic promotes a "peak phosphorus" narrative and the critical importance of phosphate resources to justify mining expansion and resist environmental regulation.
   *Source: Mosaic corporate communications; TFI policy papers*
