Musim Mas is one of the largest palm oil producers and traders in Indonesia. The company sells palm oil to Nestlé, Unilever, Colgate, and other multinational corporations.

In 2019 the Rainforest Action Network published maps of Musim Mas plantation expansion showing new clearances on peat soils in Sumatra — despite the company's declared "no deforestation" policy.

Compliance audits of Musim Mas plantations against the RSPO (Roundtable on Sustainable Palm Oil) standard revealed repeated violations: non-compliance with FPIC rights, burning of vegetation, discharge of palm oil mill effluent into water bodies causing oxygen depletion and mass fish die-offs.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. Violations of indigenous peoples' FPIC rights and smog from peatland fires in Musim Mas operating areas are documented by NGOs.

1. **Violation of indigenous peoples' FPIC rights** — The Rainforest Action Network identified cases where Musim Mas plantation expansion onto Sumatran peatlands was carried out without observing the principle of Free, Prior and Informed Consent of indigenous communities, depriving them of their traditional territories.
   *Source: Rainforest Action Network — Musim Mas Report (2019)*

2. **Smog from peatland fires** — Peatland fires near Musim Mas plantations in Sumatra in 2015 and 2019 created transboundary smog threatening the health of millions of people in Indonesia, Malaysia, and Singapore.
   *Source: ASEAN Specialised Meteorological Centre; RAN*

3. **Discharge of palm oil mill effluent into water bodies** — RSPO audits recorded that fresh fruit bunch (FFB) processing factories in Musim Mas concessions discharged untreated palm oil mill effluent (POME) into rivers. POME causes extreme oxygen depletion and mass fish die-offs — depriving coastal communities of their food source.
   *Source: RSPO complaints against Musim Mas; Indonesia EPA*

---

## 2. Has the corporation been the cause of major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. Peatland clearance and fires near Musim Mas plantations are documented environmental disasters.

1. **Peat forest clearance in Sumatra** — Satellite monitoring by RAN (2019) recorded new clearances on peat soils within Musim Mas concessions despite the declared NDPE policy. Peatlands store more than 10 times the carbon per hectare of ordinary tropical forests.
   *Source: Rainforest Action Network — Musim Mas satellite analysis*

2. **Waterway contamination by palm oil mill effluent (POME)** — Untreated effluent completely depletes rivers of oxygen when it enters them. Rivers near Musim Mas plantations in Riau recorded mass fish die-offs reported by local fishermen.
   *Source: RSPO audit findings; Jikalahari Indonesia*

3. **Threat to the Sumatran tiger and orangutan** — Plantation expansion by Musim Mas has fragmented the habitat of the Sumatran tiger and Sumatran orangutan — species listed as critically endangered.
   *Source: IUCN Red List; WWF Sumatra Tiger Conservation*

---

## 3. Has the company engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. Musim Mas holds an NDPE policy and RSPO membership, yet monitoring reveals violations.

1. **NDPE commitment alongside documented clearances** — Musim Mas signed a No Deforestation, No Peat, No Exploitation policy, but RAN documented new peatland clearances in their concessions in 2019, directly contradicting the commitment.
   *Source: RAN — Musim Mas monitoring*

2. **RSPO certification alongside violations in audits** — Musim Mas holds RSPO certification for part of its plantations, but audits revealed violations (POME discharges, non-compliance with FPIC). Certification continued to apply during periods of violation.
   *Source: RSPO complaints database*

---

## 4. Does the company engage in ESG marketing?

**Answer: YES**. Musim Mas actively communicates on sustainability in the B2B context.

1. **Sustainability Reports** — Musim Mas annually publishes sustainability reports declaring progress on NDPE, RSPO certification, and supply-chain traceability.
   *Source: Musim Mas Sustainability Report 2023*

2. **"Traceability to Plantation"** — The company declares traceability to plantation for a portion of its suppliers, which is a key ESG instrument for B2B clients (Unilever, Nestlé).
   *Source: Musim Mas Sustainability Dashboard*

---

## 5. Has the corporation won any global ESG awards?

**Answer: NO**. Musim Mas is not included in public ESG ratings and has not received international ESG awards.

1. The company is private and does not disclose MSCI or Sustainalytics ratings. RAN regularly places it on lists of NDPE violators.
   *Source: RAN Palm Oil Scorecard*

---

## 6. Has the company applied eco-labels to products with negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. Palm oil with RSPO certification is labelled as "sustainable" despite recorded violations.

1. **RSPO "Certified Sustainable Palm Oil"** — Oil certified through the RSPO scheme is labelled as "sustainable" in FMCG brand consumer products, although RSPO audits have recorded violations by Musim Mas.
   *Source: RSPO audit findings*

---

## 7. Has the company organised eco-events negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. Musim Mas biodiversity initiatives are incommensurable with the scale of plantation expansion.

1. **Buffer zone protection programme** — Musim Mas declares the establishment of buffer zones to protect biodiversity near plantations. The area of protected zones is negligible compared with the area of active plantations in Sumatra and Kalimantan.
   *Source: Musim Mas Sustainability Report*

---

## 8. Has the company engaged in lobbying for pseudo-ecological theories?

**Answer: YES**. Musim Mas participates in the industry defence of RSPO as a sufficient sustainability standard.

1. **Promoting RSPO as a substitute for stricter requirements** — Through participation in RSPO and the Palm Oil Refiners Association of Malaysia (PORAM), Musim Mas participates in opposing more stringent European supply-chain verification requirements, arguing that RSPO is sufficient.
   *Source: PORAM policy positions; EU Deforestation Regulation lobbying*

2. **Opposition to the EU Deforestation Regulation** — Musim Mas participated through industry channels in lobbying against the European Deforestation-Free Products Regulation (EUDR), which requires evidence of no deforestation in supply chains.
   *Source: Reuters — EUDR palm oil industry opposition*
