Aluminium production and bauxite mining cause red mud pollution and high carbon emissions.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. Workers at Hindalco's aluminium plants and residents of surrounding areas in India suffer from the effects of fluorides and perfluorocarbons; bauxite mining threatened to destroy the sacred lands and water sources of the Dongria Kondh indigenous people.

1. **Occupational diseases in workers from PFCs and fluorides (Odisha, India)** — Workers at Hindalco's aluminium plants in Odisha are chronically exposed to perfluorocarbons and fluorides. Long-term inhalation of fluorides causes fluorosis — a systemic disease affecting bones and teeth; PFC exposure is linked to liver and endocrine system disorders. Workers' unions have documented occupational disease cases, but the company does not publish detailed statistics.

2. **Water contamination with fluorides for farmers around plants** — Farmers living immediately adjacent to Hindalco's plants in Odisha reported contamination of drinking water sources with fluorides exceeding WHO safe levels. Elevated fluoride concentrations in water cause dental and skeletal fluorosis, particularly dangerous for children.

3. **Threat to destroy the sacred lands of the Dongria Kondh people (2013)** — Hindalco's plans to mine bauxite on Niyamgiri mountain in Odisha threatened to destroy the sacred lands and water sources of the Dongria Kondh tribe. The Supreme Court of India in 2013 transferred the decision to a referendum by the tribes themselves: all 12 village councils voted against mining. This landmark ruling became one of the greatest victories for indigenous peoples in Indian history.

## 2. Was the corporation responsible for major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. The activities of Novelis and Hindalco are associated with threats to the Amazon ecosystem, violations of EPA emission standards and risks of catastrophic red mud accidents.

1. **Red mud lagoons in Pará state (Brazil) threatening Amazon tributaries** — Bauxite mining and alumina production in Pará state generates enormous volumes of red mud — toxic waste containing iron, aluminium, sodium oxides and heavy metals. The mud lagoons are located near Amazon tributaries. If a dam were to rupture (precedent: the Ajka accident of 2010 in Hungary — 10 dead, Danube contamination), a regional environmental catastrophe would be possible.

2. **Violation of EPA PFC emission standards at Novelis plant in Oswego (USA, 2019)** — The Novelis smelting plant in Oswego, New York, recorded a violation of US Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) standards for perfluorocarbon emissions. Since CF₄ and C₂F₆ are long-lived greenhouse gases (atmospheric lifetime of thousands of years), even small exceedances have irreversible climate consequences.

3. **Systemic red mud risks from alumina production** — Primary aluminium production inevitably generates red mud at a ratio of ~1.5 tonnes per tonne of alumina. World red mud storage facilities contain more than 4 billion tonnes of toxic waste. The Ajka disaster example (Hungary, 2010) showed that a dam rupture destroys all life in rivers and can reach major water bodies.

## 3. Has the company engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. Novelis actively promotes a "green aluminium" narrative through its high recycling rate, while not disclosing the full environmental footprint of the Hindalco group, which continues primary production with PFC emissions.

1. **"80% recycled aluminium" — excluding Hindalco's primary production** — Novelis positions itself as a leader in aluminium recycling (up to 80% recycled content), creating an image of a "green" company. However, Hindalco — the parent structure — continues to produce primary aluminium by electrolysis with unavoidable PFC emissions. Novelis's separate "green" narrative masks the group's overall environmental footprint.

2. **"Green aluminium" for BMW and Audi without disclosing total environmental footprint** — Novelis's supply of "low-carbon" aluminium to automakers (BMW, Audi) is used as a marketing tool, while the consolidated emissions of the Hindalco–Novelis group include primary production where PFC emissions are unavoidable with current technology.

3. **"Net zero" target by 2050 without a PFC plan** — The group declares climate targets for 2050 without publishing a specific plan to eliminate CF₄ and C₂F₆ emissions — the main non-thermal contributions of the aluminium industry to climate change.

## 4. Does the company participate in ESG marketing?

**Answer: YES**. Both companies actively publish ESG reports and participate in international sustainable development initiatives.

1. **Novelis — Sustainability Report** — Novelis publishes an annual detailed sustainability report with data on emissions, water use and percentage of recycled raw materials. The report is used to position the company as the "most sustainable aluminium producer."

2. **Hindalco — Business Responsibility and Sustainability Report (BRSR)** — In accordance with SEBI requirements, Hindalco publishes a BRSR declaring commitment to ESG principles and UN Sustainable Development Goals.

3. **Membership in Aluminium Stewardship Initiative (ASI)** — Both companies are members of ASI — the industry initiative for standardizing responsible aluminium production, which is used as evidence of "sustainability" in negotiations with corporate clients.

## 5. Has the corporation won any global ESG competition awards?

**Answer: YES**. Novelis and Hindalco have received industry certificates and international rating recognition in the ESG field.

1. **Novelis — ASI Performance Standard certificate** — Novelis received ASI Performance Standard certification, confirming compliance with responsible aluminium production standards. The certificate is widely used in the company's marketing materials.

2. **Hindalco — Dow Jones Sustainability Index Emerging Markets** — Hindalco is included in the DJSI Emerging Markets index, which covers companies with best ESG practices among developing markets.

3. **Hindalco — CDP rating B** — Hindalco received a B rating from the Carbon Disclosure Project, indicating "awareness" of climate governance issues. The rating is positioned as evidence of progress on the climate agenda.

## 6. Has the company applied eco-labels to products with a negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. Novelis uses eco-labeling on aluminium products, creating the impression of complete environmental safety while PFC emissions continue.

1. **"Infinitely recyclable" labeling on aluminium cans** — Aluminium cans manufactured by Novelis are labeled as "infinitely recyclable." The claim is correct in terms of the metal's properties, but creates a false sense of the product's environmental neutrality, ignoring PFC emissions from primary aluminium production in the Hindalco group.

2. **"Green aluminium" advertising for automakers** — Novelis actively promotes the concept of "green aluminium" (green aluminium) for automaker clients, using labeling and marketing materials that emphasize the recycling rate. Meanwhile, the systemic PFC emissions from primary aluminium production within the group remain in the background.

3. **Branding products for BMW and Audi as "low-carbon"** — The aluminium supplied to BMW and Audi is positioned as "low-carbon," which creates for end consumers (car buyers) a sense of purchasing an "environmentally friendly" vehicle without understanding the full production footprint.

## 7. Has the company organized eco-events that are negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. Against the scale of bauxite mining and water body contamination, Hindalco's corporate environmental programs are incomparably small.

1. **5,000 hectare reforestation in Odisha while mining deforestation continues** — Hindalco declares reforestation programs in Odisha state covering about 5,000 hectares. Meanwhile, the area of forest clearing and degradation under the company's mining projects significantly exceeds this figure.

2. **"Water projects" for 2 villages while dozens of water bodies are contaminated** — Hindalco implements local water supply programs for several villages near plants, presenting them in ESG reports as contributions to "community water security." The scale of the programs is incomparable to the documented fluoride contamination of drinking water sources in dozens of settlements.

3. **School eco-lessons in several villages near plants** — The company conducts environmental education events for schoolchildren in villages near production facilities. Program coverage amounts to several hundred children annually, while systemic contamination impacts thousands of families in the industrial influence zone.

## 8. Has the company lobbied for pseudo-ecological theories and engaged in eco-education?

**Answer: YES**. The Novelis–Hindalco group promotes policy positions favorable to maintaining primary aluminium production through industry associations.

1. **Lobbying through the International Aluminium Institute against strict PFC standards** — Through the International Aluminium Institute (IAI), the group participates in shaping international climate standards for the industry, advocating for "technology-neutral" approaches that permit continued PFC emissions provided there is gradual "intensity" reduction.

2. **Promoting "technology-neutral" climate policies** — The group lobbies for the inclusion of primary-production aluminium in "low-carbon" material categories provided there is partial use of renewable electricity — without accounting for PFC emissions. This allows primary aluminium to be sold as "green" without actually resolving the perfluorocarbon problem.

3. **Shaping ASI standards in the interests of existing producers** — Participation in developing Aluminium Stewardship Initiative standards gives the group influence over requirements that it itself must meet. Critics note that ASI standards are insufficiently strict regarding PFC emission limits and do not require data disclosure at the level of individual plants.
