Potash and nitrogen fertiliser production contaminates groundwater and rivers.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. Nutrien's products — nitrogen, phosphate and potash fertilisers — when over-applied, cause documented harm to human health through drinking water contamination, cancer risks and emergency poisonings in production.

1. **Nitrates in drinking water — methemoglobinemia in infants ("blue baby syndrome")** — Nitrogen fertilisers, which Nutrien produces and sells in mass volumes, when leached into groundwater raise nitrate concentrations above WHO standards (10 mg/L NO₃-N). Nitrates are converted to nitrites in infants' bodies, blocking oxygen transport by haemoglobin — methemoglobinemia can be fatal. The problem is documented in intensive farming regions worldwide, including the US Midwest — Nutrien's key market.

2. **Elevated risk of cancer (NHL) in farmers** — Epidemiological studies in zones of intensive nitrogen fertiliser use in the US Midwest document elevated non-Hodgkin's lymphoma (NHL) rates among farmers. Nitrates in drinking water and direct contact with fertilisers are classified as probable carcinogens by the IARC.

3. **Acute ammonia poisonings among workers in Trinidad** — Emergency situations involving toxic ammonia releases have occurred at Nutrien's ammonia and urea production plants in Trinidad. Acute ammonia poisoning causes chemical burns to the respiratory tract, eyes and skin; in severe cases, death.

## 2. Was the corporation responsible for major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. Nutrien's activities are associated with contributing to the world's largest ocean "dead zone," chronic salinisation of Canadian water bodies and permit violations.

1. **Gulf of Mexico "dead zone" (22,000 km² in 2019)** — The annual hypoxic "dead zone" in the Gulf of Mexico — one of the largest in the world — is largely caused by nitrogen fertiliser runoff from the US Midwest. Nutrien is the largest supplier of nitrogen fertilisers in this market. In the hypoxic zone, fish, crustaceans and benthic organisms perish; damage to the Gulf of Mexico fishing industry amounts to hundreds of millions of dollars annually.

2. **Salinisation of small rivers in Saskatchewan from chloride tailings of potash mines** — Nutrien's potash mines in Saskatchewan generate enormous volumes of chloride salt waste (tailings). Historical and ongoing brine leaks lead to salinisation of small rivers and wetlands, destroying freshwater ecosystems and making water unsuitable for irrigation and livestock.

3. **Unauthorised brine discharge at Allan mine (Saskatchewan, 2022)** — In 2022, a licensing condition violation was identified at the Allan potash mine: unauthorised discharge of saline brine into the ground and surface water bodies. The incident confirmed that environmental violations continue even under modern legislation.

## 3. Has the company engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. Nutrien systematically presents its activities as part of solving the food security problem, concealing the climate and environmental footprint of fertiliser production.

1. **Fertilisers as "necessary for food security" — without mention of N₂O** — Nutrien positions nitrogen fertilisers exclusively in the context of their role in ensuring crop yields and global food security. Meanwhile, the application of nitrogen fertilisers is the primary source of nitrous oxide (N₂O) — a greenhouse gas with a global warming potential 273 times higher than CO₂ (over 100 years). This footprint is systematically excluded from the company's public communications.

2. **"4R Nutrient Stewardship" programme as an environmental initiative — effectively a sales tool** — Nutrien actively promotes the "4R" programme (right product, right rate, right time, right place) as an environmental one. Critics and independent agronomists note that the programme is primarily aimed at increasing fertiliser use efficiency — i.e., increasing sales while maintaining overall application volumes.

3. **ESG reports conceal Scope 3 emissions (N₂O from fertiliser application)** — Nutrien publishes Scope 1 and 2 emission data (production and energy), but does not fully disclose Scope 3 — in particular, N₂O emissions that occur when farmers apply its fertilisers. Scope 3 constitutes the main share of the company's climate footprint.

## 4. Does the company participate in ESG marketing?

**Answer: YES**. Nutrien actively publishes ESG reports, declares climate targets and participates in industry sustainable development initiatives.

1. **Annual ESG & Sustainability Report** — The company publishes a detailed sustainable development report with data on emissions, water use, safety programs and social investments. The report is used to position Nutrien as a "responsible producer" in dialogue with investors and regulators.

2. **Net zero targets for Scope 1+2 by 2050** — Nutrien declares achievement of net zero for Scope 1 and 2 emissions by 2050. Scope 3 (N₂O from fertiliser application) is not included in the target — even though these emissions dominate the company's overall climate footprint.

3. **Membership in Fertilizer Canada and CropLife** — Nutrien is a member of leading Canadian and US industry associations, participates in their ESG initiatives and uses membership as confirmation of its commitment to responsible production principles.

---

## 5. Has the corporation won any global ESG competition awards?

**Answer: YES**. Nutrien has received recognition from leading international ESG agencies and ratings.

1. **CDP rating B** — Nutrien received a B rating from the Carbon Disclosure Project, corresponding to the level of "management" of climate risks. The rating is used in communications with investors as evidence of progress.

2. **MSCI rating A** — International rating agency MSCI assigned Nutrien an A rating on the ESG scale, placing the company in the "leaders" category in its industry.

3. **Inclusion in S&P 500 ESG and TSX ESG indices** — Nutrien shares are included in the ESG indices of the S&P 500 and Toronto Stock Exchange (TSX), which automatically attracts ESG-oriented institutional investors and reinforces the company's positioning as a "sustainable" business.

---

## 6. Has the company applied eco-labels to products with a negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. Nutrien uses eco-labeling on fertilisers while continuing to produce products with a significant environmental footprint.

1. **eKonomik product line with "eco-efficiency" icon** — Some Nutrien fertilisers are sold under the eKonomik brand with labeling emphasizing the product's "ecological efficiency." The labeling creates a sense of environmental safety without disclosing data on the fertilisers' contribution to N₂O emissions.

2. **Enhanced Efficiency Fertilizers (EEF) — "environmentally responsible" labeling** — The slow-release nutrient fertiliser product line (EEF) is labeled as "environmentally responsible." Despite the real agronomic effect of reducing leaching losses, EEF use does not eliminate N₂O emissions — it merely reduces them slightly compared to standard fertilisers.

3. **"4R" logo on products** — Nutrien products complying with the "4R Nutrient Stewardship" programme are labeled with the branded logo, which consumers (farmers) perceive as ecological certification. "4R" is a proprietary programme of the industry association, not an independent ecological label.

---

## 7. Has the company organized eco-events that are negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. Nutrien's corporate environmental initiatives are incomparably small relative to the scale of environmental damage caused.

1. **Restoration of ~500 ha of wetlands in Saskatchewan** — Nutrien declares wetland restoration programmes in Saskatchewan covering approximately 500 ha. Meanwhile, the salinisation of small rivers and wetlands from chloride potash mine tailings covers thousands of hectares.

2. **"Tree Day" with planting of several hundred trees near headquarters** — The company holds annual symbolic greening events around its headquarters in Saskatoon. The scale of these events is incomparable to CO₂ emissions from fertiliser production or ecosystem degradation associated with intensive use of the company's products.

3. **Small grants to farmers for "nitrification inhibitors"** — Nutrien provides small subsidies to farmers for the use of nitrification inhibitors, which reduce nitrogen losses and N₂O emissions. The volume of the programme is negligible relative to billions in standard nitrogen fertiliser sales where the N₂O problem is not solved.

---

## 8. Has the company lobbied for pseudo-ecological theories and engaged in eco-education?

**Answer: YES**. Nutrien actively opposes regulatory measures that would limit its business model through industry associations.

1. **Lobbying against stricter nitrate standards in water** — Through Fertilizer Canada and The Fertilizer Institute (USA), Nutrien participates in opposing attempts to tighten nitrate standards in drinking water. Stricter standards would require farmers to reduce nitrogen fertiliser use, directly reducing the company's sales.

2. **Opposition to mandatory inclusion of N₂O in Scope 3 reporting** — The company lobbies against mandatory Scope 3 disclosure requirements, including N₂O from the application of produced fertilisers. Disclosure of this data would make the company's climate footprint many times more apparent to investors and regulators.

3. **Lobbying for "carbon farming" subsidies as compensation for synthetic fertiliser production** — Nutrien promotes the concept of "carbon farming," under which farmers receive subsidies for carbon sequestration in soil. The company presents this as compensation for the climate footprint of fertilisers, effectively seeking state financing that allows continued synthetic fertiliser production without reducing volumes.
