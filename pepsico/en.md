Second-largest producer of plastic waste among global food companies.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. PepsiCo's activities have been linked to harm to human health and animal cruelty in supply chains, ultimately affecting the well-being of communities.

1. **Lawsuit over BPA in Aquafina water (2025)** — Earth Island Institute filed a lawsuit claiming that an independent laboratory assessment found **bisphenol-A (BPA)** in Aquafina bottled water. According to the Mayo Clinic, BPA poses risks to the brain and prostate health of fetuses, infants and children, and may affect children's behavior.
   *Source: Plastics News — "Environmental group Earth Island sues PepsiCo over plastics use"*

2. **PETA lawsuit over cruelty to bulls in India (2026)** — PETA filed a lawsuit against PepsiCo for violating its own Global Animal Welfare Policy. The lawsuit alleges that in sugarcane supply chains in India, bulls are **forced to haul loads up to 8,000 pounds** (four times the permissible weight) in scorching heat without water or rest. Animals are beaten, suffer bloody wounds from barbed wire, and some are **beaten to death**.
   *Source: PETA — "All Fizz, No Action: PepsiCo Slammed For Ignoring Abuse of Bulls in Its Sugar Pipeline"*

3. **Lawsuit over blocking PETA shareholder resolution (2026)** — PETA sued PepsiCo for attempting to block a shareholder vote on a resolution requiring a report on animal welfare in supply chains. The company initially tried to exclude the resolution, but after the lawsuit agreed to include it in the annual meeting agenda.
   *Source: PETA — "PepsiCo Sued Over Attempt to Bury PETA Shareholder Resolution Over Abuse of Bulls"*

## 2. Was the corporation responsible for major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. Although the company avoided catastrophes on the scale of Deepwater Horizon, it was responsible for major environmental pollution incidents and is involved in litigation over systemic plastic contamination.

1. **Mountain Dew concentrate spill in Michigan (2017)** — A tank cracked at a Pepsi plant in Howell, and **7,200 gallons** of concentrated Mountain Dew syrup entered the drainage system. This caused "massive foaming" and resulted in **56,000 gallons** of sugar-containing wastewater entering a storm retention basin. The DEQ stated that high sugar content can have toxic effects on aquatic life.
   *Source: USA Today — "Pepsi mops up 'unusual' Mountain Dew spill"*

2. **Los Angeles plastic pollution lawsuit (2024)** — Los Angeles County sued PepsiCo and Coca-Cola, accusing them of a **"disinformation campaign"** about the recyclability of plastic bottles. The lawsuit claims that bottles can only be recycled once, if at all, and that the companies are the world's largest plastic polluters.
   *Source: Newsweek — "Coca-Cola and Pepsi sued for allegedly 'misleading' consumers"*

3. **Baltimore plastic pollution lawsuit (2024)** — The Mayor and City Council of Baltimore sued PepsiCo, Coca-Cola and other single-use plastic manufacturers. The case is pending and is part of a growing wave of lawsuits against plastic producers.
   *Source: Beveridge & Diamond PC — "Litigation Targeting Plastics Manufacturers"*

## 3. Has the company engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. The company has repeatedly been accused of greenwashing — misleading consumers about the environmental friendliness of its products and its efforts to combat plastic pollution.

1. **New York lawsuit (2023)** — The New York Attorney General accused PepsiCo of creating a public nuisance through plastic pollution of the Buffalo River and of **misleading statements about its efforts to reduce virgin plastic use**. The lawsuit was dismissed in December 2024, but the fact of the charges is documented.
   *Source: Beveridge & Diamond PC — "Litigation Targeting Plastics Manufacturers"*

2. **Earth Island Institute lawsuit (2025)** — The environmental group Plastic Pollution Coalition filed a lawsuit against PepsiCo, claiming the company uses **deceptive marketing**, calling its Aquafina water "pure" and its business practices "sustainable." The lawsuit also claims that bisphenol-A (BPA) was found in Aquafina bottles despite "BPA-free" labeling.
   *Source: Plastics News — "Environmental group Earth Island sues PepsiCo over plastics use"*

3. **US Virgin Islands lawsuit (2025)** — The government of the Virgin Islands sued PepsiCo and Coca-Cola for **misleading consumers** with claims about plastic bottle recyclability. The lawsuit alleges that the companies "brand themselves as sustainable" while in reality their products create an unmanageable waste problem on the islands.
   *Source: Newsweek — "Coca-Cola and Pepsi sued for allegedly 'misleading' consumers"*

## 4. Does the company participate in ESG marketing?

**Answer: YES**. PepsiCo actively participates in ESG marketing, promoting its "pep+" (PepsiCo Positive) strategy through public reports, press releases and senior management statements.

1. **Publication of Climate Transition Plan (2025)** — The company published a Climate Transition Plan, updating Scope 1, 2 and 3 emission targets, and declared its aspiration for **net zero emissions by 2050**. This is a key element of ESG communication.
   *Source: LinkedIn (Nabil Sousou, Senior VP PepsiCo) — "PepsiCo ranked among the 250 Best-Managed Companies"*

2. **Publication of 2024 ESG Report (August 2025)** — In the report, the company declared replenishing **more than 24 billion liters of water** to local watersheds, expanding regenerative agriculture to **3.5 million acres** and achieving **89%** renewable electricity at company-owned facilities.
   *Source: LinkedIn (Nabil Sousou)*

3. **The Wall Street Journal ranking (2025)** — PepsiCo ranked **14th in the WSJ 250 Best-Managed Companies** and **5th in Social Responsibility**. This ranking is actively used in the company's ESG marketing.
   *Source: LinkedIn (Nabil Sousou)*

## 6. Has the company applied eco-labels to products with a negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. The company used eco-labeling (specifically recyclability claims) on goods which, according to lawsuits, harm the environment.

1. **"100% Recyclable" labeling on plastic bottles** — The Earth Island Institute lawsuit claims that PepsiCo used misleading recyclability labeling, while in reality most plastic bottles are not recycled. The Los Angeles lawsuit contains similar allegations: companies claimed that bottles are "continuously recycled," although in reality they can be recycled only once, if at all.
   *Source: Plastics News — "Environmental group Earth Island sues PepsiCo over plastics use"; Newsweek*

2. **"BPA-free" labeling on Aquafina water** — The Earth Island Institute lawsuit claims that an independent laboratory study found **BPA in Aquafina bottles**, making the "BPA-free" labeling misleading.
   *Source: Plastics News*

3. **Claims of "purity" (pure) and "sustainability" (sustainable)** — A Washington, DC lawsuit challenges the use of these terms as "inherently false," since plastic contaminates water and the environment, and recycling does not solve the plastic pollution crisis.
   *Source: Plastics News*

## 7. Has the company organized eco-events that are negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. The company conducted eco-events that critics might call negligible compared to the scale of pollution it creates. This is also reflected in lawsuits.

1. **River cleanup project (2019)** — PepsiCo and Coca-Cola invested **$11 million** in a river cleanup project. By comparison, in the same year the companies spent **$4.24 billion** on marketing and advertising. The Virgin Islands lawsuit calls these investments "insufficient or unbalanced" compared to the scale of pollution created.
   *Source: Newsweek*

2. **Reusable cup programs (2025)** — The company expanded reusable cup programs with partners (Bold Reuse, San Francisco 49ers, Arizona Diamondbacks, University of Washington). Environmental activists during the Paris Olympics (2024) criticized Coca-Cola and Pepsi for using plastic cups at stadiums, calling it "double plastic use" and "a waste of time."
   *Source: LinkedIn (Nabil Sousou)*

3. **5% reduction in virgin plastic (2024)** — The company reported a 5% reduction in the absolute mass of virgin plastic in primary packaging in key markets. Critics note this is insufficient: in 2022 PepsiCo increased its use of virgin plastic by **11%**, despite public pledges of environmental commitment.
   *Source: LinkedIn; comparison with data from New York lawsuit*

## 8. Has the company lobbied for pseudo-ecological theories and engaged in eco-education?

**Answer: YES**. The company actively lobbies in the US Congress on issues related to plastics, recycling and sustainability, including promoting its own version of the solution.

1. **Lobbying plastic and recycling legislation (2025)** — According to lobbying reports for Q1 and Q3 2025, PepsiCo lobbied on issues related to **plastic and recycling legislation**, including H.R.4109 (Recycling and Composting Accountability Act), and engaged with Congress on **recycling programs and EPA regulation**.
   *Source: Nasdaq — "Lobbying Update: $90,000 of PEPSICO INC. lobbying was just disclosed"; Quiver Quantitative*

2. **Educating Congress members about PepsiCo's priorities** — Lobbying reports indicate that the company engages in "educating members of Congress about PepsiCo's priorities" in the context of the Farm Bill and infrastructure bills, including issues of **climate-smart agriculture, resource conservation and rural development**.
   *Source: Nasdaq; Quiver Quantitative*

3. **Promoting the concept of "responsible supply chain" and "improved residential recycling programs"** — Lobbying reports show that the company promotes its sustainability agenda, including **improving recycling programs** and **responsible supply chains**, which may be viewed as an attempt to shape legislation in accordance with corporate interests.
   *Source: Nasdaq*
