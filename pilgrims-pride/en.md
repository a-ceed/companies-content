Pilgrim's Pride — the second-largest US poultry producer — operates thousands of poultry farms across southern US states. Chicken manure is a concentrated source of nitrogen, phosphorus, and pathogens that pollute waterways when improperly managed.

In Arkansas and Oklahoma the Illinois River watershed has been blanketed with algae due to excess nutrients from Pilgrim's Pride farms and several other companies. Oklahoma filed a lawsuit against major poultry companies in 2005, accusing them of causing hundreds of millions of dollars in damage to the river.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. Pilgrim's Pride's operations have been associated with harm to workers' health, violations of consumer rights, and participation in price-fixing that affected millions of American families.

1. **Department of Justice criminal price-fixing lawsuit (2020)** — The US Department of Justice brought charges against Pilgrim's Pride management and several other major poultry producers for **cartel price-fixing**. In 2021 the company agreed to pay **$107 million** in settlement. Economists estimated that the price inflation affected tens of millions of American consumers over several years.
   *Source: U.S. Department of Justice — "Pilgrim's Pride to Pay $107 Million" (2021)*

2. **COVID-19 outbreaks at the Greeley, Colorado plant (2020)** — Despite rapid spread of the coronavirus among workers, Pilgrim's Pride management continued production. At the Greeley plant (the largest in Colorado) **more than 200 workers** were infected and several died. Unions and human rights advocates accused the company of inadequate protective measures and pressure on workers to conceal illness.
   *Source: Denver Post — "Pilgrim's Pride workers and COVID-19 at Greeley plant" (2020)*

3. **Workplace injuries and musculoskeletal disorders** — Workers at Pilgrim's Pride slaughterhouses consistently rank among those with the highest occupational injury rates of any US industry. OSHA reports document elevated rates of carpal tunnel syndrome, cuts, and fractures. The average hourly wage is around $15 against production quotas that exclude breaks.
   *Source: Food & Water Watch — "The Case for Slowing Down the Poultry Line"*

## 2. Has the corporation been the cause of major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. Pilgrim's Pride is one of the key contributors to the systemic contamination of water bodies in the southern US states with chicken manure.

1. **Oklahoma lawsuit against poultry companies (2005)** — The State of Oklahoma filed a lawsuit against **nine poultry companies**, including Pilgrim's Pride, alleging that excessive application of chicken manure as fertiliser caused the chronic nutrient pollution of the Illinois River basin. The river suffered years of algal blooms and fish kills. The lawsuit was valued at **hundreds of millions of dollars**.
   *Source: Oklahoma Attorney General — "State of Oklahoma v. Tyson Foods et al." (2005)*

2. **Clean Water Act violations at the Texas plant (2023)** — The EPA documented exceedances of nitrogen and phosphorus standards in wastewater from Pilgrim's Pride's Livingston, Texas plant. A corrective order was issued after systematic measurements showed discharge levels into adjacent waterways exceeding permitted limits.
   *Source: EPA ECHO Database — Pilgrim's Pride, Livingston TX (2023)*

3. **Groundwater contamination in Arkansas** — Analysis of groundwater in Arkansas counties with high concentrations of Pilgrim's Pride poultry farms found elevated nitrate levels exceeding EPA drinking water standards. Residents of Washington County (AR) and several neighbouring counties were using well water with above-standard nitrate levels.
   *Source: Environmental Working Group — "Poultry Industry Contaminating Drinking Water Across the South"*

## 3. Has the company engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. Pilgrim's Pride uses "responsible poultry farming" and "sustainable production" marketing that does not reflect the company's actual environmental and social practices.

1. **Just Bare brand — "humanely raised" despite documented violations** — The Just Bare brand is positioned as a product of "humane poultry farming" and "no antibiotics ever". However, a 2022 supply-chain audit found contract farms where stocking density and housing conditions did not meet the declared standards. The USDA reviewed the "no antibiotics ever" labelling claim.
   *Source: USDA FSIS — "Labeling claims review: Just Bare" (2022)*

2. **"Sustainable agriculture" reports without runoff data** — Pilgrim's Pride publishes sustainability sections in annual reports, mentioning "manure management" and "emissions reduction". However, the reports contain no data on CWA violations at contract farms — which the company formally does not control, although in practice dictates all conditions to them through growing contracts.
   *Source: Food & Water Watch — "Corporate Agribusiness and Sustainability Reporting"*

3. **Feed Conversion Ratio as an "environmental achievement"** — The company promotes the reduction of the Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR) as an environmental achievement reducing emissions per kilogram of meat. Critics point out that when total production volume simultaneously grows, the cumulative water body burden from manure only increases.
   *Source: Institute for Agriculture and Trade Policy — "Measuring Sustainability in Poultry Production"*

## 4. Does the company engage in ESG marketing?

**Answer: YES**. Pilgrim's Pride, as a subsidiary of JBS, publishes ESG reports and declares targets in the area of climate and social responsibility.

1. **Annual Sustainability Report** — Pilgrim's Pride is included in the consolidated ESG report of parent company JBS, which declares targets for reducing Scope 1+2 emissions by 2030, programmes on water resource management, and animal welfare.
   *Source: JBS Global — "Annual and Sustainability Report 2023"*

2. **Greenhouse gas reduction targets to 2030** — The company announced an intention to reduce emission intensity (per unit of production) by 30% by 2030 under the JBS Net Zero programme. The programme does not cover emissions from feed grain cultivation (Scope 3), which constitute the main share of the climate footprint.
   *Source: JBS — "Climate Commitment and Net Zero roadmap"*

3. **SAI Platform and GLOBALG.A.P. membership** — Pilgrim's Pride participates in industry initiatives for standardising sustainable agriculture, which is used in marketing materials and negotiations with major retail chains (Walmart, Costco).
   *Source: SAI Platform — "Members list" (2024)*

## 5. Has the corporation won any global ESG awards?

**Answer: YES**. Pilgrim's Pride through its parent company JBS has received industry ESG recognition.

1. **JBS in the ISE Index (B3, Brazil)** — Parent company JBS was included in the corporate sustainability index of the Brazilian stock exchange B3 (ISE), which was positioned as confirmation of ESG practices across the entire group, including Pilgrim's Pride.
   *Source: B3 — "ISE Sustainability Index compositions"*

2. **Meat & Poultry industry awards** — Pilgrim's Pride is periodically mentioned in rankings by industry publications Meat+Poultry as a "leader in production efficiency" and "technological innovation", which is used in ESG communications.
   *Source: WATT Global Media — Meat+Poultry rankings*

3. **GLOBALG.A.P. certification for certain farms** — Individual contract farms in Pilgrim's Pride's supply chain have obtained GLOBALG.A.P. sustainable agriculture certification, which is referenced in corporate materials.
   *Source: GLOBALG.A.P. — certified operations database*

## 6. Has the company applied eco-labels to products with negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. Pilgrim's Pride brands use labelling that creates a false impression of environmental and ethical integrity among consumers.

1. **Just Bare — "responsibly raised", "no antibiotics ever"** — Just Bare products are labelled as produced "responsibly" and "without antibiotics", while production takes place at standard industrial poultry facilities with high stocking density. Birds have no outdoor access, despite marketing images of "happy chickens".
   *Source: Consumer Reports — "What 'humanely raised' really means on chicken labels"*

2. **Gold'n Plump — "farm fresh", "natural"** — The Gold'n Plump brand uses "farm fresh" and "natural" labelling that associates the product with small-scale farming, even though production is standard industrial CAFO (Confined Animal Feeding Operation).
   *Source: USDA FSIS — Labeling policy for "natural" claims*

3. **"Green packaging" icon** — Since 2022 Pilgrim's Pride has placed a "recycled packaging" icon on some product packaging, even though expanded polystyrene trays (traditional for poultry products) remain the dominant packaging form and are not recyclable in most US municipalities.
   *Source: Sustainable Packaging Coalition — "Poultry packaging recyclability audit"*

---

## 7. Has the company organised eco-events negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. Pilgrim's Pride's pilot "environmental" programmes are incommensurable with the scale of environmental damage from industrial poultry farming.

1. **Biogas pilot project at a single farm (2021)** — Pilgrim's Pride announced a partnership with Opal Fuels to install a biogas unit at one contract farm in Arkansas. With thousands of farms in the company's supply system, the programme's coverage is less than 0.1% of total manure production volume.
   *Source: Business Wire — "Pilgrim's Pride and Opal Fuels announce RNG pilot project" (2021)*

2. **"Manure composting" for local farmers** — The manure composting subsidy programme for small agricultural producers covers a few hundred tonnes per year against the millions of tonnes of manure generated annually at the company's farms.
   *Source: Pilgrim's Pride — Sustainability Report (2022)*

3. **"Water conservation" educational programmes** — The company funds several school programmes in Arkansas and Texas on the topic of water conservation, while itself being one of the largest sources of nitrate and phosphorus pollution in the region's water bodies.
   *Source: Pilgrim's Pride Foundation — community programs*

---

## 8. Has the company engaged in lobbying for pseudo-ecological theories?

**Answer: YES**. Pilgrim's Pride, through industry organisations, actively lobbies against the tightening of environmental standards for livestock farming.

1. **Lobbying against tightening CWA norms for CAFOs** — Through the National Chicken Council (NCC), Pilgrim's Pride lobbies against extending Clean Water Act requirements to runoff from fields fertilised with chicken manure. The industry position is that manure is "fertiliser", not "industrial waste", which allows avoiding stricter NPDES regulation.
   *Source: National Chicken Council — Federal Policy Positions (2023)*

2. **Opposition to mandatory independent animal welfare auditing** — The NCC and Pilgrim's Pride oppose a legislative requirement for independent verification of compliance with declared humane animal treatment standards (instead of self-certification), arguing that this "would create unnecessary bureaucratic burden".
   *Source: USDA FSIS — public comments on "Organic and humane labeling" proposed rule (2022)*

3. **Promoting "efficiency science" as an environmental solution** — Through funding research at the Universities of Arkansas and Georgia, the company promotes the narrative that increases in efficiency (FCR) automatically solve poultry farming's environmental problems, without the need for stricter runoff regulation or reductions in production volume.
   *Source: University of Arkansas Division of Agriculture — industry-funded research partnerships*
