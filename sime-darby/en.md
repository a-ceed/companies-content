Sime Darby oil palm plantations have destroyed thousands of hectares of peatland forests in Malaysia.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. Forced labour of migrants and violations of labour rights on Sime Darby plantations have been documented by international organisations and have had direct consequences for thousands of workers.

1. **CBP ban due to forced labour (2021)** — The U.S. Customs and Border Protection (CBP) issued a Withhold Release Order against Sime Darby Plantation, banning imports of its palm oil after investigators found indicators of forced labour: document confiscation, debt bondage, excessive overtime, and restriction of freedom of movement for migrants from Indonesia and Bangladesh.
   *Source: U.S. Customs and Border Protection — WRO against Sime Darby Plantation Sdn. Bhd.*

2. **Amnesty International report on labour violations (2020)** — Amnesty International documented violations of the rights of 30,000+ workers: illegal wage deductions, lack of protective equipment when handling pesticides, and breaches of occupational safety standards. Workers reported being coerced into unpaid "voluntary" days off.
   *Source: Amnesty International — "The Great Palm Oil Scandal" (2016) and subsequent updates*

3. **Land seizures from indigenous peoples in Liberia (2010–2015)** — In Liberia, Sime Darby seized hundreds of thousands of hectares for palm plantations without observing the principle of Free, Prior and Informed Consent (FPIC). Communities reported demolition of homes, destruction of vegetable gardens and livelihoods; conflicts led to physical violence against local residents.
   *Source: Global Witness — "Rubber Barons" (2013)*


## 2. Has the corporation been responsible for major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. Drainage of peatlands and fires on Sime Darby plantations contribute to regional environmental catastrophes.

1. **Peatland fires in Malaysia and Indonesia** — Draining peat bogs for palm plantations makes them extremely fire-prone. The 2015 fires, in which peatlands burned — including lands of Sime Darby's suppliers — created a smog cloud the size of Europe. This disaster is estimated to have cost the lives of 100,000 people (Harvard/Columbia study, 2016).
   *Source: Environmental Research Letters — "Mortality impacts of the 2015 Indonesian fires"*

2. **Destruction of mangrove forests in Sabah** — The NGO Chain Reaction Research found that plantations within Sime Darby's concessions in Sabah encroached on mangrove zones and riparian buffer areas. Mangroves are a critical habitat for fishing by local communities.
   *Source: Chain Reaction Research — Sime Darby analysis (2020)*

3. **Degradation of Borneo's rivers** — Herbicides and fertilisers from Sime Darby's plantations on Borneo contaminate tributaries flowing into the Sulawesi Sea. Mass fish kills and the disappearance of aquatic invertebrates near plantation drainage outlets have been recorded.
   *Source: Rainforest Action Network — Palm Oil Reports*


## 3. Has the company engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. Sime Darby declares "sustainable" palm oil while continuing to violate the standards it claims to uphold.

1. **RSPO certification alongside documented violations** — Sime Darby is a member of the Roundtable on Sustainable Palm Oil (RSPO), yet NGOs have repeatedly found that RSPO-certified plantations continued to drain peatlands and violate labour standards. This discredits the certification itself as a marketing tool.
   *Source: Amnesty International; Chain Reaction Research*

2. **"No Deforestation, No Peat, No Exploitation" (NDPE) declaration** — The company signed the NDPE commitment, but independent audits found second-tier suppliers breaching obligations without corrective action being taken. CBP confirmed forced labour even after the NDPE was signed.
   *Source: U.S. CBP WRO (2021)*

3. **Public claims of "responsibly produced" oil despite the U.S. ban** — After receiving the CBP ban, the company continued to position its oil as "responsibly produced" on international markets without informing consumers about the ban.
   *Source: Reuters — Sime Darby CBP ban coverage*


## 4. Does the company participate in ESG marketing?

**Answer: YES**. Sime Darby Plantation actively publishes ESG reports and participates in sustainability ratings.

1. **Annual Sustainability Reports** — The company publishes Sustainability Reports every year, claiming progress on emissions reductions, improved labour conditions, and biodiversity conservation. Reports are prepared in accordance with GRI Standards.
   *Source: Sime Darby Plantation Sustainability Report 2023*

2. **Participation in the Science Based Targets initiative (SBTi)** — The company declared its intention to align emissions targets with the Paris Agreement and uses "net zero" language in corporate communications.
   *Source: Sime Darby Plantation Corporate Website*

3. **Inclusion in ESG ratings** — Sime Darby Plantation is included in the FTSE4Good Index Series, which is actively used in investor relations communications as evidence of ESG maturity.
   *Source: FTSE Russell — FTSE4Good*


## 5. Has the corporation won any global ESG competition nominations?

**Answer: YES**. Sime Darby has received ESG recognition that is at odds with documented violations.

1. **Inclusion in the FTSE4Good Index** — Membership in FTSE4Good, which targets ESG leaders, is positioned by the company as a mark of distinction, despite the simultaneous CBP ban.
   *Source: FTSE Russell*

2. **Malaysian market awards** — The company has received local Malaysian ESG awards (Bursa Malaysia Sustainability Reporting Awards), which are used in PR communications on the domestic market.
   *Source: Bursa Malaysia*


## 6. Has the company applied eco-labels to products with negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. The RSPO certificate on packaging of consumer products containing Sime Darby palm oil effectively functions as an eco-label.

1. **RSPO Certified Sustainable Palm Oil logo** — Food products and cosmetics containing Sime Darby palm oil are labelled with the RSPO mark. At the same time, Amnesty International and CBP documented forced labour on certified plantations — meaning the eco-label was applied to a product manufactured under conditions involving violations.
   *Source: Amnesty International "The Great Palm Oil Scandal"*

2. **"Certified Sustainable" despite NDPE breaches** — In several markets, Sime Darby used the "Certified Sustainable" label in B2B communications with FMCG buyers, while its suppliers were breaching the NDPE commitment.
   *Source: Chain Reaction Research*


## 7. Has the company organised eco-events negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. The company funds tree-planting programmes and orangutan protection initiatives that are negligible compared to the scale of forest destruction.

1. **Orangutan protection programme** — Sime Darby funds a small orangutan rescue programme in Malaysia. Critics point out that the plantation expansion destroying the very habitat of those same orangutans is incomparably larger in scale than the rehabilitation efforts.
   *Source: Chain Reaction Research analysis*

2. **Tree-planting as part of corporate social responsibility** — Annual reports record greening programmes, but the area of trees planted represents a negligible fraction of the peatland forests cleared for plantations.
   *Source: Sime Darby Plantation Sustainability Report*


## 8. Has the company lobbied for pseudo-ecological theories or greenwashing outreach?

**Answer: YES**. Sime Darby actively participates in promoting palm oil as a "sustainable" crop at international negotiation level.

1. **Lobbying against EU restrictions on palm oil** — The Malaysian palm oil industry, including Sime Darby, lobbied against the EU Renewable Energy Directive (RED II), which phased out palm oil biofuels due to environmental harm. Malaysia and Indonesia escalated the dispute to the WTO level.
   *Source: Reuters — EU palm oil biofuel dispute*

2. **Promoting the narrative that "palm is more efficient than soy"** — Industry associations of which Sime Darby is a member (MPOA, MPOC) actively promote the argument that palm produces more oil per hectare than soy or rapeseed and is therefore "more ecological". This narrative conceals the role of peatland carbon and biodiversity.
   *Source: Malaysian Palm Oil Council communications*

3. **Lobbying to weaken RSPO criteria** — Representatives of large plantation companies within the RSPO have systematically blocked stricter certification criteria, in particular a full ban on peatland drainage. As the largest member, Sime Darby exerts influence over this process.
   *Source: Greenpeace — "Certifying Destruction"*
