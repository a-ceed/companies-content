Tesla promotes electric vehicles as an ecologically clean future, but battery production requires lithium and cobalt — the extraction of which causes serious environmental problems.

In the Atacama Desert (Chile), lithium brine extraction depletes underground aquifers in one of the driest places on Earth. Flamingos and other rare species depend on the moisture of Atacama's salt lakes: drying of food sources threatens biodiversity.

The Gigafactory in Nevada caused a major industrial water spill in 2021 that contaminated the Eagle Wings Ranch nature reserve. In 2020 it emerged that the plant was exceeding permissible air emission standards.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. Tesla has been the subject of investigations into injuries and deaths linked to autopilot systems, as well as scandals regarding working conditions at its factories.

1. **Autopilot accidents and fatalities** — The US National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) initiated investigations into more than 900 accidents involving Tesla's Autopilot/FSD system, including 19 deaths as of 2023. In 2022, NHTSA required Tesla to recall 362,758 vehicles with the Full Self-Driving beta due to unsafe behaviour.
   *Source: NHTSA — Tesla Autopilot Investigation PE21-020 (2022)*

2. **Working conditions at Gigafactory Fremont** — Investigations in 2021–2022 (Cal/OSHA and journalistic investigations) found that the Fremont plant has one of the highest injury rates among automakers. Workers reported pressure to conceal injuries, forced overtime, and discrimination.
   *Source: Reveal News — "Tesla's factory of injuries" (2018); Cal/OSHA records*

3. **DRC cobalt and child labour in supply chains** — Amnesty International and the Washington Post documented the use of child labour in cobalt mines in the DRC that supply raw materials for Tesla batteries. Tesla acknowledged that it is impossible to fully exclude DRC cobalt from the supply chain.
   *Source: Amnesty International — "This is what we die for" (2016); Washington Post*

## 2. Has the corporation been the cause of major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. The water spill at Gigafactory Nevada and damage to Texas wetlands are documented incidents.

1. **Industrial water spill at Gigafactory Nevada (2021)** — A significant volume of industrial wastewater containing pollutants leaked from the Gigafactory production building onto the territory of a nature reserve near the plant. The Nevada Department of Environmental Protection recorded the violation.
   *Source: EWG; Nevada NDEP records*

2. **Destruction of Boca Chica wetlands (2023)** — The SpaceX Starship test launch in April 2023 destroyed the launch pad and scattered concrete debris and hot gases over an area including protected wetlands. The FAA found that the prior environmental impact assessment had been conducted inadequately.
   *Source: FAA — Starship Environmental Review; Earthjustice*

3. **Depletion of Atacama aquifers** — Lithium brine extraction in the Atacama Desert, feeding Tesla battery production through suppliers SQM and Albemarle, is leading to declining water levels in lagoons critical for the survival of flamingos and other endemic species.
   *Source: The Guardian — "Chile's lithium fields" (2019)*

## 3. Has the company engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. Tesla positions itself as a fully "green" company, concealing the real carbon footprint of battery production.

1. **"Zero Emissions Vehicle" marketing without lifecycle accounting** — Tesla sells cars with emphasis on "zero emissions while driving", without informing buyers that production of a Model S battery is equivalent to emissions of 8–17 tonnes of CO₂ (data from IVL Swedish Environmental Research Institute studies). The full Tesla lifecycle is better than internal combustion engines, but not "zero".
   *Source: IVL Swedish Environmental Research Institute (2019)*

2. **Selling "Emission Credits" as core business** — Tesla earned $1.78 billion from selling carbon credits to other automakers in 2022. Critics point out that this is a system in which Tesla's "green" image subsidises dirty car manufacturers without reducing total emissions.
   *Source: Tesla Annual Report 2022*

3. **Claims of "eco-friendly" production despite Nevada violations** — Despite documented emission standard violations at Gigafactory Nevada, the company continued to use the claim of operating "the most eco-friendly factory".
   *Source: EWG; Nevada NDEP*

## 4. Does the company engage in ESG marketing?

**Answer: YES**. Tesla publishes Impact Reports and actively uses the ESG narrative in investor relations.

1. **Tesla Impact Report** — The company annually publishes an Impact Report, declaring progress in reducing supply chain emissions, transitioning to renewable energy, and deploying Powerwall/Megapack battery storage.
   *Source: Tesla Impact Report 2023*

2. **Company mission as ESG narrative** — Tesla's publicly declared mission — "to accelerate the world's transition to sustainable energy" — is used as a corporate ESG brand that influences the company's valuation by ESG investors.
   *Source: Tesla Mission Statement; SEC filings*

3. **Inclusion in ESG funds** — Tesla was included in major ESG indices (MSCI ESG, S&P 500 ESG) before being removed from the S&P 500 ESG Index in 2022 for insufficient carbon strategy and labour rights violations.
   *Source: S&P Global — Tesla ESG exclusion (2022)*

---

## 5. Has the corporation won any global ESG awards?

**Answer: NO**. Tesla was excluded from key ESG ratings and has not received significant ESG awards.

1. **Exclusion from the S&P 500 ESG Index (2022)** — S&P Global removed Tesla from the index, citing "lack of a low-carbon strategy", investigations into racial discrimination at the Fremont plant, and autopilot system failures. Elon Musk called ESG "a scam".
   *Source: S&P Global ESG — Tesla exclusion statement (2022)*

2. **Low MSCI ESG scores** — MSCI assigned Tesla a "B" or "BB" rating (below industry average) due to governance issues, working conditions, and product safety.
   *Source: MSCI ESG Ratings — Tesla*

---

## 6. Has the company applied eco-labels to products with negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. The "Zero Emissions" labelling on vehicles misleads consumers about the full lifecycle.

1. **"Zero Emission Vehicle" despite high production emissions** — All Tesla vehicles in the US bear the ZEV (Zero Emission Vehicle) label, despite the fact that battery production creates significant CO₂ emissions. In California this label provides tax credits, effectively subsidising a product with an incomplete "zero" footprint.
   *Source: IVL; California ZEV program*

2. **"Sustainable Energy" on Powerwall despite lithium mining** — Powerwall home batteries are marketed as "clean" and "sustainable" energy, despite the fact that lithium and cobalt extraction for their production causes serious environmental consequences.
   *Source: Tesla Powerwall marketing materials*

---

## 7. Has the company organised eco-events negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. Tesla focuses its "green" initiatives on business products without investing in real environmental restoration.

1. **"Tree planting" in exchange for data** — In 2022 Tesla announced a programme by which users who gave permission for driving data collection would receive a "tree planted". The scale of the initiative is incommensurate with production emissions.
   *Source: Tesla App changelog (2022)*

2. **Small-scale cleanups near factories** — Gigafactory Nevada participates in local litter collection events in the Nevada desert, which is incommensurate with the documented spills and emission standard violations at the same plant.
   *Source: Tesla ESG communications*

---

## 8. Has the company engaged in lobbying for pseudo-ecological theories?

**Answer: YES**. Tesla and Elon Musk actively use the narrative "electric vehicle = climate solution", lobbying for subsidies while simultaneously rejecting other climate measures.

1. **Lobbying for EV subsidies while denying climate science** — In 2023–2024 Tesla lobbied for the preservation of the $7,500 federal tax credit for electric vehicle purchases (IRA), positioning it as "climate policy". At the same time Elon Musk denied the urgency of climate change and publicly supported politicians who oppose climate regulation.
   *Source: Federal lobbying disclosures; The Guardian*

2. **Promoting the thesis "only electric vehicles will save the climate"** — Tesla actively promotes the narrative that electrification of transport is the primary climate priority, pushing public transport, bicycles, and consumption reduction into the background. Critics call this "electric vehicle greenwashing".
   *Source: Transport & Environment analysis*

3. **Opposition to the right to repair** — Tesla lobbied against "Right to Repair" laws that would allow extending the service life of batteries and reduce electronic waste generation. This contradicts the company's declared "green" goals.
   *Source: iFixit; Right to Repair advocacy groups*
