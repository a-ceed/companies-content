In Uganda and Tanzania, TotalEnergies is constructing the world's largest heated oil pipeline (EACOP) stretching 1,443 km. The route passes through national parks, the catchment basins of Lake Victoria — a drinking water source for 40 million people — and the territories of 12,000 families being resettled.

According to the Stop EACOP Campaign, the pipeline threatens 2,000 species of plants and animals, including 352 bird species and 130 mammal species. Several European banks (BNP Paribas, Barclays, Crédit Agricole) declined to finance the project under NGO pressure.

In Nigeria, gas flaring by Total group companies is one of the country's largest sources of greenhouse gases. Despite commitments to end flaring by 2030, volumes of associated gas combustion in the Niger Delta remain high.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. TotalEnergies' operations in Uganda, Nigeria, and Mozambique are accompanied by forced displacement of populations, armed conflicts, and systematic contamination of food and water sources.

1. **EACOP, Uganda — forced resettlement (2021–2024)** — Construction of the EACOP pipeline entailed the forced resettlement of more than 12,000 families from the provinces of Hoima, Kiboga, and others. According to AFIEGO (African Institute for Energy Governance), the resettled lost their livelihoods — agricultural land — without adequate compensation. Complaints from local residents about intimidation and pressure from contractors were documented by Amnesty International.
   *Source: Amnesty International, "Uganda: Human rights defenders face crackdown", 2023; AFIEGO reports; Stop EACOP Campaign*

2. **Mozambique, Cabo Delgado — armed conflict and humanitarian crisis (2019–2023)** — TotalEnergies received a concession to develop the Mozambique LNG gas field in the Cabo Delgado region. UN experts and Human Rights Watch link the escalation of the armed conflict to social discontent caused by oil and gas projects. In March 2021, following an extremist attack on Palma, TotalEnergies evacuated its personnel, abandoning local residents. More than 800,000 people were internally displaced.
   *Source: Human Rights Watch, "Mozambique: Abuses Surge in Gas Project Area", 2021; UNHCR displacement data; Reuters*

3. **Nigeria, Niger Delta — water contamination and disease (1990s–2010s)** — Oil spills and gas flaring at Total E&P Nigeria (TEPNG) facilities contaminated drinking water and air in Delta communities. According to UNEP and Amnesty International, residents suffered from skin diseases, respiratory conditions, and heavy metal poisoning. The Nigerian government repeatedly fined Total for environmental standard violations.
   *Source: UNEP assessment of Ogoniland, 2011; Amnesty International, "Nigeria: Shell and Eni oil spills", 2017; Nigerian NOSDRA reports*

## 2. Has the corporation been the cause of major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. TotalEnergies bears responsibility for long-term pollution of Niger Delta ecosystems, threats to Uganda's unique water bodies, and destruction of Mozambique's coastal ecosystems.

1. **Erika tanker disaster off the French coast (1999)** — The tanker Erika, chartered by TotalFina (TotalEnergies' predecessor), sank in the Bay of Biscay, releasing 20,000 tonnes of heavy fuel oil. The contamination covered 400 km of the Brittany and Vendée coastlines. Approximately 150,000 seabirds died. In 2012 a French court found Total guilty and ordered it to pay €200 million in compensation.
   *Source: French Court of Cassation ruling, 2012; CEDRE (Centre of Documentation, Research and Experimentation on Accidental Water Pollution)*

2. **Gas flaring in the Niger Delta (1970s–2020s)** — Total E&P Nigeria burned associated gas in the Niger Delta for decades. According to the Nigerian Midstream and Downstream Petroleum Regulatory Authority (NMDPRA), Total is one of the country's largest sources of flaring emissions. Acid rain caused by flaring destroys crops and poisons water bodies within a radius of several dozen kilometres.
   *Source: NMDPRA flaring data 2020–2022; Amnesty International Nigerian report, 2018; NOSDRA*

3. **Threat to the Lake Victoria ecosystem through EACOP (2021–2025)** — The EACOP route passes within a few kilometres of Lake Victoria's catchment basins and crosses more than 200 rivers and watercourses. Independent hydrological assessments commissioned by an NGO coalition point to a high risk of contamination in the event of a pipeline rupture, which could affect the drinking water source for 40 million people in three countries.
   *Source: Environmental Resources Management (ERM) ESIA critique, 2021; BankTrack analysis; Friends of the Earth*

## 3. Has the company engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. The name change from Total to TotalEnergies in 2021, large-scale advertising of renewable projects, and simultaneous expansion of oil and gas capacity are signs of systemic greenwashing.

1. **Rebranding to TotalEnergies (2021)** — In May 2021 the company changed its name from "Total" to "TotalEnergies", emphasising the "energy transition". NGOs Reclaim Finance and Global Witness pointed out that of €13 billion in capital investments in 2021, only €2.5 billion was for renewable energy, while oil and gas investments amounted to €10.5 billion. The rebranding was carried out on the eve of the major COP26 climate summit.
   *Source: Global Witness, "Big Oil Reality Check", 2021; Reclaim Finance report; TotalEnergies Annual Report 2021*

2. **"Clean gas" advertising as a climate solution (2018–2022)** — TotalEnergies promoted liquefied natural gas (LNG) as a "bridge to clean energy" and "low-carbon fuel". France's advertising standards authority (ARPP) in 2022 indicated to the company that its environmental claims did not meet accuracy requirements: accounting for the full lifecycle including methane leaks, LNG emissions are comparable to coal.
   *Source: ARPP ruling, 2022; Howarth, 2019, Cornell University; Reclaim Finance*

3. **Greenwashing lawsuit in French courts (2023)** — Several French NGOs (Notre Affaire à Tous, Greenpeace France) filed a lawsuit against TotalEnergies, accusing the company of advertising that misleads consumers about its climate goals. The court found that some advertising claims did not correspond to the company's actual activities.
   *Source: Notre Affaire à Tous press release, 2023; Le Monde, February 2023*

## 4. Does the company engage in ESG marketing?

**Answer: YES**. TotalEnergies actively uses the ESG narrative to attract investors and improve its reputation, without backing it with proportionate changes to its business model.

1. **TotalEnergies Climate and ESG Report (2022–2024)** — The company publishes a separate climate report declaring a "net zero by 2050" goal, including so-called "net zero" commitments. Carbon Tracker established that TotalEnergies' production plans to 2030 envisage growth in oil and gas extraction incompatible with the IEA's 1.5°C trajectory.
   *Source: Carbon Tracker, "Absolute Impact", 2022; IEA Net Zero by 2050, 2021; TotalEnergies Climate Report 2023*

2. **Carbon emission offset programme through reforestation** — TotalEnergies announced large-scale tree-planting projects in Africa as an emissions offset mechanism. Independent auditors (Verra, Gold Standard) expressed doubts about the carbon absorption calculation methodology; several projects were challenged by local communities as land grabs.
   *Source: The Guardian, "Carbon offsetting in Africa", 2023; Verra registry audits; Global Witness*

3. **TCFD and CDP membership** — TotalEnergies discloses climate risks in accordance with TCFD recommendations and reports annually to CDP. InfluenceMap assigned the company a "C-" rating on real climate lobbying, noting the gap between public statements and political positions.
   *Source: InfluenceMap, "TotalEnergies Climate Policy Engagement", 2023; CDP response 2022*

## 5. Has the corporation won any global ESG awards?

**Answer: YES**. TotalEnergies has received industry ESG recognition used in corporate communications.

1. **Inclusion in the Euronext Vigeo Eiris — Eurozone 120 Index (2019–2022)** — TotalEnergies featured in the Vigeo Eiris sustainability index among European corporations, which was used in annual reports as evidence of ESG leadership. Critics noted that Vigeo's methodology does not account for human rights violations during EACOP construction.
   *Source: Vigeo Eiris Eurozone 120 index methodology; TotalEnergies Annual Report 2021*

2. **CDP "Climate Transparency Leader" award (2020)** — CDP assigned TotalEnergies an "A-" score for climate data disclosure — a high score in the oil and gas sector. Environmental organisations pointed out that CDP evaluates transparency, not actual emissions reductions.
   *Source: CDP Climate Change Report 2020; InfluenceMap critique*

3. **Inclusion in MSCI ESG Leaders Index (2021)** — TotalEnergies was included in the MSCI ESG Leaders Index used by a number of "responsible" investment funds. Following publication of reports on EACOP and human rights violations, several funds froze their positions in the company.
   *Source: MSCI ESG Leaders Index 2021; BankTrack investor analysis*

## 6. Has the company applied eco-labels to products with negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. TotalEnergies uses environmental labelling on fuels and lubricants without disclosing the real carbon footprint of its products.

1. **Total Excellium fuel with "clean technology" branding** — Total sells premium Excellium fuel with advertising about "cleaner combustion" and reduced harmful emissions. Independent research (Transport & Environment, Brussels) showed that the difference in CO₂ emissions at the lifecycle level is negligible, while the advertising creates a false sense of environmental choice among consumers.
   *Source: Transport & Environment, EU fuel quality analysis, 2021; Total Excellium product specifications*

2. **Total Quartz motor oils labelled "ECO"** — The Quartz product line is marked with an "Eco" designation and leaf imagery on the label. The UK Advertising Standards Authority (ASA) in 2020 issued guidelines to oil companies to refrain from green symbols on petroleum products without clear justification of environmental benefits.
   *Source: ASA rulings database, 2020; Total Quartz product labelling*

3. **LNG fuel marketed as "low-carbon" for shipping (2019–2022)** — TotalEnergies actively positioned LNG for maritime transport with labels of "low-carbon solution" and "cleaner than coal". Research by the International Council on Clean Transportation (ICCT) showed that accounting for methane leaks during extraction and transport, LNG provides no climate advantage over conventional marine fuel.
   *Source: ICCT, "Climate implications of using LNG as a marine fuel", 2020; TotalEnergies LNG marketing materials*

## 7. Has the company organised eco-events negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. TotalEnergies funds local environmental actions incommensurate with the scale of damage it causes.

1. **"Biodiversity" programme near the Donges refinery (France)** — Total organised hedge planting and creation of "ecological corridors" on several hectares near the Donges oil refinery in Loire-Atlantique. Local environmental groups pointed out that the refinery annually emits tens of thousands of tonnes of pollutants, making the "bio-corridor" a PR symbol.
   *Source: TotalEnergies France biodiversity programme report, 2020; "La Mèche" magazine, investigation 2021*

2. **Coastal cleanup in partnership with Surfrider Foundation (2019–2022)** — TotalEnergies sponsored beach cleanups in partnership with Surfrider Foundation in Europe. Environmental critics noted that the company is one of the largest sources of ocean plastic contamination through petrochemical production, while symbolic beach cleanups create a positive image without systemic change.
   *Source: Surfrider Foundation partnership press release, 2021; Plastic Atlas 2022*

3. **Educational programme on the "energy of the future" for schoolchildren (2015–2023)** — TotalEnergies implements a programme of educational visits to company facilities and interactive lessons about the "energy transition" for schoolchildren in Europe and Africa. Educational researchers in France (ATTAC Education) pointed out that the programme materials promote natural gas as a "bridge to sustainable energy".
   *Source: TotalEnergies "Energy for Generations" programme; ATTAC critique, 2022*

## 8. Has the company engaged in lobbying for pseudo-ecological theories?

**Answer: YES**. TotalEnergies systematically lobbies against tightening climate regulation in the EU and promotes the concept of natural gas as a "climate solution" that contradicts scientific consensus.

1. **Lobbying against EU methane restrictions (2019–2021)** — According to data from the EU Transparency Register and Corporate Europe Observatory investigations, TotalEnergies sent lobbying materials to the European Commission against tightening methane emission standards for the oil and gas industry. The company sought the broadest possible application of the principle of "technological neutrality".
   *Source: Corporate Europe Observatory, "Gas Lobby", 2021; EU Transparency Register data*

2. **Funding lobbying organisations opposing EU climate law** — TotalEnergies is a member of FuelsEurope and Eurogas — organisations that actively lobbied against including natural gas in the EU taxonomy of "sustainable investments" under stricter conditions. InfluenceMap recorded a divergence between the company's public climate position and the positions of its industry associations.
   *Source: InfluenceMap, TotalEnergies profile, 2023; FuelsEurope lobby disclosures*

3. **"Environmental education" through corporate media and podcasts** — TotalEnergies operates a multilingual media hub "Total.com/the-energy-collective" with publications on "the role of gas in the energy transition" and "sustainable petrochemicals". Independent media analysts (DeSmog) characterised the content as corporate propaganda aimed at forming a favourable public perception of fossil fuels.
   *Source: DeSmog, TotalEnergies media analysis, 2022; TotalEnergies Energy Collective website*
