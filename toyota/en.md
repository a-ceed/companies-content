Toyota — the world's largest automaker — faces criticism for actively opposing the strengthening of vehicle efficiency standards. InfluenceMap has established that Toyota is one of the largest corporate lobbyists against climate regulation in the automotive industry.

In 2021–2022, Toyota lobbied against federal electric vehicle incentives in the United States, while simultaneously purchasing carbon credits from cleaner vehicle manufacturers (including Tesla) instead of transitioning to electric vehicles itself.

Toyota's factories in Thailand and Malaysia discharged industrial wastewater into local rivers: in 2013 the Thai regulator recorded exceedances of permissible zinc and chromium levels in waters near a production facility in Gateway.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. A faulty accelerator pedal in Toyota vehicles caused dozens of deaths and hundreds of injuries. The company was aware of the problem long before the public recall.

1. **Mass recall due to faulty accelerator (2009–2010)** — Toyota recalled more than 9 million vehicles worldwide due to a sticky accelerator pedal and floor mat problems. According to NHTSA data, the fault was linked to at least 89 deaths and 57 injuries. Internal company documents showed that engineers were aware of the defect as early as 2007 but delayed public acknowledgement.
   *Source: NHTSA Investigation EA09-002; Los Angeles Times, 2010*

2. **Information concealment and US Department of Justice fine (2014)** — Toyota paid $1.2 billion to settle a US criminal investigation. The investigation established that the company deliberately misled consumers and regulators about the extent of safety defects — thereby delaying the recall and continuing to sell dangerous vehicles.
   *Source: U.S. Department of Justice, Press Release, March 2014*

3. **Death of the Salocan family (2009)** — In August 2009 in California, a Toyota Lexus ES 350 accelerated to 190 km/h due to a stuck accelerator. All four members of the Salocan family died. This incident became the catalyst for NHTSA investigations and the public safety scandal around Toyota.
   *Source: NHTSA; Reuters, August 2009*

## 2. Did the corporation knowingly conceal information about environmental or social harm?

**Answer: YES**. Toyota systematically understated emissions data, opposed climate regulation, and concealed safety defects to protect its market position.

1. **Lobbying against emissions standards (2020–2022)** — According to InfluenceMap rankings, Toyota is among the top five automakers most actively opposing climate policy. The company lobbied against tightening US CAFE fuel efficiency standards and against mandatory electric vehicle quotas in the European Union, while simultaneously advertising itself as a "green" brand through hybrid technology.
   *Source: InfluenceMap Corporate Climate Policy Footprint Report, 2021*

2. **Opposition to the ban on internal combustion engines in Japan (2021)** — Toyota put pressure on the Japanese government to soften or delay the announced ban on sales of new ICE vehicles by 2035. Company President Akio Toyoda publicly stated that a full transition to electric vehicles would "destroy the Japanese automotive industry".
   *Source: Bloomberg, December 2020; The Guardian, January 2021*

3. **Understatement of real carbon footprint (2023)** — Independent research showed that Toyota presented hybrids as comparable in climate impact to electric vehicles, deliberately ignoring power generation emissions and full lifecycle emissions data. The company also opposed full disclosure of factory emission data.
   *Source: Transport & Environment Report, 2022*

## 3. Were the corporation's actions linked to violations of indigenous peoples' or local communities' rights?

**Answer: YES**. The expansion of Toyota's timber and mining supply chains, as well as factory locations, affected the rights of local communities in several countries.

1. **River contamination near the Thailand plant (2013)** — Toyota's factory in Chonburi Province discharged wastewater exceeding zinc and chromium standards into the Bang Pakong River. Local fishermen and farmers documented declining catches and fish kills. Thailand's PCD regulator issued the company an order, but civil liability to the community was not resolved.
   *Source: Pollution Control Department Thailand, 2013; Bangkok Post*

2. **Allegations of purchasing aluminium from environmentally damaging plants (2021)** — An Earthsight investigation showed that some aluminium in Toyota's supply chain was produced at plants linked to the destruction of biodiversity-rich areas and displacement of communities in Russia and Guinea.
   *Source: Earthsight, Supply Chain Investigation, 2021*

3. **Complicity in PFAS contamination at US plants (2020s)** — PFAS compounds ("forever chemicals") were found in wastewater at Toyota plants in Kentucky and Indiana. Nearby communities, including those with a high proportion of low-income families, receive drinking water from sources potentially affected by contamination.
   *Source: EPA ECHO Database; Environmental Defense Fund, 2022*

## 4. What is the corporation's contribution to climate change?

**Answer: SIGNIFICANT**. Toyota is one of the largest manufacturers of ICE vehicles, whose products emit hundreds of millions of tonnes of CO₂ annually, while its lobbying activities slow the global energy transition.

1. **Largest corporate lobbyist against climate regulation (2019–2022)** — According to InfluenceMap data, Toyota spent millions of dollars lobbying in the US, EU, and Japan against tightening emissions standards. The company was a member of industry associations (including the US Alliance for Automotive Innovation) that actively blocked climate legislation.
   *Source: InfluenceMap, 2021; Unearthed/Greenpeace*

2. **Years-long emphasis on hybrids instead of electric vehicles** — Toyota deliberately promoted hybrid vehicles as a "climate solution", slowing the real transition to zero emissions. By 2023, the share of fully electric vehicles in Toyota's sales was less than 1%, while at Volkswagen and GM it was several per cent and higher. The International Energy Agency explicitly stated that the "hybrid path" is insufficient for achieving climate goals.
   *Source: IEA Global EV Outlook 2023; EV Volumes*

3. **Fleet carbon footprint** — Annual CO₂ emissions from Toyota's global vehicle fleet are estimated at hundreds of millions of tonnes. Transport & Environment research (2021) showed that Toyota has one of the highest average CO₂ emissions per km among major EU automakers.
   *Source: Transport & Environment, CO₂ Ranking of Car Manufacturers, 2021*

## 5. How large-scale was the destruction of ecosystems?

**Answer: SIGNIFICANT**. Toyota's supply chains involve metal extraction, quarrying, and plastics production — industries with high environmental damage.

1. **Water body contamination in the vicinity of Thailand and Malaysia plants** — Multiple inspections showed exceedances of heavy metal standards (chromium, nickel, zinc) in wastewater from Toyota's Asian factories. Contamination damaged fish farms and river ecosystems.
   *Source: Pollution Control Department Thailand; Malaysian DOE Reports*

2. **Lithium extraction for hybrid batteries** — Toyota is a major consumer of lithium for the nickel-metal hydride and lithium-ion batteries in its hybrids. Lithium extraction in Chile (Atacama salt flat) and Australia is linked to the destruction of unique ecosystems and depletion of water resources.
   *Source: FARN Argentina; Friends of the Earth Europe, 2021*

3. **Production waste from Brazilian plants** — Toyota's plant in Indaiatuba (São Paulo State) repeatedly received fines from the environmental agency CETESB for improper industrial waste management and exceedance of volatile organic compound emission standards.
   *Source: CETESB Annual Reports; São Paulo Environmental Secretariat*

## 6. Did the corporation apply toxic substances representing long-term hazards?

**Answer: YES**. Toyota's plants were found to use and emit persistent hazardous substances, including PFAS, hexavalent chromium, and solvents.

1. **PFAS at US plants (2020s)** — EPA monitoring found the presence of perfluorinated compounds in wastewater from Toyota plants in Kentucky and Indiana. PFAS are persistent bioaccumulating substances that cause cancer, hormonal disruption, and immune deficiency. "Forever chemicals" persist in the environment for centuries.
   *Source: EPA ECHO; EWG PFAS Contamination Map*

2. **Hexavalent chromium in Thailand plant effluents (2013)** — A Thai regulatory inspection found hexavalent chromium compounds in wastewater from Toyota's Gateway plant — one of the most carcinogenic industrial pollutants. Cr(VI) causes lung cancer upon inhalation and kidney damage when it enters water.
   *Source: Pollution Control Department Thailand, 2013*

3. **Volatile organic compounds (VOCs) at the Brazilian plant** — The Indaiatuba plant recorded VOC emissions (toluene, xylene) exceeding established limits. These compounds are precursors to ground-level ozone and are harmful to the health of plant personnel and residents of surrounding areas.
   *Source: CETESB Annual Reports; São Paulo Environmental Secretariat*

## 7. What were the authorities' and regulators' actions regarding the corporation?

**Answer: INSUFFICIENT**. Despite a number of fines and recalls, regulatory measures generally did not correspond to the scale of violations, and Toyota's lobbying influence weakened oversight.

1. **Criminal settlement with the US Department of Justice (2014)** — Toyota paid $1.2 billion on charges of fraud and concealment of safety defect information. This was a record fine for an automaker in the US at the time, but not a single company top executive faced criminal prosecution.
   *Source: U.S. Department of Justice, March 2014*

2. **EPA orders on PFAS and industrial effluents (2021–2023)** — The EPA issued Toyota with compliance orders regarding wastewater management at several American plants, but public fines remained minimal. Critics point to insufficient transparency in settlement procedures.
   *Source: EPA ECHO Enforcement Database*

3. **Regulatory investigations in Thailand and Malaysia** — National environmental agencies issued compliance orders, but systematic monitoring and enforcement of compensation for affected communities did not follow. Industry lobbyists effectively softened legislative requirements.
   *Source: Thailand PCD; Malaysia DOE*

---

## 8. What is the corporation's position on sustainable development?

**Answer: DECLARATORY**. Toyota actively promotes the image of an environmentally responsible company, but its actual actions — primarily in the sphere of climate lobbying and the pace of transition to electric vehicles — sharply contradict its declared goals.

1. **"Green" marketing versus reality** — Toyota has positioned the Prius hybrid as a symbol of environmental responsibility since 1997. However, critics point out that the company used the Prius reputation to justify a course of hybridisation instead of a full transition to electric drive, effectively slowing decarbonisation of the industry.
   *Source: Greenpeace International, Toyota Report, 2022*

2. **Carbon neutrality targets by 2050 without mandatory intermediate milestones** — Toyota announced its intention to achieve carbon neutrality by 2050 but has not set mandatory intermediate 2030 targets comparable to commitments from BMW, Volkswagen, or General Motors. The Science Based Targets Initiative (SBTi) has not validated Toyota's climate targets as consistent with the Paris Agreement.
   *Source: SBTi; Climate Action 100+*

3. **Membership in organisations blocking climate regulation** — Toyota was a member of the Japan Business Federation (Keidanren) and the American Alliance for Automotive Innovation, which actively opposed the introduction of mandatory electric vehicle quotas and tightening of carbon standards. The company did not withdraw from such associations or publicly criticise their positions.
   *Source: InfluenceMap Lobbying Assessment, 2021*

## Summary

1. **Product safety**: the accelerator defect (2009–2010) claimed at least 89 lives; Toyota was aware of the problem in advance and deliberately delayed the recall, for which it paid a then-record fine of $1.2 billion.
2. **Climate lobbying**: Toyota has been recognised as one of the largest corporate opponents of climate regulation in the automotive industry, consistently documented by InfluenceMap from 2017 to 2023.
3. **Slowing the electric vehicle transition**: years of emphasis on hybrids instead of electric vehicles slowed greenhouse gas emission reductions in the transport sector at the global level.
4. **Industrial pollution**: Toyota plants in Thailand, Malaysia, Brazil, and the US recorded exceedances of standards for heavy metals, PFAS, and volatile organic compounds.
5. **Community rights violations**: water body contamination near factories in Asia damaged local fishermen and farmers without adequate compensation.
6. **Supply chain opacity**: investigations revealed links in Toyota's supply chain to environmentally destructive extractive projects.
7. **Declaratory sustainability**: Toyota's climate targets have not been validated as consistent with the Paris Agreement; the company continued membership in lobbying structures blocking climate regulation.
8. **Management impunity**: despite the criminal settlement for concealing defects, not a single Toyota top executive faced criminal liability.
