title: TSMC
brands: N/A (контрактный производитель)
crime: Enormous consumption of water and toxic chemicals at the world's largest chip fabrication plants.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. TSMC workers are exposed to toxic chemicals, and Taiwan's water crisis has struck farming communities.

1. **Occupational diseases among workers** — Investigations by Taiwanese labour NGOs identified cases of occupational illness among semiconductor industry workers, including TSMC employees: reproductive disorders in women working with photoresists and solvents. Similar issues have been documented in the industry in the USA and South Korea.
   *Source: COSH (Coalition on Occupational Safety and Health); Taiwan Labor Research Association*

2. **Water crisis: farmers vs. factory (2021)** — During Taiwan's record drought of 2021, the government halted irrigation on 17,000 hectares of rice paddies to secure water for TSMC's plants. Farmers lost their harvests and livelihoods; acute public controversy arose over the allocation of water resources between industry and agriculture.
   *Source: Reuters — "Taiwan drought" (2021); Nikkei Asia*

3. **PFC emissions: long-term climate harm** — TSMC is one of the largest industrial sources of perfluorocarbons (PFCs) — greenhouse gases with a global warming potential thousands of times higher than CO₂. The long-term climate impact of PFCs poses indirect harm to all of humanity.
   *Source: Semiconductor Climate Consortium; TSMC ESG Report*


## 2. Has the corporation been responsible for major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. Chemical accidents at Taiwanese facilities and systemic contamination of water and air are documented problems.

1. **Exceedance of VOC emission limits at the Hsinchu plant** — Taiwan's Environmental Protection Agency (EPA Taiwan) has repeatedly recorded exceedances of volatile organic compound (VOC) standards at TSMC's Fab plants in Hsinchu, resulting in administrative fines.
   *Source: Taiwan EPA enforcement records*

2. **Emergency chemical releases** — Throughout TSMC's history, incidents involving accidental releases of toxic chemicals (solvents, acids) inside production buildings have been recorded, causing acute poisoning of workers and localised contamination.
   *Source: Taiwan Ministry of Labor accident records*

3. **Massive water consumption as a systemic problem** — Each TSMC plant consumes 150,000–200,000 tonnes of water per day. The construction of a new cluster in Tainan is intensifying pressure on southern Taiwan's water supply, where water resources are already limited due to climate change.
   *Source: Nikkei Asia — TSMC water consumption*


## 3. Has the company engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. TSMC declares ambitious climate targets while remaining one of the largest industrial sources of PFCs.

1. **"RE100" with minimal renewable energy share** — TSMC joined the RE100 initiative (100% renewable electricity), yet in Taiwan renewable energy accounts for less than 10% of the energy mix. The company purchases green certificates (RECs) that do not change the actual generation structure.
   *Source: RE100 Progress Reports; Taiwan Bureau of Energy*

2. **"Net zero by 2050" target without a PFC plan** — TSMC announced a goal of net-zero emissions by 2050, yet has not publicly disclosed a detailed plan to eliminate PFC emissions — the most potent greenhouse gases in chip manufacturing.
   *Source: TSMC Sustainability Report 2022*

3. **"Water stewardship" while depleting aquifers** — The company uses the term "water stewardship" in ESG communications, despite documented pressure from TSMC on Taiwan's water resources.
   *Source: CDP Water Security questionnaire — TSMC responses*


## 4. Does the company participate in ESG marketing?

**Answer: YES**. TSMC actively publishes ESG reports and participates in international climate initiatives.

1. **Annual sustainability reports** — TSMC publishes detailed Sustainability Reports according to GRI and SASB standards, including data on emissions intensity, water consumption and worker programmes.
   *Source: TSMC Sustainability Report 2023*

2. **Participation in Science Based Targets (SBTi)** — TSMC had its climate targets verified through SBTi, positioning itself as a climate-responsible manufacturer for clients such as Apple, Nvidia and AMD.
   *Source: SBTi Corporate Action*

3. **Semiconductor Climate Consortium** — TSMC is a founding member of the Semiconductor Climate Consortium — an industry decarbonisation initiative that it actively uses in its ESG communications.
   *Source: Semiconductor Climate Consortium*


## 5. Has the corporation won any global ESG competition nominations?

**Answer: YES**. TSMC regularly receives ESG recognition.

1. **Inclusion in the Dow Jones Sustainability Index (DJSI)** — TSMC is included annually in the DJSI World and DJSI Asia Pacific as one of the sustainability leaders in the technology sector.
   *Source: S&P Global — DJSI 2023*

2. **CDP A-List** — TSMC has received an "A" rating from CDP (Carbon Disclosure Project) multiple times in the Climate Change category, which is the highest carbon transparency score.
   *Source: CDP Scores — TSMC*


## 6. Has the company applied eco-labels to products with negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. TSMC chips feature in "green" products, even though their manufacture is extremely resource-intensive.

1. **"Green" chips for electric vehicles and renewable energy** — TSMC chips are used in Tesla vehicles, solar inverters and other "green" technology. Manufacturers of the final products label them as "environmentally friendly" while remaining silent about the water and chemical footprint of chip production.
   *Source: Supply chain analysis; Apple Environmental Progress Reports*

2. **"Energy-efficient" chip labelling** — Chips built on TSMC's advanced process nodes are labelled as "energy-efficient" and as "reducing the carbon footprint of end devices", without accounting for the energy embedded in their production (embedded carbon).
   *Source: IEEE Spectrum — embedded carbon in semiconductors*


## 7. Has the company organised eco-events negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. TSMC's environmental programmes are negligible compared to its systemic resource consumption.

1. **Water recycling programme** — TSMC claims 85%+ recycling of process water within manufacturing, yet the absolute volumes of water consumption continue to grow as production expands.
   *Source: TSMC Sustainability Report*

2. **Tree planting and environmental education** — The company funds greening and environmental education programmes in Taiwan and near its plants in Arizona. The scale of these programmes is incomparable to the ecological footprint of constructing the factory clusters.
   *Source: TSMC Community Programs*


## 8. Has the company lobbied for pseudo-ecological theories or greenwashing outreach?

**Answer: YES**. TSMC and the semiconductor industry lobby for exemptions from environmental requirements, positioning chips as a "climate solution".

1. **Lobbying for exemptions from EU PFAS Regulation** — The semiconductor industry (including TSMC's interests through its clients — Intel, ASML) actively lobbied for exemptions for chip manufacturing from the EU-wide PFAS (forever chemicals) regulation, arguing that no substitutes exist. This allows PFC emissions to continue.
   *Source: ECHA PFAS restriction dossier; industry comments*

2. **Narrative that "chips are a climate solution"** — TSMC and industry associations promote the thesis that semiconductors are a key instrument in decarbonising the economy (smart grids, electric vehicles, AI for climate modelling), pushing the discussion of the chip industry's own environmental footprint to the background.
   *Source: Semiconductor Industry Association policy papers*
