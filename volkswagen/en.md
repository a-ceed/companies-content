In September 2015, the US EPA discovered that Volkswagen had installed special software in 11 million diesel vehicles worldwide. The programme detected laboratory test conditions and switched the engine to a reduced-emission mode; under real driving conditions the vehicles emitted up to 40 times more nitrogen oxides (NOx) than permitted by EU and US standards.

NOx is one of the primary sources of urban smog and PM2.5 fine particles linked to premature death from lung and heart disease. According to estimates by MIT and Harvard University researchers, emissions from VW "diesel" vehicles in the United States caused 59 premature deaths and hundreds of thousands of asthma exacerbations.

Volkswagen's total payments exceeded $35 billion: fines in the US, vehicle buybacks, and compensation for dealers and buyers. Company top executives were convicted in Germany and the United States. The scandal accelerated the European automotive industry's transition to electric vehicles.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. Above-standard NOx emissions from 11 million VW vehicles over at least 6 years (2009–2015) led to elevated smog levels in cities worldwide, causing premature deaths and serious respiratory diseases.

1. **Premature mortality in the USA (2009–2015)** — A study by MIT and Harvard (2017) showed that excess NOx emissions from VW vehicles in the US caused at least 59 premature deaths, with approximately 100 more expected in future from the consequences of prolonged exposure. Public health damage was estimated at $450 million.
   *Source: Barrett et al., MIT / Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health, 2017*

2. **Scale of air contamination in Europe (2009–2015)** — According to the European Environment Agency (EEA), additional NOx emissions from diesel VW vehicles in EU countries annually provoked tens of thousands of cases of asthma exacerbation and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, especially among children and the elderly.
   *Source: EEA Report No 28/2016; The Guardian, 23.09.2015*

3. **Criminal conviction of top executive (2017)** — Martin Winterkorn (former VW CEO) was charged by the US Department of Justice with fraud and conspiracy. Former Vice-President James Liang pleaded guilty and received 40 months in prison — the court characterised the scheme as deliberate deception that caused real harm to public health.
   *Source: US Department of Justice, press release, 09.01.2017*

## 2. Has the corporation been the cause of major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. Prolonged mass NOx and PM2.5 particle air contamination represents a dispersed but systemic environmental catastrophe that affected the urban environment on three continents.

1. **Dieselgate: global air contamination (2009–2015)** — 11 million vehicles in 50 countries daily emitted NOx many times above the standard. In cumulative terms, this amounts to hundreds of thousands of additional tonnes of nitrogen oxides over years of operation.
   *Source: EPA Notice of Violation, 18.09.2015; ICCT Analysis, 2015*

2. **Plant contamination in Germany and Mexico** — VW production facilities in Wolfsburg repeatedly featured in reports by German environmental authorities for exceedances of volatile organic compound and wastewater emission standards; the Puebla plant (Mexico) was included in the 2000s in the register of the country's largest industrial polluters.
   *Source: Umweltbundesamt (UBA) Germany, 2016; SEMARNAT Mexico, 2012*

3. **Chronic groundwater contamination in Brazil** — The VW plant in São Bernardo do Campo (São Paulo State) used chlorinated solvents for car body degreasing. In 2019 Brazilian oversight bodies recorded contamination of underground aquifers with trichloroethylene (TCE) and tetrachloroethylene, both recognised carcinogens.
   *Source: Ministério Público do Estado de São Paulo, 2019; Reuters Brasil, 04.11.2019*

## 3. Has the company engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. For more than a decade, VW actively advertised diesel vehicles as "clean" and "green", knowing that actual emissions were many times higher than the standards.

1. **"Clean Diesel" campaign (2008–2015)** — VW spent hundreds of millions of dollars on advertising in the US, positioning diesel Jetta, Passat, and Golf models as an "eco-friendly alternative" to hybrids. The "Das Auto" slogan was combined with claims of "ultra-low emissions" — a direct lie established in court.
   *Source: FTC Complaint against Volkswagen, 2016; AdAge, 22.09.2015*

2. **Environmental awards based on falsified data** — Several VW "clean diesel" models received environmental awards from American publications and organisations, including "Green Car of the Year" (2009 — Jetta TDI). Following the exposé, these awards were publicly rescinded.
   *Source: Green Car Journal, statement 22.09.2015; Car and Driver, 2015*

3. **Post-Dieselgate rebranding as "Voltswagen" (2021)** — On the eve of Earth Day, VW launched a PR stunt about renaming itself "Voltswagen" ostensibly to emphasise the transition to electric vehicles. The stunt turned out to be a hoax that misled investors and media, aimed solely at creating an eco-friendly company image.
   *Source: Reuters, 29.03.2021; SEC investigation notice, April 2021*

## 4. Does the company engage in ESG marketing?

**Answer: YES**. After Dieselgate, VW has actively built an ESG narrative, publishing large-scale sustainability reports and announcing "green" investments of trillions of euros.

1. **"Way to Zero" programme (from 2020)** — VW Group announced a goal to achieve carbon neutrality by 2050 and invest €89 billion in electric vehicles. The company's ESG reports are regularly cited in DJSI and MSCI ESG indices, despite continuing Dieselgate litigation.
   *Source: VW Group Sustainability Report 2022; Dow Jones Sustainability Index*

2. **VW ESG bonds (2020–2023)** — Volkswagen Financial Services issued "green" and "sustainable" bonds worth several billion euros to finance electric vehicle production, attracting institutional ESG investors. Critics point out that the volume of actual ICE production still greatly exceeds the electric vehicle volume.
   *Source: Bloomberg Green, 14.09.2021; Financial Times, 2022*

3. **UN Race to Zero membership** — VW joined the UN Race to Zero and other climate initiatives, while simultaneously lobbying in the EU to extend deadlines for the internal combustion engine ban and seeking exemptions for "e-fuel" vehicles.
   *Source: Transport & Environment, report "Who's really racing to zero?", 2023*

## 5. Has the corporation won any global ESG awards?

**Answer: YES**. Despite the largest corporate environmental fraud in history, VW received high ESG ratings and industry awards.

1. **Dow Jones Sustainability Index (DJSI), 2022–2023** — Volkswagen Group was included in DJSI World and DJSI Europe as a "sustainability leader" in the automaker sector. Rating agency SAM/S&P Global assigned VW high scores for "environmental management", despite unresolved lawsuits.
   *Source: S&P Global CSA, September 2023*

2. **EcoVadis Gold rating (2021)** — VW Group divisions received an EcoVadis gold rating in the "sustainable supply chain management" category. This was used in tender documentation for public procurement as evidence of environmental responsibility.
   *Source: EcoVadis Scorecard, VW Group, 2021*

3. **"Best ESG Automaker" according to Sustainalytics (2022)** — Sustainalytics assigned VW a "low ESG risk" rating in the automaker category, which allowed a number of ESG funds to include VW shares in "green" portfolios.
   *Source: Sustainalytics ESG Risk Rating Report, 2022*

## 6. Has the company applied eco-labels to products with negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. VW placed environmental labels and certificates on vehicles whose actual emissions many times exceeded the declared levels.

1. **"Euro 5 / Euro 6 compliant" labelling on diesel vehicles (2009–2015)** — Certificates of compliance with European environmental standards Euro 5 and Euro 6 were placed on vehicles whose actual emissions under real conditions were 10–40 times above the permissible values of those same standards.
   *Source: ICCT "From laboratory to road", 2014; EPA Notice of Violation 2015*

2. **German "Blaue Engel" and similar EU labels** — Several VW diesel models were submitted for the German environmental label "Blue Angel" (Blaue Engel) and similar national labels, citing "certified" emission figures that had been obtained from falsified tests.
   *Source: Der Spiegel, "Der falsche Engel", October 2015*

3. **EPA "SmartWay" labelling** — In the United States, several TDI (TDI Clean Diesel) models received EPA SmartWay status — a programme for low-emission vehicles. After the scandal, the EPA revoked these certificates, acknowledging that the labels had been assigned on the basis of knowingly unreliable data.
   *Source: EPA SmartWay, withdrawal notice, October 2015*

## 7. Has the company organised eco-events negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. Against the backdrop of years of air pollution, VW funded targeted environmental actions incommensurate in scale with the damage caused.

1. **VW "Forest Initiatives" in Germany (2019–2022)** — After Dieselgate, VW announced tree-planting in partnership with German forest organisations. Press releases reported thousands of seedlings in Lower Saxony — a negligible measure against the backdrop of hundreds of thousands of tonnes of NOx emitted by the company's vehicles.
   *Source: VW Newsroom, 22.04.2021; Greenpeace Germany, response press release 2021*

2. **Sponsorship of environmental exhibitions at auto shows (2017–2020)** — VW funded "green" pavilions at Frankfurt IAA and other auto shows featuring electric vehicle concepts and "sustainable materials", while actual sales were dominated by ICE vehicles. Climate protection organisations characterised the events as pure PR.
   *Source: Transport & Environment, IAA press release 2019*

3. **Carbon footprint offset programme for employees (2021)** — VW launched an internal programme by which employees could "offset" their personal carbon footprint through the purchase of corporate offset certificates. Climate NGOs pointed out that the programme diverts attention from systemic emissions and is a PR exercise.
   *Source: Süddeutsche Zeitung, 12.06.2021*

## 8. Has the company engaged in lobbying for pseudo-ecological theories?

**Answer: YES**. VW funded lobbying against strict emissions standards and promoted the thesis that "clean diesel" is an environmentally safe alternative — knowingly false in light of the company's own data.

1. **Lobbying against the Euro 6 standard in the EU (2012–2014)** — Volkswagen, through the European Automobile Manufacturers' Association (ACEA), actively lobbied for a softening of real-world driving emissions (RDE) tests, seeking the broadest possible "conformity factor" for NOx. Internal documents disclosed during German investigations show this was done to protect the deception technology.
   *Source: Der Spiegel, "Dieselgate-Lobby", 2017; Transport & Environment, 2018*

2. **Funding the EUGT laboratory (2014–2017)** — Together with BMW and Daimler, VW funded the European Research Group on Environment and Health in the Transport Sector (EUGT), which conducted experiments on monkeys and humans to demonstrate the "harmlessness" of diesel exhaust. The organisation was dissolved after the scandal was exposed in 2018.
   *Source: New York Times, 29.01.2018; Spiegel Online, 30.01.2018*

3. **"Diesel vs. hybrid" campaign** — VW funded analytical publications and "independent" studies claiming that diesel vehicles are better than hybrids in terms of total carbon footprint (including battery production). These claims were widely circulated in automotive media and used to oppose electrification.
   *Source: InfluenceMap Database, Volkswagen profile, 2020*

2. **Major environmental disasters?** — **YES** — Systemic urban air NOx contamination on three continents (2009–2015); groundwater contamination with chlorinated solvents in Brazil (2019); industrial emissions from plants in Germany and Mexico.
3. **Greenwashing?** — **YES** — Years-long "Clean Diesel" campaign in the US; false environmental awards; "Voltswagen" PR stunt on the eve of Earth Day (2021).
4. **ESG marketing?** — **YES** — "Way to Zero" programme; issuance of "green" bonds worth billions of euros; membership in UN climate initiatives while lobbying to extend the ICE ban deadline.
5. **ESG award wins?** — **YES** — Inclusion in DJSI World and DJSI Europe (2022–2023); EcoVadis Gold rating (2021); "low ESG risk" from Sustainalytics (2022).
6. **Eco-labels on harmful products?** — **YES** — Euro 5/6 labelling on vehicles with understated emissions; false EPA SmartWay certification; "Blue Angel" claims.
7. **Negligible eco-events?** — **YES** — Tree-planting in Lower Saxony; sponsorship of "green" pavilions at auto shows; corporate carbon offset programme for employees.
8. **Lobbying for pseudo-ecological theories?** — **YES** — Lobbying against EU RDE tests; funding the EUGT with monkey experiments to "prove" diesel harmlessness; "diesel is better than hybrid" campaign.
