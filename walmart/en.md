Walmart — the world's largest retailer — produces billions of units of plastic packaging annually. According to Break Free From Plastic, in 2021 Walmart ranked in the top 10 corporations whose branded plastic packaging is most frequently found on shorelines and in water bodies worldwide.

Despite declared "green" goals, the company continues to source goods from suppliers practising tropical deforestation. In 2019, a Mighty Earth investigation found that Walmart's soy and palm oil supply chains are linked to the destruction of the Cerrado biome in Brazil.

In 2023, Walmart paid a $7.5 million fine in California for illegal disposal of hazardous waste — household chemicals, pesticides, and electronic scrap — in ordinary landfills. The company is also regularly criticised for creating "waste deserts" in low-income areas where the density of its stores correlates with increased waste generation.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. Walmart systematically violated workers' rights, suppressed trade union activity, created dangerous working conditions in its stores and throughout the supply chain. The Rana Plaza tragedy exposed the consequences of Walmart's pressure on suppliers to cut prices at any cost.

1. **Rana Plaza disaster (2013)** — The collapse of a garment factory in Bangladesh killed 1,134 people and injured more than 2,500. Investigations established that several subcontractor factories were making clothes for Walmart. The company refused to sign the legally binding Bangladesh Accord on Fire and Building Safety.
   *Source: The New York Times, "Rana Plaza's Legacy", 2013*

2. **Worker trampled to death on Black Friday (2008)** — On Long Island (New York), shoppers trampled Walmart employee Jdimytai Damour to death when a crowd broke down the store doors at dawn. The family filed a lawsuit accusing the company of creating a knowingly dangerous situation.
   *Source: Associated Press, November 2008*

3. **Mass labour rights violations (2005–2023)** — Walmart was repeatedly fined by the US Department of Labour for illegal use of child labour by contracted cleaning companies, forced unpaid overtime, and blocking of breaks. In 2005, the company paid $172 million in a California workers' lawsuit over unpaid meal breaks.
   *Source: U.S. Department of Labor; Los Angeles Times, 2005*

## 2. Has the corporation been the cause of major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. Walmart systematically violated hazardous waste legislation and contaminated groundwater in dozens of states.

1. **Illegal hazardous waste disposal (2013)** — The US Department of Justice and EPA required Walmart to pay an $82 million fine in a criminal case involving illegal discharge of pesticides, fertilisers, household chemicals, and other hazardous substances at ordinary landfills nationwide. This became one of the largest groundwater contamination cases in the history of retail.
   *Source: U.S. Department of Justice, "Walmart to Pay Over $81 Million", 2013*

2. **Groundwater contamination in California (2010–2023)** — A series of EPA investigations recorded seepage of toxic substances from Walmart waste containers into groundwater in California, Missouri, and Texas. In 2023, the company paid an additional $7.5 million in a California state law violation case.
   *Source: California Department of Toxic Substances Control, 2023*

3. **Ecosystem degradation near distribution centres (2015–2022)** — Earthjustice documented air contamination from the exhaust of thousands of diesel trucks around Walmart logistics hubs in Illinois and California. Residents of adjacent neighbourhoods, predominantly low-income communities of colour, reported elevated rates of asthma and cancer.
   *Source: Earthjustice, "Diesel Exhaust and Environmental Justice", 2020*

## 3. Has the company engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. Walmart regularly announces large-scale "green" programmes that are not backed by real changes to its business model.

1. **Project Gigaton (2017–2023)** — Walmart announced a programme to reduce supply chain emissions by 1 gigaton of CO₂ by 2030. An independent analysis by the Carbon Disclosure Project showed that most of the declared reductions are the result of unverified self-reports by suppliers rather than independent measurements.
   *Source: Carbon Disclosure Project, Supplier Engagement Rating 2022*

2. **"Eco-friendly" product labelling (2022)** — The US Federal Trade Commission (FTC) investigated Walmart's practice of labelling products as "bamboo" and "natural", while analysis showed the use of chemically processed synthetic fibres. Walmart paid a fine without admitting guilt.
   *Source: Federal Trade Commission press release, 2022*

3. **Palm oil commitment (2015–2023)** — Walmart publicly committed to transitioning to 100% certified palm oil by 2015, then moved the deadline to 2020, then to 2025. According to Rainforest Action Network data, as of 2023 a significant portion of Walmart's private label products still use uncertified palm oil.
   *Source: Rainforest Action Network, "Snack Food 20", 2023*

## 4. Does the company engage in ESG marketing?

**Answer: YES**. Walmart actively publishes ESG reports, joins climate alliances, and positions itself as a sustainability leader.

1. **Joining the Climate Pledge (2021)** — Walmart signed Jeff Bezos's Climate Pledge, committing to achieve zero emissions by 2040. Critics point out that without mandatory Scope 3 emission limits (across the entire supply chain), this commitment has no practical force.
   *Source: The Climate Pledge, 2021; CDP Scorecard 2022*

2. **Annual ESG report and MSCI ratings** — Walmart regularly receives an "AA" rating from MSCI ESG and publishes progress reports. Independent analysts As You Sow draw attention to the gap between declarations and actual reduction in packaging waste.
   *Source: MSCI ESG Ratings; As You Sow, "Plastic Waste Scorecard" 2022*

3. **Walmart.org eco-investment programme (2022)** — The Walmart Foundation allocated $35 million for forest restoration in Brazil — a sum negligible compared with the company's annual revenue of $611 billion and the scale of deforestation linked to its supply chain.
   *Source: Walmart ESG Report 2022*

---

## 5. Has the corporation won any global ESG awards?

**Answer: YES**. Walmart receives industry ESG awards that often reflect the quality of public communications rather than actual environmental results.

1. **Inclusion in the Dow Jones Sustainability Index (DJSI)** — Walmart has been repeatedly included in the DJSI North America index, despite numerous environmental lawsuits. The DJSI methodology is based primarily on questionnaires completed by the companies themselves.
   *Source: S&P Global, DJSI 2021–2023*

2. **CDP "Supplier Engagement Leader" award (2020–2022)** — Walmart received the status of supplier engagement leader from CDP, although most of the emission reductions did not undergo independent verification.
   *Source: CDP Supplier Engagement Leaderboard 2022*

3. **Fortune "World's Most Admired Companies" (2023)** — Walmart annually appears on this Fortune list, which includes ESG criteria among its assessment indicators.
   *Source: Fortune, "Most Admired Companies" 2023*

---

## 6. Has the company applied eco-labels to products with negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. Walmart used misleading labelling on a number of products.

1. **"Green" labelling on household chemicals (2015–2020)** — The chain sold cleaning products under its own brand labelled "eco-friendly" containing phosphates and organochlorine compounds banned in several states. Environmental Working Group (EWG) checks showed that products with "green" labels received "D" and "F" toxicity ratings.
   *Source: Environmental Working Group, "Guide to Healthy Cleaning", 2019*

2. **Pseudo-bamboo textile products (2022)** — The FTC fined Walmart for selling textiles labelled "made from bamboo", when the products were made from viscose using toxic chemical solvents.
   *Source: Federal Trade Commission, "Bamboo Labeling Cases", 2022*

3. **"Recycled plastic" labelling on packaging (2019–2022)** — A number of Great Value brand packages contained claims about recycled plastic while the actual secondary material content was less than 5%, as documented by As You Sow.
   *Source: As You Sow, "Plastic Packaging Report" 2021*

## 7. Has the company organised eco-events negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. Walmart conducted environmental PR events incommensurate with the scale of its actual impact.

1. **"Green" litter collections near stores (2018–2022)** — Walmart annually announces litter collection events near its stores as part of Earth Day. The total volume of waste collected amounts to thousands of kilograms — against the backdrop of billions of units of plastic packaging sold annually, these figures are negligible.
   *Source: Walmart press releases, 2018–2022*

2. **Tree-planting in the USA (2020–2022)** — Under the Project Gigaton programme, Walmart announced a partnership to plant 50 million trees — despite its suppliers indirectly destroying hundreds of thousands of hectares of tropical forests annually.
   *Source: Walmart Sustainability Hub, 2021*

3. **Reusable bag distribution in China (2021)** — Walmart China launched a campaign to replace plastic bags with reusable bags in 400 stores as a model of "sustainable retail". Meanwhile, stores continued to sell billions of units of single-use packaging.
   *Source: Walmart China sustainability report, 2021*

---

## 8. Has the company engaged in lobbying for pseudo-ecological theories?

**Answer: YES**. Walmart uses eco-educational initiatives and lobbying to form a favourable image while opposing mandatory regulation.

1. **Lobbying against extended producer responsibility (2018–2023)** — Walmart actively opposed the adoption of extended producer responsibility (EPR) laws in California and Oregon that shift packaging disposal costs from taxpayers to producers and retailers. The company spent millions lobbying to soften these laws.
   *Source: OpenSecrets, lobbying disclosures 2019–2022; Oregon DEQ public records*

2. **"Environmental awareness" programme for shoppers (2022)** — Walmart launched a "sustainable shopping" section in its app, promoting selected "green" products and shifting responsibility for environmental choice onto consumers instead of systemically changing its supply chain.
   *Source: Walmart app sustainability feature, 2022; criticism in The Guardian*

3. **Funding ESG educational programmes (2020–2023)** — The Walmart Foundation sponsored academic conferences and publications promoting the concept of "voluntary market mechanisms" as an alternative to government regulation of plastic waste, which experts characterise as corporate capture of the agenda.
   *Source: InfluenceMap, "Plastic Industry Lobbying" 2022*
