Weyerhaeuser — the largest private forest landowner in the United States — controls approximately 11 million acres of forest lands. The company is criticised for replacing biologically diverse old-growth forests with monoculture plantations that provide timber harvests but do not perform the ecological functions of natural forests.

In the Pacific Northwest (Washington and Oregon states), Weyerhaeuser logging led to large-scale slope erosion and silting of salmon spawning rivers, catastrophically reducing salmon populations. Several subspecies of spotted owl and marbled murrelet — birds nesting only in old-growth forests — are listed as threatened species.

In 2002, Weyerhaeuser acquired Willamette Industries, making it the largest timber company in the United States. Environmental NGOs warned at the time about increasing pressure on the remaining old-growth forests of the Pacific Northwest.

## 1. Has the corporation's activity led to mass harm to human lives?

**Answer: YES**. Deaths of forestry workers and destruction of fish resources on which indigenous peoples depend are documented.

1. **Worker fatalities in logging operations** — Logging is traditionally one of the most dangerous industries in the US. Fatal accidents involving harvester operators, log truck drivers, and river drivers were recorded at Weyerhaeuser facilities and the company's contractor operations.
   *Source: U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics — Logging Industry Fatality Data; OSHA citations*

2. **Destruction of salmon spawning grounds and indigenous peoples' rights** — The felling of old-growth forests and their replacement with monoculture plantations caused large-scale silting of Pacific salmon spawning rivers in the Northwest US. Indigenous peoples (Tulalip, Nisqually, and other tribes), whose fishing rights are protected by 19th-century treaties, document a catastrophic decline in Pacific salmon populations.
   *Source: Earthjustice — Pacific Northwest salmon litigation; NRDC*

3. **Nitrate contamination of water intakes** — Intensive herbicide (glyphosate) and fertiliser use on Weyerhaeuser plantations leads to nitrate contamination of watercourses used for drinking water supply in rural areas of Washington and Oregon.
   *Source: FSEEE (Forest Service Employees for Environmental Ethics)*

## 2. Has the corporation been the cause of major environmental disasters?

**Answer: YES**. Slope erosion, river silting, and destruction of threatened species habitats are documented consequences of Weyerhaeuser's activities.

1. **Large-scale erosion following clear-cuts** — In the Pacific Northwest, Weyerhaeuser logging on steep slopes repeatedly led to landslides and stream destruction. USFS research documents that the road network density of logging companies is the primary factor in accelerated erosion and river silting.
   *Source: U.S. Forest Service research; Earthjustice litigation records*

2. **Destruction of spotted owl habitat** — Weyerhaeuser's felling of old-growth forests and replacement with young plantations led to a sharp decline in the Northern Spotted Owl population, listed as a threatened species under the ESA in 1990. The company became one of the defendants in environmental lawsuits related to this species.
   *Source: Audubon Society; U.S. Fish & Wildlife Service ESA listings*

3. **Biodiversity loss from transition to monoculture plantations** — Replacing biologically diverse old-growth forests with monoculture Douglas fir plantations reduces biodiversity by 90%+. FSEEE documents long-term losses of bird, amphibian, and mycorrhizal fungi species composition.
   *Source: FSEEE; Audubon Society*

## 3. Has the company engaged in greenwashing?

**Answer: YES**. An industry-controlled sustainable forestry certificate (SFI) is used as a marketing tool.

1. **SFI certificate as an "industry" standard** — Weyerhaeuser uses the SFI (Sustainable Forestry Initiative) certificate to market forest products as "sustainable". Critics (NRDC, FSC supporters) point out that SFI was created by the forestry industry itself and permits practices banned under FSC: clear-cutting, destruction of threatened species habitats.
   *Source: NRDC — "SFI: The logging industry grades its own homework"*

2. **"Certified Responsible Wood" for products from monoculture plantations** — Lumber from monoculture Douglas fir plantations is labelled "certified responsible wood", although these plantations do not perform the ecological functions of natural forests.
   *Source: FSC vs SFI comparative analysis*

3. **REIT status as a "climate-responsible" asset** — Weyerhaeuser is positioned in ESG investment circles as a "forest REIT" that sequesters carbon. Critics point out that intensive rotational logging reduces the long-term carbon stock compared with protected forests.
   *Source: NRDC Carbon analysis*

---

## 4. Does the company engage in ESG marketing?

**Answer: YES**. Weyerhaeuser actively promotes the narrative of forests as a "renewable resource" and carbon store.

1. **ESG reports and "Nature Positive" narrative** — Weyerhaeuser's annual ESG reports declare contributions to biodiversity, water resource management, and carbon sequestration. The company promotes the narrative that managed forest is the best climate solution.
   *Source: Weyerhaeuser Sustainability Report 2023*

2. **CDP and TCFD membership** — Weyerhaeuser discloses climate risks in accordance with TCFD recommendations and reports to CDP, which is positioned as ESG leadership.
   *Source: Weyerhaeuser CDP disclosure*

3. **"Timber construction reduces carbon footprint" narrative** — The company actively promotes mass timber construction (CLT, Mass Timber) as a climate solution, positioning demand for forest products as "green".
   *Source: Weyerhaeuser Mass Timber marketing*

---

## 5. Has the corporation won any global ESG awards?

**Answer: YES**. Weyerhaeuser receives recognition in ESG ratings oriented towards "sustainable forestry".

1. **Inclusion in DJSI North America** — Weyerhaeuser is included in the Dow Jones Sustainability Index North America as one of the "sustainable" forest REITs.
   *Source: S&P Global DJSI*

2. **CDP Forest A-List** — The company received high CDP scores in the Forest category, which is used in investor relations communications.
   *Source: CDP Forest disclosures*

---

## 6. Has the company applied eco-labels to products with negative environmental impact?

**Answer: YES**. Lumber and timber construction products are labelled with the SFI certificate as "sustainable".

1. **SFI labelling on lumber from monoculture plantations** — Products from plantations created on the site of felled old-growth forests bear the SFI Certified logo, creating among consumers an impression of an ecologically responsible choice.
   *Source: NRDC SFI analysis*

2. **"Sustainably sourced" labelling for construction retailers** — Weyerhaeuser lumber is sold at Home Depot and Lowe's with "sustainably sourced" labelling, although some products come from plantations in threatened species habitats.
   *Source: FSEEE; Earthjustice*

---

## 7. Has the company organised eco-events negligible in scale?

**Answer: YES**. Weyerhaeuser runs wildlife protection programmes on its lands that are incommensurate with the scale of logging.

1. **Spotted owl conservation programme** — The company declares "management" of spotted owl habitats on some of its forest lands, but USFWS notes continuing population decline of the owl in the Pacific Northwest.
   *Source: USFWS Spotted Owl Status Reviews*

2. **Spawning stream restoration** — Weyerhaeuser funds small spawning stream restoration projects, but the scale of these projects is incommensurate with the area of silted and degraded watercourses near the company's logging operations.
   *Source: Weyerhaeuser Environmental Programs*

---

## 8. Has the company engaged in lobbying for pseudo-ecological theories?

**Answer: YES**. Weyerhaeuser lobbies for the narrative of "managed forest" as the best climate strategy.

1. **Lobbying against expanding protected forest areas** — Weyerhaeuser actively lobbies against the creation of new nature reserves and national monuments in forest areas of the northwestern US, arguing that "managed" private forests store carbon better than "abandoned" protected ones.
   *Source: Earthjustice; NRDC congressional testimony records*

2. **Promoting "forest management" as a substitute for conservation** — The company and its trade associations promote legislation equating industrial forestry with "forest management" for climate purposes, using this to obtain subsidies and tax benefits.
   *Source: American Forest & Paper Association lobbying disclosures*

3. **Lobbying for mass timber construction** — Weyerhaeuser actively lobbies for amendments to building codes expanding the use of timber structures (Mass Timber), positioning this as a climate solution while simultaneously creating a new market for its products.
   *Source: WoodWorks lobbying; Building codes federal advocacy*
